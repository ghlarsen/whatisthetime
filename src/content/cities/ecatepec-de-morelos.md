Ecatepec de Morelos, a municipality in the State of Mexico immediately north of Mexico City, is one of the most densely populated municipalities in the Western Hemisphere, with over 1.6 million residents. It uses `America/Mexico_City`, UTC-6 in winter and UTC-5 in summer (CDT), following Mexico's standard DST schedule.

## commuting into the capital before dawn

Ecatepec is functionally part of the Mexico City megalopolis, connected to the capital by metro Line B and a network of buses that run from before 5am to accommodate the factory workers and service workers who commute daily. The workday here starts earlier than in [Mexico City](/mexico-city) proper because commute times are long: a worker leaving Ecatepec at 5:30am may not arrive at their job in the capital until 7:30am or later.

Mexico's DST policy changed in 2023, when a majority of states eliminated seasonal clock changes. However, states bordering the United States (which need to maintain coordination with American schedules) continue observing DST. The State of Mexico, in which Ecatepec sits, is among those that kept DST, meaning Ecatepec's clock still shifts twice a year.
