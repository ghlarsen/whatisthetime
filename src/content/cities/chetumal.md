Chetumal is the capital of Quintana Roo, Mexico's easternmost state, sitting on Chetumal Bay at the border with Belize. It runs on America/Cancun: UTC-5, year-round. In 2015, the entire state of Quintana Roo switched from Central Time (with DST) to permanent Eastern Standard Time. [Cancún](/cancun) and Chetumal made the change together.

The switch aligned Quintana Roo with the tourism market: most American tourists come from the Eastern Seaboard, and the permanent UTC-5 offset reduces seasonal timezone confusion for visitors.

## The border with Belize

Chetumal is directly on the Belize border, with the crossing at Santa Elena/Subteniente López just south of the city. [Belize City](/belize-city) is a few hours drive south. Belize uses UTC-6 year-round. This means that Chetumal at UTC-5 is one hour ahead of Belize City.

The border is active with trade, shopping, and movement of people. Belizeans cross into Chetumal for goods; Mexicans and tourists cross into Belize for the cayes and reef. The one-hour difference requires adjustment but is a small practical matter in a region with daily cross-border traffic.

## Chetumal Bay and the Maya coast

Chetumal Bay is a large, shallow lagoon extending into Belize, protected by the barrier reef. The bay's ecosystem is an important manatee habitat and a Ramsar-designated wetland. The city's waterfront development and the bay's ecology create a nature-tourism dimension to the city's economy.

The pre-Columbian Maya civilization was concentrated throughout this region. Kohunlich, Dzibanche, and other archaeological sites are within driving distance. The Maya Long Count calendar, one of history's most sophisticated timekeeping systems, was developed by cultures whose descendants still inhabit this landscape.

The [time conversion from Chetumal to London](/when/9am-chetumal/london) puts London five hours ahead in winter, six in summer when the UK observes BST.
