Port-Gentil has no road connection to the rest of Gabon. The city sits on the Mandji Peninsula, surrounded by the Ogooué River delta and the Atlantic, accessible only by plane or boat. It is an island city in practice, and this isolation shapes the rhythm of daily life.

[Gabon](/country/gabon) uses West Africa Time, UTC+1, year-round without daylight saving. Port-Gentil shares this clock with [Libreville](/libreville) to the north, connected by regular flights across the delta. At less than 1 degree south of the equator, the time experience is thoroughly equatorial: sunrise at 6am, sunset at 6pm, 365 days a year, with almost no seasonal variation.

The city grew around oil. Gabon's offshore petroleum industry, developed from the 1950s onward, made Port-Gentil the economic engine of the country. The port handles oil exports, supply vessels for offshore platforms, and the general logistics of a resource economy. The rigs visible offshore operate on their own continuous schedules: 24 hours, every day, regardless of what the sun is doing.

The Ogooué River delta around Port-Gentil is one of central Africa's significant wetland systems, home to forest elephants, hippos, and sitatunga antelopes in the mangroves. The city and the wilderness exist in close proximity.
