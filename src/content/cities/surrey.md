Surrey is the most populous city in British Columbia by area and the second most populous in [Canada](/country/canada)'s Metro Vancouver region after the City of Vancouver itself. It runs on Pacific Standard Time (UTC-8) in winter and Pacific Daylight Time (UTC-7) in summer, following the same schedule as Vancouver.

British Columbia observes DST in line with the US Pacific Coast states to the south, advancing clocks on the second Sunday of March and returning on the first Sunday of November. The synchronization with California and Washington State is commercially important for a region deeply integrated with the US Pacific economy.

British Columbia has debated permanently switching to Pacific Daylight Time (UTC-7) year-round, which would match parts of the US when the US eventually makes a similar change. The Lower Mainland, including Surrey, has generally been in favor, with the agricultural interior more cautious. The change requires federal Canadian authorization and coordination with US neighbors.

At 49 degrees north, Surrey has significant seasonal light variation. Summer evenings on PDT push sunset past 9pm. Winter afternoons on PST end before 5pm.

Surrey's population has grown substantially through immigration, particularly from South and East Asia, giving the city a distinctive multicultural character.

[9am in Surrey is 5pm in London](/when/9am-surrey/london) in winter, 4pm during summer when both observe their respective DST.
