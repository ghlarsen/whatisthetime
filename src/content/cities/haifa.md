Haifa is Israel's third-largest city, a port city on the Mediterranean coast at the foot of Mount Carmel. It runs on Israel Standard Time, UTC+2 in winter and Israel Daylight Time, UTC+3, in summer. The timezone is Asia/Jerusalem in the IANA database.

Israel's daylight saving transition dates are set annually by the government, typically in late March and early October. The exact date varies by year, occasionally shifted to avoid conflicts with Jewish holidays.

## The city known for coexistence

Haifa has a reputation within Israel for coexistence between Jewish and Arab communities. Jewish, Christian, and Muslim residents share many neighborhoods, and the Baha'i World Centre, with its stunning terraced gardens on Mount Carmel, has made the city a pilgrimage destination for the global Baha'i community.

This coexistence is visible in time too. The call to prayer from mosques in the lower city marks the Muslim day. Church bells in the Christian neighborhoods ring on Sunday mornings. Jewish Shabbat begins at sunset on Friday and ends when three stars appear Saturday night. Multiple time systems operate in parallel.

## Port rhythms and industrial time

Haifa's port is Israel's main cargo port, operating around the clock. The Haifa Chemicals industrial complex, the oil refineries, and the Technion university research campus all impose their own temporal structures on the city. The port's 24-hour operation means night shifts, early morning deliveries, and a schedule that never fully stops.
