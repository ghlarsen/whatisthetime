On July 4, 1776, the Second Continental Congress adopted the Declaration of Independence in Philadelphia. The exact hour is not recorded with certainty, but it was afternoon. The bell in the State House steeple rang out over the city. Time, in a sense, restarted for a new republic.

Philadelphia follows Eastern Time, UTC-5 in winter and UTC-4 during daylight saving. The city shares this clock with [New York](/new-york) to the northeast and [Washington DC](/washington-dc) to the southwest. That corridor of political and financial power runs on one synchronized schedule, though each city has built its own character within it.

## seasons, markets, and city character

The city sits at about 40 degrees north, giving it four distinct seasons. Summer arrives early and humid, winter cold enough to freeze the Delaware River in severe years. Philadelphians know what each season costs and what it gives: outdoor markets in June, Reading Terminal Market warmth in February.

The Liberty Bell now sits in a glass pavilion where visitors can see it any hour of the day, though officially it only rings on significant national occasions. Its original ringing in 1776 was tied to a specific moment. Most great moments are.

[9am in Philadelphia is 2pm in London](/when/9am-philadelphia/london), useful for the many academic and medical institutions here that collaborate internationally.
