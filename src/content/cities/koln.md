Köln sits on the Rhine at 51 degrees north, where the river curves wide and the cathedral towers over a city that has been continuously inhabited for over 2,000 years. It runs on Europe/Berlin time: UTC+1 in winter (CET), UTC+2 in summer (CEST). The DST transitions follow the EU-wide schedule.

## cathedral light and the debate over permanent summertime

At this latitude, the daylight swings are pronounced. June evenings stay light past 10pm. December afternoons fade before 4:30pm. The cathedral's western facade catches the last light of winter evenings in a way that has been orienting the city since 1880, when the building was finally completed after 632 years of construction.

Germany has not broken from EU timezone rules, but there has been ongoing discussion about abolishing seasonal clock changes across Europe. Köln, with its strong media and business sectors, would likely favor a permanent summertime (UTC+2) if the choice were made, extending working daylight into the evening for a city with a significant outdoor culture.

The Karneval season, Cologne's famous pre-Lent carnival, runs on its own temporal logic entirely. The city's rhythms from November 11 through Rosenmontag follow a calendar determined by the church year, not the solar or business clock.
