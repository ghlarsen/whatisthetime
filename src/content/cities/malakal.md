Malakal sits on the west bank of the White Nile in South Sudan's Upper Nile State, roughly 500 kilometers north of [Juba](/juba), the national capital. It runs on East Africa Time, UTC+3, the single timezone that [South Sudan](/country/south-sudan) adopted when it became the world's newest nation in 2011.

## conflict, displacement, and civil time

The city was historically an important Nile trading point and has been one of the most severely affected locations in South Sudan's ongoing civil conflict, which intensified after 2013. The population has been displaced multiple times, and the city has been partially destroyed and rebuilt. Its clock keeps ticking through the disruptions, but the practical rhythms of daily life have been shaped more by humanitarian supply schedules and security conditions than by civil time.

At 9 degrees north latitude, day length varies modestly across the year. The White Nile here is wide and slow, and the seasonal flooding patterns remain a primary organizer of the agricultural year.

[Juba](/juba) shares this timezone. [9am in Malakal is 6am in London](/when/9am-malakal/london) during European winter.
