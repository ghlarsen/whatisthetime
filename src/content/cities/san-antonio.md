On February 23, 1836, the Mexican army under General Santa Anna laid siege to the Alamo mission in San Antonio de Béxar. The siege lasted 13 days. On March 6, the final assault killed the 182 to 260 defenders inside. "Remember the Alamo" became the battle cry of the Texas Revolution. The exact morning of March 6, 1836, at pre-dawn, is one of the most precisely remembered moments in Texan mythology.

San Antonio runs on Central Time, UTC-6 in winter (CST) and UTC-5 in summer (CDT), the same clock as [Houston](/houston) and [Austin](/austin) across Texas. The uniform time reflects the state's commerce and communication patterns, though Texas's longitude spans from UTC-7 territory in the west (El Paso) to the east.

The River Walk, the famous paseo along the San Antonio River below street level, defines the city's social geography. Restaurants and bars face the water; the ambient noise of the city is muted by the embankment walls. The River Walk operates late by Texas standards, the tables full until midnight in summer.

## military bases and River Walk nights

San Antonio's military presence: Fort Sam Houston, Lackland Air Force Base, Randolph Air Force Base, gives it a military clock overlay. Base schedules, deployments, and the 24-hour operational rhythms of the installations run beneath the civilian city's schedule.
