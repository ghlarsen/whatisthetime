Khulna sits at the edge of the Sundarbans, the world's largest tidal mangrove forest, where the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta dissolves into a maze of waterways before reaching the Bay of Bengal. The city runs on Bangladesh Standard Time, UTC+6, year-round, the same clock as [Dhaka](/dhaka). No daylight saving.

## Sundarbans, shrimp exports, and tidal schedules

At 23 degrees north, Khulna has moderate seasonal daylight variation. The monsoon season from June through September is the more meaningful annual cycle: the Sundarbans floods, river levels rise, and the fishing and shipping industries shift their operations around the tidal schedule rather than the clock.

Bangladesh moved from UTC+6 to UTC+6:30 during the energy crisis of 2009, advancing clocks by half an hour to reduce evening electricity demand. The change was reversed in 2010 when it proved unpopular. Bangladesh has stayed at UTC+6 since.

Khulna's shrimp processing industry runs on export schedules timed to European and North American markets. That means cold chain logistics and overnight processing facilities operate regardless of local clock time, another case where global trade overrides solar rhythm.

[9am in Khulna is 3am in London](/when/9am-khulna/london), six hours ahead of GMT.
