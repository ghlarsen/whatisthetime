Kwajalein is a long, thin atoll enclosing one of the world's largest lagoons, and it hosts the Ronald Reagan Ballistic Missile Defense Test Site, a US Army facility that has tracked missile tests across the Pacific since the Cold War. The atoll runs on Pacific/Majuro time: UTC+12, year-round, no daylight saving.

The Marshall Islands moved to UTC+12 when the country crossed the International Date Line in 1993. Before that, the islands were at UTC-12, the furthest behind timezone on earth. The government chose to be aligned with Asia and Australia, its main trading partners, rather than with Hawaii and the Americas. Kwajalein changed dates overnight, skipping an entire calendar day.

The US military presence on Kwajalein operates partly on its own schedule, tied to missile test windows and coordination with mainland US facilities. The military base time has historically been kept separate from the Marshall Islands civil time, creating a miniature two-clock situation within the atoll.

The lagoon at Kwajalein is one of the world's premier wreck diving sites, with dozens of Japanese vessels sunk during the US assault of February 1944. The wrecks rest in shallow, clear water, undisturbed in the atoll's protected depths.
