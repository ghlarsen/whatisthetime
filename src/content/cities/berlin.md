Berlin runs on Central European Time: UTC+1 in winter, UTC+2 in summer. Germany observes DST alongside most of the European Union. Clocks spring forward in late March and fall back in late October. The IANA identifier is Europe/Berlin.

The city's timezone history has a darker chapter. Between 1949 and 1990, Berlin was divided. East Berlin, capital of the German Democratic Republic, and West Berlin, an enclave within East Germany, existed in separate political systems. Both sides observed Central European Time. But DST schedules occasionally diverged, particularly in the 1970s when West Germany and East Germany made different decisions about when to apply the clock change. For brief periods, citizens separated by a wall were also separated by an hour.

## The reunified clock

German reunification in 1990 resolved the timezone split instantly. One city, one clock. The European Union subsequently harmonized DST schedules across member states, so the question of when Germany's clocks change is now set at the EU level, not in Berlin.

The EU has repeatedly discussed abolishing DST altogether. A 2018 public consultation found that 84% of respondents wanted the biannual change to end. Germany, and Berlin specifically, would need to choose whether to stay on UTC+1 (winter time) permanently or UTC+2 (summer time) permanently. The German government favored permanent summer time (UTC+2). The decision stalled in the European Parliament and has not been resolved.

## Sunset and city culture

At 52 degrees north, Berlin has notable daylight variation. In June, sunset arrives past 9:30pm. The city's outdoor culture, street cafes, park gatherings, open-air venues, depends on these long summer evenings. In December, it's dark before 4pm, and the city shifts to a warmer, indoor rhythm of markets, bars, and heated venues.

The [time conversion from Berlin to London](/when/9am-berlin/london) is one hour ahead in winter. [Brussels](/brussels) and [Amsterdam](/amsterdam) share Europe/Brussels but behave identically to Berlin's schedule.
