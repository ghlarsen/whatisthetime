San Jose is the largest city in Silicon Valley and the seat of Santa Clara County, the county that contains more technology company headquarters per square kilometer than anywhere else on Earth. Intel, Apple, Cisco, Adobe, eBay, and hundreds of other companies are headquartered here or within commuting distance. Pacific Time is the timezone their engineers, product managers, and executives work in by default.

[United States](/country/united-states) Pacific Time, UTC-8 in winter and UTC-7 in summer, runs from Mexico to Canada along the West Coast. San Jose shares it with [San Francisco](/san-francisco) 80 kilometers north and [San Diego](/san-diego) to the south.

The Santa Clara Valley, before it became Silicon Valley, was covered in orchards: prunes, apricots, cherries. The agricultural rhythm of the valley ran from spring blossom to autumn harvest. The last orchard in San Jose proper was replaced by development decades ago. The valley runs on quarterly earnings cycles and product launch windows now.
