Faaa is the commune on Tahiti's northwestern coast that contains Faa'a International Airport, French Polynesia's main gateway. It uses `Pacific/Tahiti`, UTC-10, year-round, with no daylight saving. Tahiti Time is 10 hours behind UTC, putting it in the same offset as [East Honolulu](/east-honolulu) in Hawaii.

French Polynesia spans a vast stretch of the Pacific, and different island groups use different offsets: the Marquesas use UTC-9:30, the Gambier Islands use UTC-9, and the Society Islands (including Tahiti) use UTC-10. Faaa and Papeete, just to its east, are on Tahiti Time.

The airport at Faaa processes flights from Los Angeles, Auckland, and Tokyo, all of which require adjusting from UTC-10 to significantly different offsets. Arriving from Los Angeles (UTC-8 in winter, UTC-7 in summer), passengers subtract 2-3 hours from their previous clock. From Auckland (UTC+13 in summer), they subtract an extraordinary 23 hours of clock time, crossing the date line in the process. Faaa's timezone at UTC-10 makes it one of the last places on Earth to start each calendar day.
