Zambia sits neatly between the tropics, and its capital Lusaka sits at about 15 degrees south latitude. Day length shifts, but modestly: around 11 hours of daylight in June's southern winter, 13 hours in December's summer. Central Africa Time, UTC+2, applies year-round for [Zambia](/country/zambia) with no daylight saving adjustment.

Lusaka became the capital in 1935 when the British South Africa Company moved administrative functions from Livingstone, a city named for the Scottish explorer. The shift was partly about rail logistics, Lusaka sat on the main line, and partly about geography: more central, less associated with Victoria Falls tourism.

The city's copper belt links connect northward to [Lubumbashi](/lubumbashi) in the DRC, which runs on the same UTC+2 offset, making cross-border mining coordination simple. South to Zimbabwe and east to [Lilongwe](/lilongwe) in Malawi, the regional clock is consistent.

[9am in Lusaka is 7am in London](/when/9am-lusaka/london) during European winter, a gap that matters for the NGOs, mining firms, and development banks with strong Zambia programs.
