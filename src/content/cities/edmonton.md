Edmonton, Alberta's capital and a gateway to Canada's oil sands region, runs on Mountain Time: UTC-7 in winter and UTC-6 in summer (MDT). The IANA identifier `America/Edmonton` covers most of Alberta. Saskatchewan to the east uses permanent UTC-6, creating a small permanent timezone difference between neighbors.

At 53.5 degrees north, Edmonton's summer days are remarkable. Near the summer solstice, the sun rises before 5am and sets after 10pm, giving the city over 17 hours of daylight. The long evenings drive outdoor activity: the North Saskatchewan River valley pathway system fills with cyclists, and patios are occupied past 9pm. Winter inverts this: in December, daylight lasts only about 8 hours.

Edmonton is connected to [Calgary](/calgary) by the Queen Elizabeth II Highway and shares Alberta's Mountain Time, though the two cities are 300 kilometers apart. The timezone is a reasonable fit for Alberta's longitude (113-114 degrees west), where solar noon falls around 12:30pm. The oil industry that dominates Alberta's economy runs 24-hour operations at the upgrader facilities north of the city, making the clock relevant more for shift handoffs than for daylight.
