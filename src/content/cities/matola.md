Matola is the second-largest city in [Mozambique](/country/mozambique) and effectively the industrial suburb of the capital [Maputo](/maputo), which surrounds it on three sides. They share the same timezone: Central Africa Time, UTC+2, year-round with no daylight saving.

## aluminum smelters and continuous shifts

The city is home to Mozambique's main industrial zone, with aluminum smelting, petroleum refining, and manufacturing operations that connect to Maputo's port a few kilometers east. The aluminum smelter MOZAL, one of the largest in Africa, operates on continuous industrial shifts that care little about civil clock changes.

Matola grew rapidly after independence and through the civil war period, as people moved to the urban edge for work and relative safety. The city now has over a million residents, more than Maputo itself in many counts, though the two cities' infrastructure blurs the distinction.

[9am in Matola is 7am in London](/when/9am-matola/london) during European winter.
