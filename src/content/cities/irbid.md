Irbid is Jordan's second-largest city, in the northern part of the country near the borders with Syria and Israel. It runs on UTC+3 year-round. Jordan observed daylight saving until 2022, when the king issued a decree abolishing the practice. The country moved to permanent winter time.

At UTC+3, Irbid shares its clock with [Amman](/amman), [Riyadh](/riyadh), and [Baghdad](/baghdad). The permanent UTC+3 offset puts solar noon in Irbid at about 12:00pm, a near-perfect alignment for the city's longitude.

## Yarmouk University and the student city

Irbid is home to Yarmouk University, one of Jordan's largest, and several other institutions. The student population gives the city a younger demographic and a social rhythm tied to the academic calendar: the semester schedule, exam periods, and the graduate seasons structure the year alongside religious holidays.

## Northern Jordan's ancient crossroads

Irbid sits on what was ancient Arbila, a city of the Decapolis in the Greco-Roman period. The region's fertile land made it an agricultural center for millennia. Today it is industrializing, with pharmaceutical and manufacturing sectors supplementing the traditional agricultural economy.
