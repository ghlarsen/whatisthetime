Krasnoyarsk straddles the Yenisei River in the middle of Siberia, a city of over a million that grew from a 17th-century Cossack fort into a major industrial center. It runs on Asia/Novosibirsk time, UTC+7, year-round. Russia abolished daylight saving in 2014.

## the great dam and Trans-Siberian split clocks

The Yenisei is one of the world's great rivers, and the Krasnoyarsk Hydroelectric Dam, completed in 1972, is among the world's largest by capacity. The dam created the massive Krasnoyarsk Reservoir and fundamentally altered the local environment. It also created a 24-hour industrial operation that runs regardless of solar time.

At 56 degrees north, Krasnoyarsk has dramatic seasonal light. Summer evenings in June stay bright past 11pm. December days last barely 8 hours. The permanent UTC+7 setting (without DST) means that in summer, the clock doesn't advance to take advantage of late evenings, which makes late summer nights unusually bright by clock standards.

Krasnoyarsk is a significant stop on the Trans-Siberian Railway, which uses Moscow Time for all ticketing regardless of local time (a 4-hour difference at UTC+7 Moscow +3). Travelers on the train learn to navigate this split quickly or miss their stops.
