Shubrā al Khaymah sits immediately north of Cairo on the Nile, so close to the capital that the two cities form a continuous urban mass. It is one of Egypt's most densely populated and industrially active areas, known for textiles, metalworking, and manufacturing. It runs on Eastern European Time (UTC+2) all year.

[Egypt](/country/egypt) abolished daylight saving time in 2011 after previously observing it inconsistently, and has maintained the fixed UTC+2 offset since. At 30 degrees north, Cairo and its northern suburbs have moderate daylight variation: about 14 hours in June and about 10 hours in December. The fixed clock makes scheduling straightforward for the factories and commercial operations that define Shubrā al Khaymah's economic character.

The city's textile mills and industrial facilities operate on shift schedules that run around the clock, making the exact offset less relevant to workers than the rotation schedule that governs when they work. The call to prayer marks the day's structure for many residents alongside or instead of the mechanical clock.

## the Nile and industrial transformation

The Nile's western bank here, and the agricultural land beyond, has historically been where Cairenes could access food supplies. The urban industrialization of the area over the twentieth century has changed that relationship.
