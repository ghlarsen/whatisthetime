Bujumbura is Burundi's largest city and commercial capital, sitting at the northern end of Lake Tanganyika in the Great Rift Valley. It runs on Africa/Bujumbura: UTC+2, Central Africa Time, year-round. No daylight saving time is observed. Burundi moved its administrative capital to Gitega in 2019, but Bujumbura remains the economic heart of the country.

At roughly 3 degrees south latitude, Bujumbura is near enough to the equator that daylight varies minimally through the year. The sun rises around 6am and sets around 6:15pm regardless of season. The UTC+2 offset keeps solar noon close to 12:15pm, a good solar alignment for the 29th meridian east.

## Lakeside city

Lake Tanganyika is one of the world's oldest and deepest lakes, part of the East African Rift system. The lake moderates Bujumbura's climate: temperatures are consistent year-round, warm but not extreme. The lakefront is the city's social heart, with beach areas, restaurants, and promenades.

Lake Tanganyika borders four countries: Burundi, Tanzania, Zambia, and the DRC. All four use UTC+2 in the regions bordering the lake (Tanzania uses UTC+3, but its western regions near the lake are close). The regional timezone consistency simplifies the cross-border trade and fisheries that the lake economy supports.

## A post-conflict city

Burundi experienced severe ethnic conflict in the 1990s and early 2000s, and Bujumbura was heavily affected. The Arusha Peace and Reconciliation Agreement in 2000 ended the main conflict, though political instability has continued periodically. The city has rebuilt and its commercial activity has resumed, but the demographic and social consequences of the conflict continue to shape urban life.

The [time difference between Bujumbura and London](/when/9am-bujumbura/london) is two hours in winter, one in summer when the UK observes BST. [Blantyre](/blantyre) in Malawi is on the same clock.
