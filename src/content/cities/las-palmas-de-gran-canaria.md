Las Palmas de Gran Canaria is a city of over 300,000 sitting on the northeastern tip of Gran Canaria, with a natural harbor that has made it a transatlantic waypoint for centuries. Columbus stopped here four times on his voyages to the Americas. Today it runs on Atlantic/Canary time: UTC+0 in winter, UTC+1 in summer, one hour behind mainland Spain.

The timezone gap between Las Palmas and Madrid is politically interesting. Spain as a whole is on Central European Time (UTC+1/+2), but the Canary Islands use Western European Time (UTC+0/+1) because their geographic reality demanded it. Las Palmas is at 28 degrees north and 15 degrees west, which puts it at a natural solar position close to UTC+0. Keeping CET would mean solar noon arriving around 1:30pm in winter, far behind the clock.

Las Palmas has an old city (Vegueta) that dates to the 15th-century Spanish conquest of the Canaries, and a modern city built up along the beach during the late 20th-century tourism boom. The two parts operate on somewhat different schedules: the old city's cultural institutions and the new city's hotel zones don't entirely overlap in their daily rhythms.

[La Laguna](/la-laguna) on Tenerife keeps the same clock. [9am in Las Palmas is 9am in London](/when/9am-las-palmas-de-gran-canaria/london) in winter, matching UTC+0 exactly.
