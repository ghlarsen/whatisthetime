Limassol is Cyprus's most dynamic city, a port that handles the island's commercial and passenger shipping, a financial center that has attracted international business since the 1980s, and a coastal resort strip of hotels and restaurants that runs well past midnight in summer. The city uses Asia/Nicosia time: Eastern European Time, UTC+2 in winter, UTC+3 during daylight saving.

The deep-water port at Limassol is the gateway for much of Cyprus's trade, and it operates around the clock. Container ships arrive on global schedules, dock workers handle shifts regardless of local time, and the logistics behind import-export trade runs on UTC reference rather than local EET.

At 34.7 degrees north, Limassol has good seasonal daylight variation. Summer evenings during CEST stretch close to 9pm. The Mediterranean climate brings long, hot, dry summers and short, mild winters. The seafront promenade, stretching several kilometers along the coast, is a different place at 10pm in July than at 5pm in January.

[Larnaca](/larnaca) to the east keeps the same clock. Cyprus is an EU member and follows EU DST scheduling. [9am in Limassol is 7am in London](/when/9am-limassol/london) in winter, two hours ahead of GMT.
