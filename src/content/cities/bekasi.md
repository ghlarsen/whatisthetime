Bekasi is one of those cities that rarely appears in global city rankings but by population belongs near the top of any list. With over 3 million registered residents and millions more in the surrounding metropolitan area, Bekasi is one of the most populous cities in Southeast Asia. It is the eastern continuation of Jakarta's urban sprawl, separated from the capital by a boundary that means little to the people who commute between them daily.

The clock is Western Indonesia Time, UTC+7. The IANA identifier is Asia/Jakarta. [Bandung](/bandung) to the southeast shares the same timezone.

## Living next to Jakarta

Bekasi's identity is shaped by its relationship to Jakarta. It houses Jakarta's overflow population, its industrial districts, and a growing middle class that has moved east as Jakarta's housing costs have risen. The daily commute into Jakarta along the elevated toll roads and the KRL commuter rail line is one of the defining experiences of Bekasi life.

Indonesia does not observe daylight saving time, so UTC+7 is the permanent year-round clock. At roughly 6 degrees south latitude, Bekasi has no seasonal daylight variation worth adjusting for.

The city's industrial parks, particularly Jababeka and MM2100, house international manufacturing operations. The timezone UTC+7 creates morning overlap with Singapore (UTC+8) and connects comfortably to the broader Asian manufacturing supply chain.

[9am in Bekasi is 2am in London](/when/9am-bekasi/london) during European winter.
