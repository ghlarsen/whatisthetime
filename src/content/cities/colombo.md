Sri Lanka Standard Time sits at UTC+5:30, the same offset as [Delhi](/delhi) and India. This isn't coincidence. Under British colonial rule, Ceylon (as Sri Lanka was then known) used Indian Standard Time. After independence, the country kept the offset.

In 1996, Sri Lanka briefly moved its clocks forward by 30 minutes to UTC+6, creating a separate Sri Lanka Standard Time distinct from India. The stated reasons included energy savings and better alignment with business hours in East Asia. The change was reversed in 2006, moving back to UTC+5:30. The IANA identifier `Asia/Colombo` records both shifts. Sri Lanka tried a unique clock and decided the coordination costs with India outweighed the theoretical benefits.

Colombo sits at 6 degrees north latitude, close enough to the equator that sunrise and sunset are almost constant year-round: around 6am and 6pm throughout the year, shifting by only about 40 minutes between solstices. Daylight saving would accomplish essentially nothing here. The city's colonial-era streets and Dutch-built fortifications are lit by a sun that keeps a reliable schedule regardless of what the government has declared.
