Flying Fish Cove is the main settlement on Christmas Island, an Australian territory in the Indian Ocean, 2,600 kilometers northwest of Perth and 360 kilometers south of Java. The island uses `Indian/Christmas`, UTC+7, year-round. No daylight saving.

## crabs, detention, and an island between two worlds

Christmas Island's timezone reflects its geographic position between Australia and Southeast Asia. UTC+7 aligns it with Jakarta rather than Perth (UTC+8), a choice that makes geographic sense for an island that is closer to Java than to the Australian continent.

The island is famous for two things: its annual red crab migration (tens of millions of crabs move from the rainforest to the sea each year after the first rains of the wet season) and the Australian immigration detention center that processes asylum seekers who arrive by boat. The crab migration follows its own calendar, triggered by rainfall and lunar cycles. The detention center runs on Australian administrative time. Flying Fish Cove, caught between ecological and bureaucratic rhythms, maintains UTC+7 as its formal clock.
