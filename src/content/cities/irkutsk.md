Irkutsk is a major city in eastern Siberia, on the Angara River near Lake Baikal. It runs on Irkutsk Time, UTC+8, year-round. Russia abolished daylight saving nationally in 2014, and Irkutsk has kept a fixed clock since.

At UTC+8, Irkutsk is 5 hours ahead of [Moscow](/moscow) and shares its offset with [Beijing](/beijing), [Shanghai](/shanghai), and [Singapore](/singapore). When it is noon in Irkutsk, it is 7am in Moscow and 4am in [London](/london).

## The Trans-Siberian Railway and railroad time

The Trans-Siberian Railway, completed across its full extent by 1916, solved a massive timezone coordination problem. The entire Russian imperial rail network used Moscow time for scheduling, regardless of local solar time. A train departing Irkutsk would list its departure in Moscow time even though that time was completely different from what any local clock showed.

This practice continues today. Russian long-distance train schedules are printed in Moscow time. Passengers boarding in Irkutsk must add 5 hours to find the local time. It is one of the world's most persistent examples of operational time diverging from civil time.

## Lake Baikal and the Siberian year

Lake Baikal, 70 kilometers from Irkutsk, is the world's deepest lake. It freezes solid from January to May, then opens again in summer. The ice season and the navigation season create a natural binary year that matters more to many residents than any administrative calendar.
