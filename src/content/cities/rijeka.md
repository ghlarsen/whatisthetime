Rijeka is one of Europe's genuinely contested cities. In 1920, after WWI, it was claimed by both Italy and the newly formed Kingdom of Yugoslavia. The Italian nationalist poet Gabriele d'Annunzio led a paramilitary takeover of the city that lasted 15 months before Italian annexation. After WWII, most Italians left and Yugoslavia took over. Croatia inherited the city in 1991. The layers of occupation are still visible in the street names, the Habsburg buildings, and the dialects.

[Croatia](/country/croatia) follows Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter (CET) and UTC+2 in summer (CEST). Rijeka shares this clock with [Zagreb](/zagreb) to the northeast and with the rest of Central Europe.

Rijeka sits on a steep hillside descending to the Kvarner Gulf, with the Gorski Kotar mountains behind it. The city faces the Adriatic and catches the bura wind, the fierce cold northeast wind that blows off the mountains in winter. The bura can reach 200 km/h in extreme cases. The city has learned to live with it.

The harbor handles containers, passenger ferries to the Dalmatian islands and beyond, and the general business of Croatia's main port. The ferry to the islands is where island time begins: the boat pulls away from the dock and mainland schedules become abstract.

[9am in Rijeka is 8am in London](/when/9am-rijeka/london) in winter.
