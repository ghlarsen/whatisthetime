Juba is the capital of South Sudan, which declared independence from Sudan on July 9, 2011, making it the world's most recently founded country. Juba runs on East Africa Time, UTC+3, year-round. No daylight saving.

South Sudan uses the same UTC+3 offset as [Nairobi](/nairobi), [Kampala](/kampala), and [Addis Ababa](/addis-ababa). When Sudan proper, to the north, uses UTC+3 as well, the separation between north and south has not created a timezone divergence.

## The independence moment

South Sudan's declaration of independence on July 9, 2011, was a specific moment in time. The flag was raised, the new country began. That date, meticulously recorded in UTC+3, is now a national holiday. The country's brief existence has already included years of civil war, famine, and ongoing reconstruction.

## The Nile and the seasonal calendar

Juba sits on the White Nile. The river's flooding season, which historically shaped the agricultural calendar across northeastern Africa, matters here too. Nile flooding peaks in late summer. The rainy season runs from April to October. Agricultural and pastoral rhythms follow these water patterns more than the civil clock.

At 4.9 degrees North, Juba's day length is nearly constant. Solar noon falls around 12:10pm, a good fit with UTC+3.
