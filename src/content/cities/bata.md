Bata is the main city of Equatorial Guinea's mainland territory, Rio Muni, and despite not being the country's capital (that is Malabo, on the island of Bioko), Bata is the country's largest city and its commercial hub. Equatorial Guinea is one of Africa's few Spanish-speaking countries, a legacy of Spanish colonial rule that makes it culturally distinct from its French and Portuguese-speaking neighbors.

The clock is West Africa Time, UTC+1. The IANA identifier is Africa/Malabo. No daylight saving.

Equatorial Guinea's oil discovery in the 1990s transformed it from one of Africa's poorest countries to one of its highest-income-per-capita countries within a generation. The wealth has not distributed evenly, but the oil industry has made Equatorial Guinea a significant player in Gulf of Guinea energy markets. Much of the operational activity is centered around Malabo, but Bata serves the mainland economy.

At 1.9 degrees north latitude, Bata sits almost exactly on the equator. Day length is essentially constant year-round: just over 12 hours of daylight every day. No seasonal variation, no argument for adjusting clocks. The equatorial climate is hot and humid throughout the year.

The Gulf of Guinea coastline here is lined with mangroves and tropical forest. Bata's rhythm is shaped by fishing, commerce, and the oil service economy.
