Santa Cruz de Tenerife is the co-capital of the Canary Islands, a Spanish archipelago sitting in the Atlantic some 1,700 kilometers from Madrid and only 100 kilometers from the northwest African coast. Geographically, the islands belong to the same longitude as the United Kingdom and Portugal. Their timezone reflects this: Western European Time (UTC+0 in winter, UTC+1 in summer), one hour behind mainland [Spain](/country/spain).

This creates the unusual situation of a Spanish city that is legally in Spain but operating on a different clock than the country's mainland. The practical consequence is that a Canary Islands business call at 10am local time reaches Madrid at 11am. Spaniards traveling from the peninsula need to set their watches back on arrival.

## mild climate and year-round daylight

The Canary Islands sit at about 28 degrees north, well south of mainland Europe. This means long summer evenings do not come in the same dramatic form as in northern Spain. The sun sets by around 9:30pm in June rather than past 10pm. The year-round mild climate makes daylight shifting less significant anyway: the temperature rarely drives indoor retreat in the way that winter cold does in Madrid or Barcelona.

[Las Palmas de Gran Canaria](/las-palmas) across the archipelago shares the same Atlantic/Canary timezone.

[9am in Santa Cruz de Tenerife is 9am in London](/when/9am-santa-cruz-de-tenerife/london) in winter. Both are on UTC+0. In summer, both shift to UTC+1 simultaneously, so the offset stays the same year-round.
