Kara sits in northern Togo at about 9 degrees north latitude, close enough to the equator that the sun rises and sets within a narrow band all year: roughly 6am to 6:30pm with little variation. The city runs on Africa/Lome time, which is GMT (UTC+0) year-round, the same timezone as [Lomé](/lome) on the coast and every city in [Togo](/country/togo).

This makes Kara one of the few cities in the world where legal clock time and solar noon align almost perfectly. True solar noon falls close to 12:00, which is unusual. Most cities are offset from their solar meridian by the political geometry of timezone borders.

Kara developed as an administrative hub during the French colonial period, and the weekly market still anchors the city's rhythms. Traders arrive from villages well before dawn, setting up by headlamp and lantern. By the time the sun clears the hills to the east, the market is already in full swing.

[Noon in Kara is noon in London in winter](/when/12pm-kara/london), because Togo's timezone exactly matches UTC, no offsets needed. In summer, when London moves to BST (UTC+1), Kara falls one hour behind.
