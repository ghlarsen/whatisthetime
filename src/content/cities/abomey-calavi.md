Abomey-Calavi sits just north of Cotonou, Benin's economic capital, and has grown into one of the country's most important cities in its own right. It is home to the University of Abomey-Calavi, Benin's largest university, which means its daily rhythm is partly shaped by student schedules layered over the city's original market and fishing community patterns.

Benin runs on West Africa Time, UTC+1. No daylight saving. The clock is fixed. [Africa/Porto-Novo](/country/benin) is the IANA identifier, named for the political capital, though Cotonou and Abomey-Calavi are where most of the country's economic life actually happens.

At this latitude, close to 6 degrees north, the sun behaves consistently. Days are roughly 11 to 13 hours long year-round. The variation is gentle, nothing like the dramatic seasonal swings of higher latitudes. This makes the static UTC+1 feel intuitive.

The city's university population has introduced a slightly later rhythm to some neighborhoods. Cafes and study spots in the student districts stay active past midnight. The older market areas follow their own older schedule, up before 6am and winding down by early afternoon.
