Ibb is a city in the central highlands of Yemen, known for its lush, green landscape fed by Yemen's highest rainfall. It runs on Arabia Standard Time, UTC+3, year-round, the same as [Aden](/aden) and all of Yemen. No daylight saving.

At UTC+3, Ibb shares its clock with [Riyadh](/riyadh), [Nairobi](/nairobi), and [Moscow](/moscow), despite being geographically and culturally distant from all of them.

## The green city in a water-stressed country

Ibb receives significantly more rainfall than most of Yemen, making it one of the few genuinely green parts of the Arabian Peninsula. Coffee and qat (the mildly stimulant leaf widely consumed in Yemen) are the main crops. The qat harvest and daily qat chewing sessions structure social time in Ibb in ways that no civil clock governs: afternoons from about 1pm onward are culturally reserved for qat gatherings, a deeply social ritual.

## Conflict and the disruption of civil time

Yemen's ongoing civil war, which intensified from 2015, has disrupted normal civil life across the country. Power outages, displacement, and economic collapse have made formal schedules less meaningful. The call to prayer from the city's mosques remains the most reliable time signal in a city where electricity is intermittent.
