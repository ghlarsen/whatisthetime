Hamhŭng is North Korea's second-largest city, an industrial center on the eastern coast of the Korean Peninsula. It runs on Pyongyang Time, UTC+9, the same as the capital and the same as [Seoul](/seoul) in South Korea.

North Korea's timezone history reflects political decisions more than solar logic. In August 2015, North Korea shifted its clocks back 30 minutes to UTC+8:30, creating a unique offset it called "Pyongyang Time," explicitly rejecting the Japanese timezone imposed during colonial rule. Then, in May 2018, ahead of an inter-Korean summit, North Korea moved its clocks forward again to match South Korea at UTC+9.

## An industrial city on the eastern coast

Hamhŭng is home to major chemical and industrial facilities. The city was heavily bombed during the Korean War and substantially rebuilt, partly with East German assistance, in a modernist grid pattern. The industrial schedule, with shift work and factory production quotas, dominates the city's daily rhythm in ways that solar time never has.

## Shared timezone, divided peninsula

Hamhŭng and [Incheon](/incheon) share the UTC+9 offset. The two cities are on the same peninsula, speak the same language, and now share the same timezone. The border between them is one of the most heavily militarized on Earth.
