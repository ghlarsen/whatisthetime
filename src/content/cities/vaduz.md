Vaduz is the capital of [Liechtenstein](/country/liechtenstein), a principality of about 38,000 people wedged between Switzerland and Austria, entirely landlocked within countries that are themselves largely landlocked. Liechtenstein and Uzbekistan are the world's two doubly landlocked countries: surrounded entirely by other landlocked nations.

The country keeps Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter and UTC+2 during summer DST, matching [Switzerland](/country/switzerland) and Austria on both sides. The `Europe/Vaduz` IANA identifier exists as a distinct entry, but the rules are identical to `Europe/Zurich`. The separation is a matter of sovereign record rather than practical difference.

Vaduz Castle sits above the capital on a hillside, home to the ruling Princely House. The Prince of Liechtenstein is an active constitutional monarch, and the small scale of the country means the head of state's presence is not abstract. Residents can, and do, meet him at local events.

## sovereign identifier, shared Zurich clock

Liechtenstein has one of the world's highest per capita incomes, driven by financial services and precision manufacturing. The business day in Vaduz runs on Central European business hours, tightly coordinated with Zurich and Vienna on either side.

[9am in Vaduz is 8am in London](/when/9am-vaduz/london). [Zurich](/zurich) a short drive away shares the same clock.
