Port Moresby has always had an unusual relationship with its own country. The capital sits on the southern coast of Papua New Guinea, separated from the Highlands and the densely populated interior by the Owen Stanley Range. There are no roads connecting the capital to most of the country. The Kokoda Track begins in the mountains north of [Popondetta](/popondetta). Port Moresby connects to the rest by air.

[Papua New Guinea](/country/papua-new-guinea) runs on PNG Time, UTC+10, year-round. No daylight saving. At 9 degrees south latitude, daylight variation is minimal: the sun rises around 6am and sets around 6pm with modest seasonal shift.

The harbor that gives Port Moresby its character was named by Captain John Moresby in 1873, who claimed it for Britain. The city that grew around it became the administrative center of what was then British New Guinea, later Papua, later merged into the post-independence capital.

Port Moresby hosts the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) meetings and sees itself as a Pacific hub. The time zone, UTC+10, aligns it with eastern Australia: Brisbane is on the same clock, Sydney and Melbourne are one to two hours ahead depending on Australia's DST cycles.
