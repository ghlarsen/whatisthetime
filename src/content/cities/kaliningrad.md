Kaliningrad is a Russian exclave on the Baltic Sea, surrounded by Poland to the south and Lithuania to the north and east. It has no land connection to the rest of Russia. It runs on Eastern European Time, UTC+2, year-round, making it 2 hours behind Moscow.

This is unusual. Every other part of Russia is on Moscow Time or ahead of it. Kaliningrad is the only Russian territory that runs behind Moscow, a consequence of its geographic isolation at the western edge of Europe.

## Königsberg to Kaliningrad

The city was Königsberg until 1945, when the Soviet Union seized it from Germany at the end of World War II and renamed it Kaliningrad. The German population was expelled and replaced by Soviet settlers. The philosopher Immanuel Kant was born and spent his entire life in Königsberg, famously so punctual that neighbors set their clocks by his daily walks.

The city's complete demographic replacement after 1945 means that the living connection to its Kantian punctuality tradition was severed. The modern city is Russian in culture.

## The timezone isolation

Kaliningrad has changed its timezone several times. After the Soviet Union adopted daylight saving in 1981, Kaliningrad followed Moscow's schedule, which put it at UTC+3 in winter and UTC+4 in summer. When Russia abolished DST in 2014, Kaliningrad was left at UTC+3, which felt too far ahead for its geography. In 2014, Kaliningrad was moved to UTC+2, its current permanent position.
