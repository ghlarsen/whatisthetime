Brazzaville is the capital of the Republic of the Congo (Congo-Brazzaville), sitting on the north bank of the Congo River. On the opposite bank, visible from the waterfront, is Kinshasa, capital of the Democratic Republic of Congo. The two capitals face each other across one of the world's great rivers, and they run on the same clock: West Africa Time, UTC+1.

The Republic of the Congo uses Africa/Brazzaville: UTC+1, year-round, no daylight saving time. The DRC's western region, where Kinshasa sits, also uses UTC+1. This means the twin capitals share both a view and a timezone, simplifying the constant cross-river commerce and family connections between them.

## The pool and the crossing

The Congo River widens into a massive pool, the Malebo Pool, at this point. The pool is several kilometers across, and both cities developed on its banks. The ferries that cross the pool carry goods, workers, families, and traders. The crossing takes about 20 minutes.

Stanley Pool, as it was known during the colonial era, was named after Henry Morton Stanley, who explored the region in the 1870s. The two colonial powers, France (on the north bank) and Belgium (on the south), built their capitals facing each other, creating this unusual geopolitical mirror image.

## Near-equatorial time

Brazzaville sits just south of the equator, at about 4 degrees south. Daylight is consistent year-round. Sunrise around 5:45am, sunset around 6pm. The UTC+1 offset keeps solar noon at approximately 12:45pm, a reasonable alignment for the 15-degree east meridian.

The [time difference between Brazzaville and London](/when/9am-brazzaville/london) is one hour in winter, zero in summer when the UK observes BST.
