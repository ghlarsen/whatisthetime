Yelizovo sits about 30 kilometers from Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky on the Kamchatka Peninsula, and it houses Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky Airport, the main gateway to the entire peninsula. For a remote region accessible by air, the airport's timezone and schedule coordination with the rest of [Russia](/country/russia) is the primary temporal link to the outside world.

Kamchatka Time, UTC+12, is the clock, applied year-round since Russia's 2014 elimination of DST. At UTC+12, Yelizovo is among the first permanently inhabited places to enter each new day.

The Kamchatka Peninsula has 29 active volcanoes, making it one of the most volcanically active places on Earth. Klyuchevskaya Sopka, the highest active volcano in Eurasia, erupts frequently. Ash clouds from Kamchatka's volcanoes directly affect aviation, and the airport at Yelizovo operates with awareness that local eruptions can close the facility with little warning.

## volcanic eruptions and airport operations

At 53 degrees north on the Pacific coast, Yelizovo gets significant daylight variation moderated by the maritime climate. The Pacific keeps temperatures more moderate than inland Siberia, but winters are still serious, and the summer salmon runs in local rivers set a biological clock that many residents organize their year around.

[9am in Yelizovo is 9pm the previous day in London](/when/9am-yelizovo/london). [Vilyuchinsk](/vilyuchinsk) on the peninsula shares Kamchatka Time.
