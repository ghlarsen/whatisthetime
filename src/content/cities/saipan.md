Saipan is the most populous island of the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands, a US territory in the Pacific that sits about 200 kilometers north of Guam. The island has been Spanish, German, Japanese, and since WWII, American. Each layer of history is visible in different ways: Spanish architecture in some churches, Japanese war memorials on the cliffsides, American fast food chains on the main road.

The [Northern Mariana Islands](/country/northern-mariana-islands) use Chamorro Standard Time, UTC+10, year-round without daylight saving. Saipan shares this clock with Guam to the south, which uses the same identifier but has its own entry in the IANA database.

At 15 degrees north, Saipan has minimal daylight variation: the sun rises and sets within a roughly 30-minute range year-round. The climate is tropical: warm, with a wet season from July to November when typhoons are possible. The typhoon season is one of the island's major annual rhythms, a period when the Pacific's power is felt directly.

The Battle of Saipan in June-July 1944 was one of the Pacific War's decisive actions. Nearly 30,000 Japanese soldiers and thousands of civilians died on the island. The Banzai Cliff and Suicide Cliff are preserved memorial sites where civilians jumped rather than surrender.
