Gros Islet is a town on the northern tip of Saint Lucia, known for its Friday night street party that has run for decades. It runs on Atlantic Standard Time, UTC-4, year-round. No daylight saving in Saint Lucia.

UTC-4 is the same offset as [Barbados](/bridgetown) and other eastern Caribbean islands that have opted for a fixed clock. When [New York](/new-york) shifts to daylight saving in spring, Saint Lucia does not follow, creating a gap: New York goes to UTC-4 and briefly matches Saint Lucia's clock before moving to UTC-4 and creating a one-hour difference.

## The Friday night street party

Gros Islet's Friday Jump-Up, the weekly street party on the main road, starts after dark and runs until early morning. This rhythm, tied entirely to nighttime and social time rather than the solar day, is one of the most distinctive time markers in the eastern Caribbean. The whole town reorganizes itself around Friday evening from around 9pm.

## Tropical latitude and consistent light

Saint Lucia sits at 14 degrees North latitude. Day length varies minimally through the year. Sunrise stays between 5:40 and 6:30am all year, sunset between 6:00 and 6:45pm. With such consistency, daylight saving offers no practical benefit, and the island has never adopted it.
