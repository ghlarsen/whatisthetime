Rustavi was built to make steel. The Soviet planners chose the location in the Kura River valley, near Tbilisi, for a major metallurgical combine in the 1940s. The city that grew around the plant was a showpiece of Soviet industrialization: wide boulevards, workers' apartments, a football club. The Rustavi Metallurgical Plant, now privatized, still operates, though at a fraction of its Soviet-era scale.

[Georgia](/country/georgia) uses Georgia Standard Time, UTC+4, year-round with no daylight saving. Rustavi shares this fixed clock with [Tbilisi](/tbilisi) just 25 kilometers northwest. The country moved to UTC+4 permanently in 2004, abandoning the DST system it had used under Soviet influence.

At 41 degrees north, Rustavi has warm summers and cold winters in the Kura River valley. The valley is lower and more arid than the mountains surrounding it. Snow arrives most winters, though not with the intensity of the Georgian highland towns.

## football culture and industrial identity

Rustavi FC, one of Georgia's historic football clubs, gives the industrial city a cultural marker beyond the steelworks. Georgian football culture, particularly around Tbilisi's Dinamo, has always been intense, and Rustavi holds its own place in that tradition.
