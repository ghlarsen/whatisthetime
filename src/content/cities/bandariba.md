Bandariba is a neighborhood in Curaçao, the Caribbean island that forms part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands. Curaçao is a constituent country of the Kingdom, with its own government and currency, distinct from both the Netherlands and the other Dutch Caribbean islands.

The clock is Atlantic Standard Time, UTC-4. The IANA identifier is America/Curacao. No daylight saving.

Curaçao's permanent UTC-4 keeps it aligned with the eastern Caribbean year-round. Most of the island's Caribbean neighbors, including [Baie-Mahault](/baie-mahault) in Guadeloupe and [Basseterre](/basseterre) in Saint Kitts, share this offset or use it for most of the year.

Curaçao's economy is anchored by oil refining, tourism, and offshore financial services. The island's Schottegat harbor is one of the Caribbean's largest natural deep-water harbors, and the ISLA refinery complex has processed Venezuelan crude oil since 1918.

The island's Dutch-influenced architecture, particularly in Willemstad's Punda and Otrobanda districts, is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The pastel-colored buildings along St. Anna Bay are among the most photographed in the Caribbean.

Bandariba's own character is quieter and more residential than the commercial districts of Willemstad. Life here follows the rhythms of a small Caribbean community: morning market activity, afternoon heat, evening coastal breeze.
