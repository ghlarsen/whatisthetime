Andorra la Vella is the smallest capital in Europe by area and one of its highest, sitting at 1,023 meters in the Pyrenees mountains. Andorra is a microstate squeezed between [France](/country/france) and [Spain](/country/spain), with both countries serving as co-princes under a constitutional arrangement dating to 1278.

The clock is Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter and UTC+2 during daylight saving. The IANA identifier is Europe/Andorra. This aligns Andorra with both its larger neighbors, since France and Spain both use Central European Time. Border crossings don't require watch adjustments.

Andorra's economy is built substantially on its status as a tax haven and duty-free shopping destination. Millions of visitors come each year to buy electronics, alcohol, tobacco, and perfume at prices lower than neighboring countries. This tourism economy means Andorra's commercial district operates at the convenience of visitors coming from Barcelona (2 hours away) and Toulouse (3 hours away).

At 1,023 meters, the capital has alpine weather. Winters are cold and snowy. Summer evenings cool quickly. The seasonal rhythm is strongly felt: ski season draws visitors from November to April, hiking and cycling season from May to October. The city's heartbeat follows this visitor calendar more than any clock-based rhythm.

[Barcelona](/barcelona) is two hours south and shares the same timezone. [9am in Andorra la Vella is 8am in London](/when/9am-andorra-la-vella/london) during European winter.
