Marigot is the capital of Saint-Martin, the French overseas collectivity that shares the island of Saint Martin with the Dutch territory Sint Maarten. The island is one of the smallest territories in the world divided between two nations. Marigot runs on Atlantic Standard Time, UTC-4, with no daylight saving.

The island's shared status means two currencies, two languages, and two administrations on a tiny 88-square-kilometer piece of land. The Dutch side runs on AST as well, so at least the clocks match even as everything else differs. There is no border control between the two sides.

## two nations, one island, one clock

Saint Martin is a major Caribbean tourist destination, and the Princess Juliana International Airport on the Dutch side is one of the most photographed airports in the world for its low approach path over a beach. Marigot's day runs on tourist rhythms: beach activity in the mornings, afternoon shopping in the French capital's market, late dinners.

[9am in Marigot is 1pm in London](/when/9am-marigot/london) during European winter, four hours behind UTC.
