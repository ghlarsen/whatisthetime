Bununka Kunda is a small settlement in Gambia's Central River Division, deep in the narrow strip of territory that constitutes the country along the Gambia River. It runs on Africa/Banjul: UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. No daylight saving time is observed.

The Gambia is one of the world's smallest and most unusually shaped countries: a narrow riverine territory surrounded on three sides by Senegal. Bununka Kunda sits roughly in the middle of this strip, far from the capital Banjul near the coast but still part of the same country and the same timezone.

## The river as road

In much of Gambia's interior, the Gambia River is the primary transportation route. Communities like Bununka Kunda are oriented toward the river: pirogues (wooden boats) carry goods and people, and market days are often timed around river traffic rather than road schedules. The river's tidal influences extend far inland, affecting when boats can navigate certain sections.

The UTC+0 timezone means this community's clock matches the coast and matches Senegal across the border. Agricultural schedules, fishing rhythms, and market days all operate on natural light cycles that align well with the UTC+0 offset at this latitude (about 13 degrees north).

## Groundnut economy

The Gambia's economy has historically been organized around groundnut (peanut) cultivation. The harvest season in late autumn sets the most important annual economic rhythm in rural communities like Bununka Kunda. The agricultural calendar is as important as the clock calendar in organizing rural life here.

The [time difference between Bununka Kunda and London](/when/9am-bununka-kunda/london) is zero in winter, one hour behind when the UK observes BST. [Brikama](/brikama) near the coast is on the same clock.
