Herceg Novi sits at the mouth of the Bay of Kotor in Montenegro, a medieval walled town overlooking the Adriatic. It runs on Central European Time: UTC+1 in winter, UTC+2 in summer. The timezone is Europe/Podgorica, shared with Montenegro's capital.

At 42.5 degrees North, Herceg Novi has Mediterranean-influenced weather and light. Summer days are long and warm. The Adriatic glitters in the late afternoon sun, and the city's terraced hillsides fill with tourists. The daylight saving shift to UTC+2 in summer gives an extra hour of usable evening light, which the tourism economy appreciates.

## A city that changes hands with its clock

Herceg Novi has been under Venetian, Ottoman, Spanish, Russian, French, Austrian, and Yugoslav rule. Each occupying power brought its own administrative calendar. The Venetians ran the city from the fifteenth century and brought their commercial clock discipline. The Ottomans introduced Islamic prayer time schedules. Austria standardized civil time across the empire in the nineteenth century.

Montenegro became independent in 2006 and adopted Central European Time as its standard, aligning with the EU neighbors it aspires to join.

## The botanical legacy and the seasonal clock

Herceg Novi is famous for its flowering plants and mild winters. The city celebrates Mimosa Festival in February, when Mediterranean winters give way to early spring blooms. This seasonal awareness, tied to the flowering calendar rather than the civil clock, is a local form of time reckoning.
