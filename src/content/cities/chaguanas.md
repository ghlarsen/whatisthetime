Chaguanas is the most populous municipality in Trinidad and Tobago and one of the fastest-growing urban areas in the Caribbean. It sits in the center of Trinidad's main island. It runs on America/Port_of_Spain: UTC-4, Atlantic Standard Time, year-round. Trinidad and Tobago does not observe daylight saving time.

Chaguanas has grown as a commercial and retail center, particularly after the development of shopping malls in the 1980s and 1990s drew commerce away from the older urban centers of Port of Spain and San Fernando. The city's central location on the island makes it a natural distribution hub.

## Trinidadian Indian heritage

Chaguanas and the central belt of Trinidad has the highest concentration of Indo-Trinidadian population, descendants of indentured laborers brought from India by the British after the abolition of slavery to work the sugar plantations. V.S. Naipaul, the Nobel Prize-winning author, was born in Chaguanas in 1932. His novels, including "A House for Mr Biswas," draw directly on this community's experience of displacement, adaptation, and the construction of new identities in a new world.

The Hindu and Muslim festivals observed by the Indo-Trinidadian community, Diwali, Eid, Phagwa, add a lunar and solar calendar dimension to the island's rhythm that sits alongside the fixed UTC-4 clock.

## Oil, gas, and Carnival time

Trinidad has one of the Caribbean's most significant petroleum industries. The oil and gas sector creates a workforce that interacts constantly with Houston, London, and other energy hub timezones. But the country is also the home of Carnival, the pre-Lenten celebration that is perhaps the Caribbean's most famous festival. Carnival's calendar, set by the Catholic liturgical year, is as important as any clock.

The [time conversion from Chaguanas to London](/when/9am-chaguanas/london) puts London four hours ahead in winter, five in summer when the UK observes BST.
