Al Mansourah is one of Doha's older commercial and residential districts, a dense urban neighborhood that predates the wave of contemporary development that transformed Qatar's capital. It is not a separate city but part of greater Doha's fabric, known for its market streets, traditional fabric shops, and older apartment blocks that stand alongside newer construction.

The clock is Arabia Standard Time, UTC+3. Qatar adopted this timezone and has maintained it without daylight saving adjustments. The IANA identifier is Asia/Qatar.

## A stable clock for global business

Qatar's stable timezone is partly a financial decision. Doha's ambitions as a global financial and events hub are better served by a fixed clock that doesn't shift every spring and autumn. The Qatar Investment Authority and the Qatar Stock Exchange operate on schedules that benefit from predictable offsets from London and New York.

[Ar Rayyan](/ar-rayyan), another large Qatar municipality, shares the same clock, as does the rest of the country. Qatar is small enough that a single timezone covers everything without controversy.

At 25 degrees north, Qatar gets plenty of daylight but not extreme variation. Winter days run about 10 hours. Summer days run about 14. The case for adjusting clocks seasonally is weak at this latitude, and Qatar has never found a compelling reason to make the change.

[9am in Al Mansourah is 6am in London](/when/9am-al-mansurah/london) during European winter.
