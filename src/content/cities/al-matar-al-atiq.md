Al Maţār al 'Atīq translates roughly as "the old airport," a name that marks this Doha district's history as the site of Qatar's original international airport before Hamad International Airport opened in 2014. The old terminal area has since been redeveloped, but the neighborhood name persists.

Like all of [Qatar](/country/qatar), this district runs on Arabia Standard Time, UTC+3. The IANA identifier is Asia/Qatar. No daylight saving. The clock is fixed.

## Redevelopment after the airport moved

The area sits in the central part of Doha and has seen significant urban redevelopment since the airport moved. Residential blocks, commercial strips, and the layered texture of a mid-city neighborhood that is neither the oldest part of Doha nor the newest.

Qatar's single timezone covers the entire country without complication. It is a small nation, roughly 11,500 square kilometers, and longitude differences across the country are negligible. [Al Mansourah](/al-mansurah) to the west and [Ar Rayyan](/ar-rayyan) to the south all run on the same clock.

The district's proximity to the old airport meant it historically had a transient population of airline crew, travelers, and logistics workers. That particular rhythm has shifted to the new airport's vicinity, but the clock remains the same.

[9am here is 6am in London](/when/9am-al-matar-al-atiq/london) during European winter.
