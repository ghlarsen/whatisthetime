Juan Díaz is a corregimiento (district) in Panama Province, immediately east of Panama City. It runs on UTC-5 year-round. Panama does not observe daylight saving.

Panama's use of UTC-5 (Eastern Standard Time) year-round means it matches New York in winter but runs an hour behind in summer when the US shifts to daylight saving. The Panama Canal's international operations influence this preference for a stable, predictable timezone.

## The Canal and international time

The Panama Canal connects the Atlantic and Pacific oceans and handles about 5% of world trade. Ships from every timezone transit the canal. The Panama Canal Authority schedules transits, manages queues, and coordinates with port authorities on both ends of the canal. This operation uses UTC for navigation and scheduling, while the administrative office uses Panama's UTC-5.

## Life east of the capital

Juan Díaz has grown rapidly as Panama City's urban expansion pushed eastward. Residential neighborhoods, shopping centers, and commercial strips have replaced agricultural land over the past two decades. The suburban rhythm, commuting to Panama City for work, shopping centers in the afternoon, evening activities at home, is the defining daily schedule.

At 9 degrees North latitude, day length varies minimally. Sunrise is around 6:15am year-round, sunset around 6:30pm.
