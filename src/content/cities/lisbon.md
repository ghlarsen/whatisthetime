Lisbon is the westernmost capital in continental Europe, sitting at 38.7 degrees north on the Tagus estuary, nine degrees of longitude west of the prime meridian. It uses Western European Time: UTC+0 in winter (WET), UTC+1 in summer (WEST). This puts Lisbon one hour behind its neighbor Madrid, despite the two cities being on almost the same longitude.

The discrepancy with Spain is political. Portugal and Spain are geographically close to the same meridian, but Spain uses Central European Time (UTC+1/+2) for political reasons, aligning with France and Germany rather than the sun. Portugal uses its natural time, UTC+0 in winter.

## the Atlantic light and the gap with Madrid

This means that in summer, both cities use UTC+1 (Lisbon as WEST, Madrid as CET standard), and they align. In winter, Portugal reverts to UTC+0 while Spain stays at UTC+1. The gap appears and disappears twice a year.

Lisbon at 38 degrees north has moderate seasonal daylight variation: about 9.5 hours in December, 14.5 in June. The Atlantic influence keeps the climate mild. The famous Lisbon light, the quality that has drawn painters and photographers for centuries, has to do with the Atlantic moisture and the city's particular white-and-blue color palette, reflecting that light differently than any other European city.

[La Laguna](/la-laguna) in the Canary Islands shares the same winter UTC+0 time. [9am in Lisbon is 9am in London](/when/9am-lisbon/london) in winter, the same clock.
