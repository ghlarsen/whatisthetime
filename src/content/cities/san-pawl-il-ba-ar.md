San Pawl il-Baħar, known in English as St. Paul's Bay, takes its name from the account in Acts 27-28 of Paul's shipwreck on Malta in approximately 60 AD. The bay on Malta's northern coast is traditionally identified as the landing site. The town built around that memory keeps Central European Time (UTC+1 in winter, UTC+2 in summer), the same clock as [Valletta](/valletta) and [Sliema](/sliema) across the island.

## Mediterranean light and the quiet season

Malta sits at about 36 degrees north, far enough south to enjoy genuine Mediterranean light. In summer, daylight lasts past 8pm. The bay fills with tourists and pleasure craft, and the pace slows in the heat of the afternoon. In winter, the island is quieter, the light more horizontal, the churches and fishing boats more visible without the crowds.

The [country](/country/malta) operates on a single timezone with the rest of Central Europe, observing DST in line with EU rules. This positions Malta on the same clock as much of the continent's business and administrative life, despite being geographically closer to North Africa.

[9am in San Pawl il-Baħar is 8am in London](/when/9am-san-pawl-il-ba-ar/london), the standard one-hour gap that holds through most of the year.
