Koutiala sits in southern Mali, in the center of the country's cotton-growing zone. Mali is one of Africa's largest cotton producers, and Koutiala is a hub of that industry, with ginning facilities, transport links, and a busy market serving a large agricultural hinterland. The city runs on Africa/Bamako time: GMT, UTC+0, year-round.

Mali kept UTC+0 after independence from France in 1960, a logical choice given the country's geographic position near the prime meridian. No daylight saving has ever been observed.

At 12 degrees north, Koutiala receives close to 12 hours of daylight year-round. The Sahel climate means the critical annual division is the rainy season (roughly June through September) versus the dry season. Cotton is planted with the first rains and harvested in the dry months. Agricultural activity peaks in October and November, when the harvest comes in and the trading season begins.

The city's market days draw traders from a wide radius. Market logistics, including transport of cotton bales and consumer goods, follow the agricultural and commercial calendar more than any clock-based schedule.
