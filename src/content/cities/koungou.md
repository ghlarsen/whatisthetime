Koungou occupies the northern tip of Grande-Terre in Mayotte, where the island narrows before the coast curves around to face the strait separating the main island from Petite-Terre. The commune runs on Indian/Mayotte time, UTC+3, the same clock as [Kaouéni](/kaoueni) to the south and all of [Mayotte](/country/mayotte).

## migration, French schools, and Indian Ocean tides

The northern shore of Mayotte faces toward the Comoros islands to the north, and the fishing communities in Koungou have historically maintained connections to that wider archipelago despite the political divide between Mayotte (French) and the Comoros (independent). Fishing schedules follow the tide and the fish, not the administrative clock.

Koungou has experienced rapid population growth as Mayotte has seen large migration from the Comoros, driven by the territory's status as part of France with access to French social services. The commune's infrastructure has been under strain, with new housing and schools built to handle demand. Schools here run on the French academic calendar, timed to European rhythms despite being in the Indian Ocean at UTC+3.

At 12 degrees south, Koungou has mild seasonal variation in daylight: 11 to 13 hours across the year. The cyclone season from November through April brings meteorological drama more relevant to daily life than any clock transition.
