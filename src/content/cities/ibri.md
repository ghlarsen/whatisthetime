'Ibrī is a city in the Ad Dhahirah region of Oman's interior, historically an important oasis and trading center on routes between the Gulf coast and the interior of Arabia. It runs on Arabia Standard Time, UTC+4, year-round, the same as [Muscat](/muscat) and all of Oman. No daylight saving.

Oman's UTC+4 offset is one hour ahead of neighboring Saudi Arabia's UTC+3, a difference that exists despite significant cross-border economic integration. Oman chose this offset to align better with its eastern trade partners: [Dubai](/dubai) and the UAE also use UTC+4.

## Falconry and the desert calendar

'Ibrī's interior location and Bedouin heritage mean that traditional time systems remain culturally significant. Falconry seasons, tied to the migration of prey birds, structure autumn and winter. Dates, Oman's most important traditional crop, ripen in late summer and require harvest schedules tied to the agricultural calendar rather than the clock.

## Ancient copper mines and modern infrastructure

The Wadi Jizzi region near 'Ibrī has been mined for copper since at least 3000 BCE, supplying copper to ancient Mesopotamia. Today, the region has modern road infrastructure, oil production facilities, and a growing commercial sector tied to the regional economy.
