Sumqayıt sits on the Caspian coast about 30 kilometers north of Baku, the capital of [Azerbaijan](/country/azerbaijan). It is a major industrial city, built up in the Soviet era as a petrochemical and steel production center. The city keeps Azerbaijan Time (UTC+4) all year. Azerbaijan abolished daylight saving time in 2016, maintaining the fixed UTC+4 offset.

The city's Soviet industrial heritage is marked in its urban fabric: wide streets built for heavy vehicles, large factory complexes, and residential blocks that housed industrial workers. The petrochemical plants that produced much of the Soviet Union's chemicals are still largely present, though many are in various states of operation or remediation.

At about 40 degrees north, Sumqayıt has meaningful seasonal variation: roughly 15 hours of daylight in June and about 9.5 hours in December. The Caspian Sea, which the city faces, moderates the temperature somewhat, though Baku's winds, locally known as the khazri, bring cold dry air off the Caspian in winter.

Azerbaijan's fixed UTC+4 aligns the country with Oman and the UAE, and also with Armenia, despite the two countries' ongoing territorial tensions.

[Shengavit](/shengavit) in neighboring Armenia keeps the same UTC+4 offset.

[9am in Sumqayıt is 5am in London](/when/9am-sumqay-t/london) in winter, 6am during British Summer Time.
