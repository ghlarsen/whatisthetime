Carnot is a city in the Mambéré-Kadéï prefecture of the western Central African Republic, near the border with Cameroon. It runs on Africa/Bangui: UTC+1, West Africa Time, year-round. The Central African Republic uses a single timezone throughout.

Carnot sits at about 5 degrees north latitude, in the equatorial forest zone of western CAR. Daylight is consistent year-round: sunrise around 6am, sunset around 6:15pm. The brief equatorial twilight means the transition from day to night is rapid.

## Diamonds in the forest

Carnot is in one of the Central African Republic's diamond-mining regions. Artisanal diamond mining in the western CAR is a significant informal economic activity, with diggers working riverbeds and alluvial deposits. The Kimberley Process, an international certification scheme for conflict diamonds, has flagged CAR diamonds at various points due to the country's instability.

The broader CAR conflict that began in 2012 affected the western regions, including the Carnot area, as armed groups fought for control of resource-rich territory.

## Forest ecology and the clock

Carnot is in the transition zone between the dense equatorial forest to the south and the savanna woodlands to the north. Agricultural and forest-based activities, including logging and small-scale farming, follow seasonal rainfall patterns rather than fixed clock schedules. The rainy season (roughly April to November) and dry season structure economic life in ways that transcend official time.

The [time difference between Carnot and London](/when/9am-carnot/london) is one hour in winter, zero in summer when the UK observes BST. [Bimbo](/bimbo) in the eastern part of the country is on the same clock.
