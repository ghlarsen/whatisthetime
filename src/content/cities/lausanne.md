Lausanne sits above Lake Geneva on terraced hillsides, a city that climbs steeply from the lakefront to the cathedral on the ridge above. It runs on Europe/Zurich time: UTC+1 in winter (CET), UTC+2 in summer (CEST). Switzerland follows EU DST transitions as a practical matter, despite not being an EU member.

The International Olympic Committee has been headquartered in Lausanne since 1915, making the city the administrative capital of international sports. The IOC's headquarters coordinates events on six continents, and Lausanne time (Central European) is the reference for much of that coordination. Every Olympic event time listed in the program is ultimately reconciled against the CET clock in this city.

## 700 years of the cathedral nightwatch

Lausanne Cathedral has maintained a "nightwatch" since the 13th century: a watchman calls out the hours from 10pm to 2am from the cathedral tower, every night, a practice unbroken for 700 years. In a city of precision watchmaking culture and diplomatic timekeeping, this ceremonial relationship with the clock feels entirely appropriate.

At 46 degrees north, the seasonal light difference is noticeable but not extreme. Summer evenings extend past 9:30pm during CEST. Winter afternoons dim by 5pm.

[9am in Lausanne is 8am in London](/when/9am-lausanne/london) in winter, one hour ahead of GMT.
