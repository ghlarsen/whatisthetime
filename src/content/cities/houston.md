Houston runs on Central Standard Time, UTC-6 in winter and Central Daylight Time, UTC-5, from mid-March to early November. It shares the America/Chicago timezone with [Chicago](/chicago), Dallas, and Minneapolis.

The clock change happens the second Sunday in March and the first Sunday in November. Houston follows standard US federal daylight saving law.

## The energy capital's trading clock

Houston is the center of the global oil and gas industry. The energy trading desks here coordinate with [London](/london) (the ICE Futures exchange), [New York](/new-york) (NYMEX), [Dubai](/dubai), and [Singapore](/singapore). Crude oil prices are quoted continuously 24 hours a day.

At UTC-6, Houston's morning begins when London's trading day is already half over. Energy traders in Houston are often at their desks by 6am to catch the tail end of the European market.

## NASA and mission time

The Johnson Space Center in Houston controls human spaceflight missions, and mission operations run on a separate time system: Mission Elapsed Time (MET) counts from launch, not from any civil clock. Ground controllers work rotating shifts to cover continuous operations. "Houston" in "Houston, we have a problem" refers to this control center, operating on its own temporal logic independent of Central Time.
