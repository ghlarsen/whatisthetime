Freetown, Sierra Leone's capital, sits on a mountainous peninsula at the junction of the Sierra Leone River and the Atlantic Ocean. It runs on GMT, UTC+0, year-round, under the IANA identifier `Africa/Freetown`. No daylight saving.

## founded for freedom, shaped by the harbor

Founded in 1792 as a settlement for freed slaves, Freetown has one of the most significant histories of African freedom struggles in the Atlantic world. The name was not chosen casually. The city's founding population included Black Loyalists from Nova Scotia, freed slaves from Britain, and survivors of the abolition movement's practical work. Their descendants, the Krio people, continue to shape the city's culture and identity.

At 8.5 degrees north, Freetown has stable sunrise and sunset times: around 6:15am and 6:45pm in both equinox periods, varying by less than an hour across the year. GMT is geographically appropriate for the city's longitude (13.2 degrees west). Like [Dakar](/dakar) and [Conakry](/conakry) nearby, Freetown is part of the GMT belt along West Africa's Atlantic bulge. The harbor, the main economic driver, operates day and night regardless of the clock.
