Malatia-Sebastia is one of Yerevan's twelve administrative districts, covering the southern part of the Armenian capital. Like all of [Armenia](/country/armenia), it runs on Armenia Time, UTC+4, year-round. Armenia abandoned daylight saving time in 2012, setting clocks permanently to UTC+4.

Armenia's naming of this district reflects its historical connection to the Armenian communities of Anatolia. Malatia and Sebastia (modern Malatya and Sivas in Turkey) were centers of Armenian population before the 1915 Genocide. The district's name is a form of collective memory, marking the geography of loss.

Yerevan sits at about 40 degrees north latitude, where day length varies significantly: roughly 9 hours in December, 15 hours in June. The absence of DST means summer evenings get long by the clock, with sunset not until 8:30pm in July. Cafes and outdoor gatherings run late into the evening.

[9am in Malatia-Sebastia is 5am in London](/when/9am-malatia-sebastia/london) during European summer, when the UK shifts to BST and the four-hour gap reduces to three.
