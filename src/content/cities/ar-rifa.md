Ar Rifa is Bahrain's second city and the home of the Riffa Palace complex, the primary residence of Bahrain's royal family. It sits in the southern part of the country, on a ridge that offers one of the higher elevations in an otherwise flat archipelago. The city is predominantly Sunni in character, with a different demographic texture than some of Bahrain's other districts.

The clock is Arabia Standard Time, UTC+3. The IANA identifier is Asia/Bahrain. No daylight saving. Bahrain has maintained this stable clock for decades.

[Al Muharraq](/al-muharraq), Bahrain's northern city and historic pearl diving capital, shares the same timezone. The whole country runs on a single clock, which is easy to maintain given Bahrain's tiny size, roughly 760 square kilometers.

Ar Rifa's Royal Golf Club and some of Bahrain's premium residential developments are in this area, giving the city a more affluent character than the dense commercial districts of Manama. The working rhythms here follow Bahrain's official schedule: Sunday through Thursday, with Friday-Saturday as the weekend.

## Linked to Saudi Arabia by the causeway

The proximity to Saudi Arabia, just across the King Fahd Causeway, means Bahrain's economy and rhythms are closely tied to the Saudi mainland. Saudi Arabia is also UTC+3, making cross-causeway business straightforward from a timezone perspective.
