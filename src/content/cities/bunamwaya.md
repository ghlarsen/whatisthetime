Bunamwaya is a residential suburb south of Kampala, Uganda's capital. It runs on Africa/Kampala: UTC+3, East Africa Time, year-round. Uganda does not observe daylight saving time.

Uganda straddles the equator. Kampala and its suburbs, including Bunamwaya, sit very close to 0 degrees latitude, which means daylight is almost perfectly consistent year-round: sunrise around 6:30am, sunset around 6:45pm, with variations of only a few minutes through the seasons. The sun rises steeply and sets steeply. Twilight is brief.

## Kampala's suburban sprawl

Kampala is one of Africa's fastest-growing cities, expanding rapidly into surrounding hills and valleys. Bunamwaya, on the southern edge of the metropolitan area, is a mix of middle-class residential development, small commercial strips, and agricultural land. Ugandan urban growth has been characterized by a dispersed, low-density pattern that fills in valleys and hills rather than building upward.

The city's layout across multiple hills means that local sunrise and sunset times can vary slightly depending on which hill you're on, but the official clock is the same for all of greater Kampala.

## East Africa's consistent clock

Uganda shares UTC+3 with [Kenya](/nairobi), [Tanzania](/dar-es-salaam), and [Ethiopia](/addis-ababa). The regional timezone consistency across East Africa is one of the most stable in the world: these countries have used UTC+3 without adjustment for decades. Cross-border commerce, tourism, and the regional integration under the East African Community are all simplified by the shared clock.

The [time difference between Bunamwaya and London](/when/9am-bunamwaya/london) is three hours in winter, two in summer when the UK observes BST.
