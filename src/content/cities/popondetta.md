Popondetta is the town where the Kokoda Track begins, or ends, depending on which direction you're walking. The Owen Stanley Range rises immediately to the southwest. During World War II, the track through these mountains became the site of some of the most brutal jungle fighting in the Pacific campaign. Australian and Japanese forces fought for months in terrain that the time of year barely changes: hot, wet, relentless.

[Papua New Guinea](/country/papua-new-guinea) runs on Papua New Guinea Time, UTC+10, year-round with no daylight saving. Popondetta and [Port Moresby](/port-moresby) on the south coast share this clock. Close to the equator at 8 degrees south, daylight variation is minimal: the sun rises close to 6am and sets close to 6pm regardless of season.

The Kokoda Track walk takes most hikers 8 to 12 days in either direction. The schedule is driven by how many daylight hours you can physically use before heat and humidity and mountains stop you. The sun, not the clock, defines the day on the track.

Popondetta town itself sits in the Oro River valley, a flat agricultural lowland surrounded by volcanic soil that produces some of Papua New Guinea's finest cocoa and rubber. The market opens early, before the heat of the day builds.
