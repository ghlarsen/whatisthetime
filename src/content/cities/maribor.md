Maribor is Slovenia's second-largest city and sits in the northeastern part of the country on the Drava River, close to the Austrian border. It follows Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter, UTC+2 during summer daylight saving, the same as [Ljubljana](/ljubljana) and the rest of [Slovenia](/country/slovenia).

The city is the heart of Slovenia's wine country, particularly the Styrian wine region. One of the world's oldest continuously cultivated grapevines, the Old Vine (Stara trta), grows on the wall of a house in Maribor's old town and is about 450 years old. The wine calendar here is more relevant than the civil clock for much of the agricultural sector.

## long summer evenings in wine country

Maribor sits at 46 degrees north, where daylight shifts substantially across the year. Summer evenings are long enough that outdoor dining continues past 10pm. Winter afternoons are dark by 4:30pm. The DST switch in spring and autumn is genuinely felt at this latitude.

[9am in Maribor is 8am in London](/when/9am-maribor/london) during European winter, the standard continental-to-British gap that reduces to zero during the brief period when clocks change on different dates.
