Gazipur is an industrial and satellite city directly north of [Dhaka](/dhaka), Bangladesh's capital. The two cities share Bangladesh Standard Time, UTC+6. No daylight saving. The clock stays fixed all year.

UTC+6 is an unusual offset: it puts Bangladesh 30 minutes ahead of India (UTC+5:30) and 30 minutes behind Myanmar (UTC+6:30). Bangladesh chose this offset at independence in 1971, and the half-hour neighbors on either side reflect each country's individual decision about where to draw the line.

## Factories and the factory clock

Gazipur is home to hundreds of garment factories, part of Bangladesh's massive ready-made garment industry. Shift schedules here are precise: workers arrive at 8am, factories run in two or three shifts, and production targets are tied to the clock in ways that pre-industrial Gazipur never experienced.

This industrial time discipline is relatively new. A generation ago, the area was farmland where agricultural rhythms, not factory bells, set the pace of the day.

## Sharing a clock with [Dhaka](/dhaka)

Both cities use Asia/Dhaka in the IANA database. When it is noon in Gazipur, it is 6am in [London](/london), noon in [Tashkent](/tashkent), and 1pm in [Bangkok](/bangkok).
