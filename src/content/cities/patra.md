Pátra sits at the entrance to the Gulf of Corinth, on the northwestern Peloponnese coast, directly facing the Ionian Islands. The city is Greece's main ferry port to Italy and Ancona, Brindisi, and Bari are reachable overnight, making Pátra a transit city as much as a destination. The late-night ferry departures and early-morning arrivals give the waterfront a particular energy.

[Greece](/country/greece) follows Eastern European Time, UTC+2 in winter (EET) and UTC+3 in summer (EEST), with EU standard transitions. Pátra shares this with [Athens](/athens) to the east. At 38.2 degrees north on the Mediterranean, the city has meaningful seasonal light variation: summer evenings stretch past 8:30pm, winter afternoons end before 5:30pm.

## carnival and cultural landmarks

Pátra's Carnival (Apokries) is the largest in Greece and one of Europe's oldest, running for several weeks before Orthodox Lent. The city's entire schedule reorganizes around Carnival: evening parades, the Grand Parade on the final Sunday, and the symbolic burning of the Carnival King at the port. The Carnival calendar follows the Orthodox Easter date rather than the Gregorian one.

The Rion-Antirion Bridge, which opened in 2004, connects the Peloponnese to mainland Greece across the Gulf of Corinth and is one of the longest cable-stayed bridges in the world. Pátra's geography, which once made it a place you had to pass through by ferry, now allows a direct crossing.
