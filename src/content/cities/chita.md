Chita is the capital of Zabaykalsky Krai in Russia's Far East, situated near the confluence of the Chita and Ingoda rivers in the Transbaikal region. It runs on Asia/Chita: UTC+9, year-round. Russia abolished seasonal clock changes in 2014.

Chita gives its name to the Asia/Chita IANA timezone identifier, which covers a swathe of Russia's Far Eastern territory. Cities including [Blagoveshchensk](/blagoveshchensk) and [Belogorsk](/belogorsk) share this zone.

## Decembrist exile capital

Chita has a particular place in Russian history as a city of exile. After the failed Decembrist Uprising against Tsar Nicholas I in 1825, many of the revolutionary intellectuals and officers were exiled to Siberia. Chita became one of their main places of confinement. Their wives famously followed them into exile, giving up noble privileges to live in Siberia. The Decembrists' presence introduced educated, reform-minded people to a remote region and left a cultural legacy.

The Decembrist House museum in Chita preserves artifacts and documents from this period.

## The Transsib and two clocks

Chita is a stop on the Trans-Siberian Railway. As with all Russian railway stations, the station displays two times: Moscow time (UTC+3) and local time (UTC+9). The six-hour difference requires attention from travelers: a train listed as departing at 14:00 on the board means 14:00 Moscow time, which is 20:00 in Chita.

At 52 degrees north, Chita has significant seasonal daylight variation. Summer evenings stretch past 9pm. December mornings are dark until after 9am.

The [time difference between Chita and London](/when/9am-chita/london) is nine hours in winter, eight in summer when the UK observes BST.
