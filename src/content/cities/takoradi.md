Takoradi is Ghana's main seaport, located in the Western Region on the Gulf of Guinea coast. It has grown in significance since offshore oil discoveries in the Jubilee Field in 2007 made it the center of Ghana's emerging petroleum industry. The city runs on Greenwich Mean Time (UTC+0) all year. [Ghana](/country/ghana) has not observed daylight saving time since 1942.

Ghana's decision to remain on GMT is geographically unusual: the country straddles the prime meridian, but its major cities fall just west of 0 degrees longitude. GMT is a reasonable fit for the coastal cities. The country's colonial period under British administration established GMT as the default, and it has remained since independence in 1957.

At roughly 5 degrees north, Takoradi has essentially no seasonal daylight variation. Sunrise and sunset times stay within about 30 minutes of 6am and 6pm year-round. The clock and the sun are in reliable alignment, which simplifies scheduling for a port city that coordinates shipping on UTC.

## port logistics and UTC coordination

The port handles cocoa exports (Ghana is the world's second-largest cocoa producer), timber, and petroleum-related logistics. Container ship arrivals and departures follow tide and berth schedules that are themselves coordinated against UTC, making the city's fixed GMT offset a practical convenience.

[Tamale](/tamale) to the north and [Accra](/accra) to the east share the same GMT offset.

[9am in Takoradi is 9am in London](/when/9am-takoradi/london) in winter. During British Summer Time, London advances and the gap opens to one hour.
