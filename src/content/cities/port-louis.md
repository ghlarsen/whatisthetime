Port Louis was made by the trade winds. The Dutch settled Mauritius in 1638 using it as a waypoint on the route to the East Indies. The French built the harbor into a serious port in the 18th century. The British took it in 1810. Each colonial power recognized the same thing: this natural harbor on the northwest coast of Mauritius, sheltered by a ring of mountains, was one of the finest anchorages in the Indian Ocean.

[Mauritius](/country/mauritius) uses Mauritius Time, UTC+4, year-round with no daylight saving. The island sits at about 20 degrees south latitude, roughly 900 kilometers east of Madagascar. Port Louis shares its offset with [Réunion](/saint-denis) to the west and is two hours behind India to the east.

The city's stock exchange, founded in 1989, was one of Africa's first, and its financial sector means that Port Louis is plugged into global trading schedules. The exchange operates on the same clock it always has, but global finance demands awareness of London, New York, and Singapore in the same working day.

The Aapravasi Ghat, a UNESCO World Heritage site in Port Louis, was the first immigration depot established by the British Empire after the abolition of slavery, where indentured laborers from India arrived to work the sugar plantations. Between 1834 and 1923, nearly half a million people passed through this gate. Every one of them arrived having crossed a vast ocean to a new timezone.
