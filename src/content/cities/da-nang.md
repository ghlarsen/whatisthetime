Da Nang sits at Vietnam's narrow central waist, flanked by the Hải Vân Pass to the north and the Marble Mountains to the south. Like all of [Vietnam](/vietnam), it runs on Indochina Time, UTC+7, with no daylight saving. The IANA identifier `Asia/Ho_Chi_Minh` covers the whole country despite Da Nang being in the center and Hanoi in the north.

Vietnam unified its timezone after reunification in 1975. The north had been using UTC+7 since 1954; the south briefly used UTC+8 in the 1960s under American influence. After reunification, the unified government chose UTC+7 and standardized the country. For Da Nang, which sits roughly at the geographic midpoint, UTC+7 is a fair solar approximation.

## A resort city between Asian time zones

At 16 degrees north, Da Nang has about 11 hours of daylight in winter and 13.5 hours in summer. The city became a major resort destination in the 2010s, and its beach hotels and resorts operate on schedules that cater to South Korean, Chinese, and Australian tourists arriving across a wide range of offsets. UTC+7 falls conveniently between those markets. An early-morning beach walk in Da Nang while South Korea is already at lunch and Australia is mid-evening makes the place feel central, even if that's not why the timezone was chosen.
