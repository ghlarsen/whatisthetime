The Marquesas Islands sit in the South Pacific at UTC-9:30, one of the rarest timezone offsets in the world. They are 30 minutes behind most of French Polynesia's other islands, which run on UTC-10. No daylight saving applies.

The half-hour offset has no grand historical explanation. It reflects the geographic position of the islands and a practical decision to set a local time that felt more aligned with solar noon than the full UTC-10 offset of the neighboring [Tahiti](/papeete)-centered Society Islands.

## artists who came and stayed

Herman Melville spent time in the Marquesas and described them in *Typee* (1846). Paul Gauguin spent the last years of his life on Hiva Oa, one of the Marquesas islands, dying there in 1903. Jacques Brel, the Belgian singer-songwriter, retired to the Marquesas in the 1970s and is buried on Hiva Oa beside Gauguin. The islands have an outsized presence in Western artistic imagination.

At 9 to 10 degrees south latitude, day length barely varies. The half-hour offset is more visible in scheduling than in daylight experience.

[9am in the Marquesas is 6:30pm in London](/when/9am-marquesas/london) during European winter, that unusual half-hour making the calculation briefly pause.
