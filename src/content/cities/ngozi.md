Ngozi sits in the northern highlands of Burundi at around 1,700 meters elevation, the highest of the country's major cities. The altitude gives it cooler temperatures than the tropical latitude suggests, with fresh mornings and afternoons that can become warm before cooling again. The northern hills of Burundi are some of the most densely populated rural areas in Africa.

[Burundi](/country/burundi) runs on Central Africa Time, UTC+2, year-round, no daylight saving. Ngozi shares this with [Bujumbura](/bujumbura) near Lake Tanganyika to the southwest. The IANA identifier is Africa/Bujumbura. At 2.9 degrees south of the equator, the city's daylight barely varies: roughly 11.5 to 12.5 hours across the year, with sunrise around 5:45am and sunset around 5:45pm year-round.

Ngozi is a provincial capital and administrative center. The weekly market is one of the largest in the north of the country, drawing trade from surrounding hills. The agricultural calendar, driven by two rainy seasons (February to May and September to November), shapes the rhythm more than any administrative schedule.

Coffee is a major crop in Burundi's highlands, and the harvest timing around Ngozi is economically critical. The processing cooperatives operate on the seasonal schedule of the coffee cherries more than any fiscal year calendar.
