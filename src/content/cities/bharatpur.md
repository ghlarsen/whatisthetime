Bharatpur sits in Nepal's Chitwan District, in the flat Terai lowlands near the Indian border. It runs on Nepal Standard Time: UTC+5:45. This 45-minute offset from UTC is unique in the world. No other country uses a 45-minute offset as its primary standard. Nepal set it deliberately to distinguish itself from India's UTC+5:30, a political and cultural statement of independence as much as a practical one.

Nepal does not observe daylight saving time. The UTC+5:45 offset applies year-round.

## The 15-minute difference from India

India's border is only a few kilometers south of Bharatpur. The city of Birgunj, a major border crossing, is directly connected to the Indian city of Raxaul. Traders, laborers, and families cross this border constantly. The 15-minute time difference between Nepal and India is one of the world's smallest timezone gaps across a political boundary. It creates genuine daily friction for people whose lives span both sides: phone alarms, meeting schedules, and business calls require constant mental adjustment.

Bharatpur itself has grown rapidly in recent decades as people migrate from Nepal's hill districts to the lower, warmer Terai. The city is now one of Nepal's metropolitan areas and a major commercial hub.

## Chitwan and tourism timing

Bharatpur is the gateway to Chitwan National Park, one of the world's premier wildlife conservation areas and home to endangered one-horned rhinoceros and Bengal tigers. The park operates on Nepali time, but the tourists who fill Bharatpur's lodges arrive from [Kathmandu](/kathmandu) (on the same clock) and from India (15 minutes behind). Early morning game drives depart around 6am local time, when dawn light in the subtropical forest reveals the animals most clearly.

The [time conversion from Bharatpur to London](/when/9am-bharatpur/london) is 5 hours 45 minutes ahead in winter, 4 hours 45 minutes in summer when the UK observes BST.
