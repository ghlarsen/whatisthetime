Isil'kul' is a small city in Omsk Oblast, in the western Siberian lowlands near the border with Kazakhstan. It runs on Omsk Time, UTC+6, year-round. Russia abolished daylight saving in 2014, and all Russian timezones have been fixed since.

UTC+6 puts Isil'kul' 3 hours ahead of [Moscow](/moscow) and in the same offset as [Dhaka](/dhaka), [Almaty](/almaty), and [Bishkek](/bishkek). When it is noon in Isil'kul', it is 9am in Moscow and 6am in [London](/london).

## Agricultural Siberia

The Omsk Oblast steppe is prime agricultural land. The region produces wheat, and Isil'kul' is a center of the surrounding agricultural economy. The agricultural calendar, dominated by the short Siberian growing season, structures the year far more than any civic schedule.

At 54.9 degrees North latitude, Isil'kul' has dramatic seasonal variation. Summers are short and intense, with very long days. Winters are brutal and dark. The UTC+6 offset means summers bring sunrise before 4am, far earlier than the clock-based workday begins.

## Railway junction

Like many Siberian towns, Isil'kul' exists partly because of the Trans-Siberian Railway. The railway created towns along its route, and train schedules (printed in Moscow time) connected these settlements to the wider Russian economy. The tension between local solar time and railway Moscow time is a constant of Siberian urban life.
