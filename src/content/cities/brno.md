Brno is the capital of the Moravia region and Czechia's second-largest city. It runs on Europe/Prague: Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter, UTC+2 in summer, following EU DST schedules. The rules are identical to Europe/Berlin and most of Central Europe.

Brno sits at about 49 degrees north, giving it noticeable seasonal daylight variation. Summer evenings extend past 9pm. December afternoons are dark by 4pm.

## Where genetics began

The Augustinian Monastery of St. Thomas in Brno is where Gregor Mendel spent most of his working life. Between 1856 and 1863, Mendel grew and observed 29,000 pea plants in the monastery garden, developing the laws of heredity that became the foundation of modern genetics. He published his findings in 1866, and they were ignored for 35 years until rediscovered simultaneously by three botanists in 1900.

The monastery garden still exists. The Mendel Museum occupies the site. The meticulous record-keeping Mendel used, counting peas through multiple generations across years, required careful temporal tracking. His notebooks are dated precisely, and the seasonal planting and harvest cycles of his experiments were as important as the counts themselves.

## A judicial city

Brno hosts the Czech Constitutional Court, the Supreme Court, and the Supreme Administrative Court. The city has become a center of legal and judicial life in a way that concentrates a certain kind of structured, formal time-consciousness: court schedules, filing deadlines, hearing calendars.

The [time conversion from Brno to London](/when/9am-brno/london) is one hour ahead in winter. [Prague](/prague) is on the same clock.
