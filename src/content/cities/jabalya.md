Jabālyā is a city and refugee camp in the northern Gaza Strip, one of the most densely populated areas on Earth. It runs on Palestine Standard Time, UTC+2 in winter and UTC+3 in summer, the same as [Gaza](/gaza) and the rest of the Palestinian territories. The IANA timezone identifier is Asia/Hebron.

Palestine's daylight saving transitions are announced by the Palestinian Authority rather than following a fixed date rule. The exact transition dates can differ from neighboring Israel's, creating brief periods where the two territories, though geographically adjacent, run on different clocks.

## Jabālyā camp and the displacement calendar

Jabālyā Refugee Camp, established in 1948, is the largest refugee camp in the Gaza Strip. Many residents are descendants of people displaced from what is now Israel during the 1948 Arab-Israeli War. The camp's population keeps track of time in multiple registers: civil time on the clock, the Islamic calendar for religious observances, and the personal calendar of displacement and return.

## Coordinating with humanitarian time

The Gaza Strip's dense population and ongoing crises have made it a center of international humanitarian operations. Aid organizations, medical teams, and coordination bodies operate on schedules synchronized with [Geneva](/geneve), [New York](/new-york), and regional headquarters. The civil clock at UTC+2/+3 governs all of this.
