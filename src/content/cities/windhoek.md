Windhoek sits at about 1,700 meters elevation in the Khomas Highland, which gives [Namibia](/country/namibia)'s capital a mild, dry climate despite being at 22 degrees south. The city averages over 300 sunny days per year, and the thin air and altitude give the light a particular clarity.

Namibia keeps West Africa Time, UTC+1 in standard time and UTC+2 in summer (DST runs from the first Sunday in September to the first Sunday in April, the southern hemisphere summer). The DST adoption is unusual for an African country; most of sub-Saharan Africa does not observe DST. Namibia's decision reflects practical alignment with South Africa, its dominant trading partner.

The German colonial history is still visible in Windhoek's architecture. The Christuskirche (1910), the Alt Feste fortress, and the Tintenpalast (parliament building) all carry German colonial design. The Herero and Nama genocide committed by German colonial forces between 1904 and 1908 is Namibia's foundational historical trauma and is now internationally recognized as genocide.

## colonial architecture and desert capital

The Namibian desert begins at the edge of the city. The Namib, one of the world's oldest deserts, stretches to the Atlantic coast. The distance between Windhoek and the sea is roughly 350 kilometers of largely uninhabited arid landscape.

[9am in Windhoek is 7am in London](/when/9am-windhoek/london). [Walvis Bay](/walvis-bay) on the coast keeps the same timezone.
