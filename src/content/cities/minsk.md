Minsk is the capital of [Belarus](/country/belarus), and it keeps a politically significant clock. Since 2011, Belarus has run on UTC+3 year-round, placing it in Moscow Time even though its geography would suggest UTC+2 (Eastern European Time) was more natural.

## a political clock change that stuck

In 2011, President Lukashenko moved the clocks forward for summer daylight saving as usual, then decided not to move them back in autumn. Belarus has never changed clocks again. The fixed UTC+3 aligns Minsk with Moscow rather than with Warsaw and Vilnius to the west, a choice that reflects the political orientation of the Lukashenko government toward Russia rather than the EU.

At 53 degrees north, Minsk experiences dramatic daylight variation. Summer evenings are very long, with sunset after 9pm in June. December afternoons go dark before 4pm. The absence of any DST adjustment means these extremes are simply absorbed into the civil schedule: late summer evenings, dark winter mornings.

[9am in Minsk is 6am in London](/when/9am-minsk/london) during European winter. The three-hour gap places Minsk in a different daylight context from its nearest EU neighbor, Vilnius, despite geographic proximity.
