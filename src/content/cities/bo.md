Bo is Sierra Leone's second-largest city, the capital of the Southern Province, and the country's interior commercial center. It runs on Africa/Freetown: UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. Sierra Leone does not observe daylight saving time. The West African country sits close enough to the prime meridian that UTC+0 is an accurate solar time: noon on the clock is close to solar noon.

At 7 degrees north latitude, Bo is near enough to the equator that daylight variation is minimal. Sunrise falls between 6am and 6:30am regardless of season. The light here is consistent, and the distinction between seasons is primarily about rainfall, not daylight.

## The second city

Bo grew significantly during the colonial period as an administrative and commercial center for the British Protectorate. It has the Bo School, one of Sierra Leone's most prominent secondary schools, founded in 1906. The city was historically important as a chieftaincy center: the surrounding region is organized around hereditary chiefs, and Bo was the point where traditional authority and colonial administration met.

During Sierra Leone's civil war (1991-2002), Bo was a relatively stable refuge compared to other parts of the country, taking in displaced people from more heavily affected areas. The city's post-war recovery has been steady. It is home to several educational institutions and a growing market economy.

## UTC+0 in context

Sierra Leone shares UTC+0 with its neighbor [Guinea](/conakry), with Liberia to the southeast, and with several West African coastal countries. [Freetown](/freetown), the capital, is on the same clock. The alignment simplifies regional commerce.

The [time difference between Bo and London](/when/9am-bo/london) is zero in winter, one hour behind when the UK observes BST.
