Saint-Laurent-du-Maroni is divided from Albina, Suriname, by the Maroni River. Pirogues, the long wooden dugout canoes, cross the river continuously, carrying people between two countries and two different time systems: French Guiana at UTC-3, Suriname also at UTC-3. They share the same clock, which makes the crossing simple, but the two sides of the river are otherwise worlds apart in language, currency, and governance.

[French Guiana](/country/french-guiana) is an overseas department of France: legally part of the EU, using the euro, governed from Paris, yet physically on the northern coast of South America. It uses French Guiana Time, UTC-3, year-round, with no daylight saving.

Saint-Laurent's history is dark. It served as the administrative center for the French penal colony that operated across French Guiana until 1953, including the notorious Devil's Island. The Camp de la Transportation, the main deportation facility, still stands in the city center as a museum. More than 70,000 prisoners were transported here over the colony's history.

The Maroni River is the highway into the interior for the Maroon communities of the rainforest. The Aluku, Ndyuka, and Saramaka peoples navigate the river in pirogues, reaching communities that have no road access. The river is time as much as it is a waterway.
