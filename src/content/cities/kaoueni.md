Kaouéni occupies the northwestern corner of Grande-Terre, Mayotte's main island, where the Indian Ocean sets the daily rhythm. The fishing boats go out early, before the equatorial sun climbs high, and the market day begins with the cool of early morning. Clocks here stay fixed at UTC+3 year-round.

## French department, East African timezone

Mayotte is geographically part of the Comoros archipelago but politically a French overseas department, which creates an interesting administrative split: the island uses the same timezone as [Nairobi](/nairobi) and [Kigali](/kigali) rather than Paris. No daylight saving is observed. The [Indian/Mayotte](/country/mayotte) identifier covers the whole island, including Kaouéni and the commune of [Koungou](/koungou) to the north.

At 12 degrees south latitude, seasonal daylight variation is mild. Days run roughly 11 to 13 hours across the year, enough that artificial light is rarely the organizing force it becomes in northern cities. The call to prayer, heard through Kaouéni's mosques (Islam is practiced by the majority of Mayotte's population), marks the day with more precision than the clock for many residents.

[9am in Kaouéni is 6am in London](/when/9am-kaoueni/london), meaning that by the time European offices open, Kaouéni has already been moving for hours.
