Ölgii is Mongolia's westernmost provincial capital, sitting at 1,710 meters in the Altai mountains near where Mongolia, Russia, and China come close together. The city is the capital of Bayan-Ölgii province, which is over 80% Kazakh, making it the heart of Mongolia's Kazakh community, a people who arrived from Kazakhstan in the early 20th century and maintained their language, culture, and eagle-hunting traditions in these remote mountains.

Mongolia's western provinces use IANA Asia/Hovd, UTC+7, year-round. The rest of Mongolia is on UTC+8. Ölgii's western timezone offset reflects its position at the far edge of the country. No daylight saving applies.

At 48.8 degrees north at high altitude, Ölgii has an extreme continental climate. Winters are severe: temperatures can drop below -40 degrees, and snow covers the ground from October through April. Summers are short and intense. The Altai light in summer, at nearly 16 hours of daylight, is brilliant and sharp at this altitude.

The Golden Eagle Festival, held each October in the nearby countryside, has become internationally known. Eagle hunters demonstrate the ancient Kazakh practice of hunting with trained golden eagles, timed to the autumn hunting season before winter closes the mountains.
