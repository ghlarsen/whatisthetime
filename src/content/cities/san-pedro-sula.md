San Pedro Sula occupies the Sula Valley in northwestern [Honduras](/country/honduras), ringed by mountains, with the Merendon range to the west. It is the country's industrial and commercial capital, with more manufacturing activity than the political capital Tegucigalpa. The city runs on Central Standard Time (UTC-6) all year. Honduras has not changed its clocks since discontinuing daylight saving time in 1987.

At 15 degrees north, the city sees minimal variation in daylight hours across the year. The sun rises between roughly 5:30am and 6:15am depending on the season, and sets between 5:30pm and 6:30pm. This consistency means the city's working day has a reliable shape throughout the year.

## factory shifts and industrial time

The city's manufacturing base runs on shift work, which means the concept of "day" and "night" is practical rather than solar. The maquiladora factories that produce textiles for export operate around the clock on rotating shifts, and the clock those workers follow is the same fixed UTC-6 offset.

San Pedro Sula and [San Salvador](/san-salvador) in neighboring El Salvador share the same Central Standard offset.

[9am in San Pedro Sula is 3pm in London](/when/9am-san-pedro-sula/london) during summer, 4pm when European clocks fall back.
