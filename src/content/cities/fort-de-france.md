Fort-de-France, the capital of Martinique, runs on UTC-4 year-round. Martinique is a French overseas territory and uses `America/Martinique`: Atlantic Standard Time, UTC-4, with no daylight saving. France's metropolitan territory is UTC+1/+2, but French Caribbean islands use UTC-4, reflecting their geographic position.

At 14.6 degrees north, Martinique's daylight varies only modestly across the year, making DST unnecessary. The year-round UTC-4 also keeps Martinique in reasonable alignment with neighboring Caribbean islands. [Barbados](/barbados), [Saint Lucia](/saint-lucia), and several other Eastern Caribbean islands also use UTC-4 permanently, creating a consistent timezone across the Lesser Antilles.

Fort-de-France is built around its harbor, and the daily rhythm of the city follows the harbor's trade: morning cargo, afternoon ferries to neighboring islands, evening restaurant and café life around the Savane. The four-hour gap from Paris means morning meetings with French metropolitan authorities come at inconvenient times. Many Martinicans working with France learn to schedule their calls in the late morning Martinique time, when Paris's afternoon is just beginning.
