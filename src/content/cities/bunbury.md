Bunbury is Western Australia's second-largest city, a port town about 175 kilometers south of Perth. It runs on Australia/Perth: UTC+8, year-round. Western Australia does not observe daylight saving time.

Western Australia held four DST referendums: in 1975, 1984, 1992, and 2009. A three-year trial ran from 2006 to 2009. The 2009 referendum rejected DST with 55% of votes. Western Australians in Bunbury and across the state consistently vote to keep the fixed UTC+8 year-round.

## The east-west clock gap

During summer, when eastern states observe Daylight Saving Time (UTC+11 for the eastern mainland), Perth and Bunbury are on UTC+8, creating a three-hour difference. In winter, when the eastern states are on standard time (UTC+10), the gap narrows to two hours. Businesses operating nationally from Bunbury must navigate this fluctuating gap.

Western Australia's arguments against DST center on the practical experience of summer: the state already gets natural sunrise before 5am in midsummer without DST, making earlier mornings impractical. The long summer days are a feature, not something to manipulate. Farmers in the state's grain belt, which extends inland from Bunbury, consistently vote against DST.

## A natural harbor

Bunbury's port handles agricultural exports, particularly from the southwest's timber and farming regions. The city sits on a low peninsula with Koombana Bay to the east, and dolphins regularly enter the bay, making dolphin-watching a significant tourism draw. The harbor's shipping schedule runs on international maritime UTC, while locals keep Australian Western Standard Time.

The [time conversion from Bunbury to London](/when/9am-bunbury/london) puts London eight hours behind. [Brisbane](/brisbane) in Queensland also refuses DST but at UTC+10.
