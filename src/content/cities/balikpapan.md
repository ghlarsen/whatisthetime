Balikpapan is the main city of East Kalimantan on the island of Borneo, an oil and logistics hub that has been at the center of Indonesian petroleum production for over a century. The city also gained attention as the site identified for Indonesia's new capital, Nusantara, which is being built about 80 kilometers to the north.

The clock is Central Indonesia Time, UTC+8. The IANA identifier is Asia/Makassar. Indonesia has three timezones, and Kalimantan's eastern provinces use this middle zone, one hour behind the eastern zone (Java, Bali) and one hour ahead of the western zone.

Balikpapan's oil industry keeps the city running 24 hours. Refinery operations, port logistics, and the supporting services economy don't follow a strict 9-to-5 pattern. But the city's formal business day aligns with UTC+8, which provides reasonable overlap with Singapore (UTC+8), the regional financial hub.

The development of Nusantara nearby has transformed Balikpapan from a regional oil city into a major construction logistics node. Materials, workers, and equipment flow through its port. The scale of the capital relocation project, moving Indonesia's administrative capital from Java to Borneo, is one of the world's most ambitious urban developments.

[Bandung](/bandung) in West Java uses UTC+7, one hour behind Balikpapan. [9am in Balikpapan is 1am in London](/when/9am-balikpapan/london) during European winter.
