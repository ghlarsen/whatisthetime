Savannakhet sits on the Mekong River in central [Laos](/country/laos), directly across from Mukdahan in Thailand. The Friendship Bridge connecting the two sides carries one of the most active trade flows on the Mekong, with goods moving between Laos, Thailand, and Vietnam on a daily basis. Both sides of the river keep the same time: Indochina Time (UTC+7), used across mainland Southeast Asia.

Thailand and Laos sharing UTC+7 makes the border crossing logistically straightforward. There is no timezone adjustment when the trucks cross. The economic rhythm of the crossing is instead shaped by border opening hours, customs schedules, and transport union agreements rather than clock differences.

At 16 degrees north, Savannakhet gets about 13 hours of daylight in the hot season and about 11 in the cool season. The Mekong's level changes dramatically between the dry and wet seasons, and the river itself tells the city what time of year it is more clearly than any calendar.

## French colonial streets and the Mekong crossing

The city retains a French colonial architectural character in its historic center, a reminder of its role as an administrative outpost during the Indochina period. The French-era streets are mostly quiet now, the old villas slowly weathering.

[Vientiane](/vientiane) to the north shares the same Indochina Time offset.

[9am in Savannakhet is 2am in London](/when/9am-savannakhet/london) in winter, 3am during British Summer Time.
