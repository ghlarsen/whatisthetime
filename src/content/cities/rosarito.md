Rosarito sits 30 kilometers south of the US-Mexico border on the Pacific coast of Baja California. For decades it was the weekend destination for San Diego and Los Angeles residents: close, cheap, and on the beach. The Fox Studios Baja facility, built here for the filming of Titanic in 1996, made it briefly famous globally.

Baja California is the one Mexican state that follows Pacific Time, matching California across the border. UTC-8 in winter, UTC-7 in summer. Rosarito uses the same clock as [Tijuana](/tijuana) to the north and as [San Diego](/san-diego) across the border. The timezone alignment is commercial logic: Baja California's economy is deeply connected to Southern California.

[Mexico](/country/mexico)'s other border states use Central Time, but the Baja California peninsula's longitude and its California connections make Pacific Time the sensible choice. The state has consistently voted to maintain DST observance, unlike most of Mexico, because keeping the same clock as California matters for business and daily crossings.

The Pacific at Rosarito is colder than the Gulf of Mexico or the Caribbean, fed by the California Current bringing cold water from the north. The coast is dramatic: the road south toward Ensenada runs along bluffs above the ocean, with the waves visible far below.

[9am in Rosarito is 5pm in London](/when/9am-rosarito/london) in winter.
