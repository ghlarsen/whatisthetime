Owendo is the port and industrial zone of greater Libreville, sitting on the Gabon Estuary just south of the capital. The manganese and timber exports that form the backbone of Gabon's non-oil economy pass through Owendo's port facilities. The SETRAG railway terminus, handling the Transgabonais line that brings manganese ore from Moanda in the interior, is here. Industrial time, shift time, and the rhythms of bulk commodity export define this part of the city.

[Gabon](/country/gabon) uses West Africa Time, UTC+1, year-round with no daylight saving. Owendo shares this with [Libreville](/libreville) immediately to the north. At 0.3 degrees north of the equator, the daylight is essentially constant: sunrise near 6am, sunset near 6pm, every day of the year with minimal variation.

The equatorial position means that the concept of seasons is replaced by the wet and dry season cycle. The main dry season from June to September and the main wet season from October to December govern what can be done outdoors. Humidity is consistently high regardless of season.

## declining oil and the manganese port

Gabon's oil wealth, which funded much of the country's development since the 1970s, is declining as reserves deplete. The manganese exports from Owendo port take on increasing importance as Gabon manages this transition.
