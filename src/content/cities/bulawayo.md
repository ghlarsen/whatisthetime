Bulawayo is Zimbabwe's second-largest city and the historical heart of Matabeleland, the region that was the center of the Ndebele Kingdom before colonization. It runs on Africa/Harare: UTC+2, Central Africa Time, year-round. No daylight saving time is observed.

The city sits at 1,340 meters elevation on Zimbabwe's Highveld plateau. The altitude gives it a temperate climate despite its position at 20 degrees south latitude. Sunrise falls between 5:30am and 6:30am depending on season. Winters are cool and dry; summers warm and wet.

## Seat of the Ndebele Kingdom

Bulawayo, whose name means "the place of slaughter" in Ndebele, was the royal kraal of the Ndebele kings. King Lobengula made it his capital in the 1870s and 1880s. The city's pre-colonial significance gives it a different historical identity from Harare, the post-colonial administrative capital to the north.

British South Africa Company forces under Cecil Rhodes occupied the city in 1893 after the First Matabele War. The colonial city was built on and around the site of Lobengula's destroyed kraal. The railway reached Bulawayo in 1897, making it an early terminus of the Cape to Cairo railway ambition.

## Railway city

Bulawayo's identity is shaped by the railway more than almost any other African city. The National Railways of Zimbabwe has its headquarters here. The city was built as a railway junction, and the grid of wide streets (reportedly designed wide enough for a full ox wagon span to turn) reflects its origin as a transport hub.

The [time difference between Bulawayo and London](/when/9am-bulawayo/london) is two hours in winter, one in summer when the UK observes BST. [Harare](/harare) and [Chitungwiza](/chitungwiza) are on the same clock.
