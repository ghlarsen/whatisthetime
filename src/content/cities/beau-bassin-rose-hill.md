Beau Bassin-Rose Hill is a twin-city municipality in the central-western plateau of Mauritius, effectively the island's urban commercial center. It houses government offices, shopping districts, hospitals, and the National Assembly of Mauritius. Despite Port Louis technically being the capital, much of the island's daily business and civic life happens here.

The clock is Mauritius Time, UTC+4. The IANA identifier is Indian/Mauritius. No daylight saving.

Mauritius sits in the Indian Ocean at roughly 20 degrees south latitude. The island tried daylight saving briefly in 1982 and 1983 but abandoned it. UTC+4 places Mauritius four hours ahead of London in winter, three hours ahead in European summer. It aligns with [Baku](/baku) in Azerbaijan and the UAE, though these are very different geographic and economic worlds.

Mauritius's economy is built on financial services, tourism, and textiles. The financial sector coordinates globally, with particular connections to Africa (Mauritius is a major investment hub for African-bound capital), India (a significant source of trade and tourism), and Europe. UTC+4 allows morning overlap with Europe and afternoon overlap with the Asian financial centers.

The island's sugar industry, which defined its economy for 200 years, still operates in the surrounding agricultural plateau. Harvest season (June to December) marks a distinct agricultural rhythm alongside the tourism-driven economy.
