Chattogram, formerly known as Chittagong, is Bangladesh's main port city and its second-largest urban area. It runs on Asia/Dhaka: UTC+6, Bangladesh Standard Time, year-round. Bangladesh does not observe daylight saving time.

At 22 degrees north, Chattogram has moderate tropical daylight variation. June sees sunrise around 5:15am and sunset around 6:30pm. December brings sunrise around 6:30am and sunset around 5:20pm. The fixed UTC+6 keeps solar noon close to 12:15pm year-round.

## Asia's busiest garment port

Chattogram handles roughly 90% of Bangladesh's international trade. The garment industry, which makes Bangladesh one of the world's largest clothing exporters, depends on Chattogram to move billions of dollars of finished goods to markets in the EU, the US, and beyond. The port's operating schedule connects to the global supply chain in real time, with container ships running on UTC-based schedules while dock workers and customs officials operate on Bangladesh Standard Time.

The ship-breaking industry at Sitakunda, just north of Chattogram, is one of the world's largest. Massive ocean-going vessels are dismantled on the beaches here by hand. The tidal schedule determines when ships are beached and when work is possible.

## The Karnaphuli River

Chattogram sits at the mouth of the Karnaphuli River. The river's tidal flows govern shipping movements in and out of the port. Tide tables, not clocks, determine the optimal window for large vessels to navigate the channel. The harbor pilots who guide ships in and out carry tide schedules as essential tools.

The [time conversion from Chattogram to London](/when/9am-chattogram/london) puts London six hours behind in winter, seven when the UK observes BST. [Dhaka](/dhaka) is on the same clock.
