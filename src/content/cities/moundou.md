Moundou sits near the southern tip of Chad, close to the border with Cameroon and the Central African Republic. At 8 degrees north latitude, it barely registers the seasonal daylight variation that defines life in higher latitudes. Sunrise stays between 5:30am and 6:30am year-round. The clock barely matters for light, but the seasons matter enormously for water.

[Chad](/country/chad) runs on West Africa Time, UTC+1, year-round, no daylight saving. Moundou shares that clock with [N'Djamena](/n-djamena) to the north, though the two cities feel worlds apart. Where the capital is arid and Saharan-influenced, Moundou sits in the wetter south, its economy historically built on cotton processing and the Cotontchad textile industry.

The Logone River, which defines life and agriculture in this region, dictates a seasonal rhythm more powerful than any clock. The rainy season swells it from June through September, flooding fields and reshaping roads. The dry season contracts it. Farmers here track time not primarily by date but by what the river is doing.

[9am in Moundou is 8am in London](/when/9am-moundou/london), a one-hour gap that makes Moundou one of the easier African cities to connect with European business hours.
