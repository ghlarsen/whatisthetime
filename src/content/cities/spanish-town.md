Spanish Town is the capital of the parish of Saint Catherine and was the colonial capital of [Jamaica](/country/jamaica) from the Spanish period through most of British rule, losing that status to Kingston only in 1872. The Cathedral of Saint Jago de la Vega, completed in the seventeenth century, is one of the oldest cathedrals in the western hemisphere still in use.

Jamaica runs on Eastern Standard Time (UTC-5) all year, with no daylight saving. The country sits at about 18 degrees north, where daylight variation is modest: roughly 13 hours in June, about 11 hours in December. The fixed offset has been stable for decades and keeps Jamaica's scheduling consistent with the eastern Caribbean.

## syncing with New York by accident

The fixed UTC-5 creates an interesting seasonal relationship with the US East Coast. When the US observes Eastern Daylight Time (UTC-4), Jamaica and New York share the same clock. When the US falls back to Eastern Standard Time (UTC-5) in November, Jamaica suddenly shares the same time as New York again, having not moved itself. Jamaica effectively "syncs" with New York twice a year without doing anything.

[9am in Spanish Town is 2pm in London](/when/9am-spanish-town/london) in winter, 3pm during British Summer Time.
