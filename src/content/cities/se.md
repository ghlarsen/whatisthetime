Sé is one of the original historic parishes of [Macao](/country/macao), the former Portuguese territory on the Pearl River Delta now administered as a Special Administrative Region of China. The parish takes its name from the Sé Cathedral, Macao's oldest Christian church. The narrow lanes of Sé carry some of the territory's densest concentration of colonial-era architecture, UNESCO-listed and heavily photographed.

Macao runs on China Standard Time (UTC+8) all year, no daylight saving. Despite its Portuguese heritage and distinct legal system, the territory aligns its clock with mainland China and [Hong Kong](/hong-kong) across the water. The practical logic is clear: Macao's economy is deeply integrated with the Pearl River Delta region, and a different timezone would create unnecessary friction for the millions of day-trippers arriving by ferry from Hong Kong and Guangzhou.

## casinos and the historic parish pace

Macao's identity as a major gaming destination means its commercial rhythm does not follow the standard day-night pattern. The casinos operate continuously, the gaming floors indifferent to sunrise and sunset. The historic Sé parish, with its churches and colonial squares, is part of a different Macao: quieter, more historically layered, visible on the same UTC+8 clock but running at a different pace.

[Taipa](/taipa) across the inner harbor shares the same China Standard Time.

[9am in Sé is 1am in London](/when/9am-se/london) in winter, 2am during British Summer Time.
