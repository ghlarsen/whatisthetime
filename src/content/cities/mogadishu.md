Mogadishu is the capital of [Somalia](/country/somalia) and one of Africa's most ancient port cities, a trading hub on the Indian Ocean that has existed for over a millennium. It runs on East Africa Time, UTC+3, year-round with no daylight saving.

## civil war, collapse, and recovery

Somalia's civil war, which began in 1991 with the collapse of the central government, lasted for decades and fundamentally disrupted civil infrastructure including the institutional functions that timekeeping supports: business, transport, courts, schools. The city's relationship with formal scheduled time was fractured. Recovery since the mid-2010s has gradually restored these structures, and Mogadishu is now a functioning city with growing commercial activity, though security challenges remain.

At 2 degrees north latitude, Mogadishu is essentially equatorial. Day length barely changes across the year. Sunrise and sunset fall predictably at about 6am and 6pm. The monsoon seasons, not daylight variation, are the primary temporal organizers.

[9am in Mogadishu is 6am in London](/when/9am-mogadishu/london) during European winter.
