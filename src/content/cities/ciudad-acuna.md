Ciudad Acuña is a Mexican city in the state of Coahuila, sitting on the Rio Grande directly across from Del Rio, Texas. It runs on America/Matamoros: UTC-6 in winter (Central Standard Time), UTC-5 in summer (Central Daylight Time), following the US Central Time DST schedule.

This is notable because most of Mexico's interior uses Central Time but historically with different DST dates than the United States. The Matamoros timezone zone, which covers northeast Mexican border cities, was aligned with US Central Time to facilitate cross-border commerce. Del Rio, Texas operates on the same schedule. When the US springs forward in March, Ciudad Acuña springs forward too.

## The border crossing

The Del Rio-Ciudad Acuña International Bridge connects the two cities. It is an active commercial and pedestrian crossing. The fact that both sides use the same UTC offset (and the same DST schedule) means that business meetings, appointments, and phone calls across the bridge require no timezone adjustment. Both sides run on the same clock.

This border alignment is not universal along the US-Mexico frontier. Some Mexican border cities use different timezone rules from their US counterparts, creating complications. Ciudad Acuña's alignment with Del Rio is an explicit choice to reduce friction.

## Coahuila's economy

Coahuila is one of Mexico's major industrial states, with steel production, automobile manufacturing, and maquiladora assembly plants. Ciudad Acuña has an industrial sector and a significant maquiladora presence. The cross-border economic relationship with Texas shapes the city's commercial rhythm.
