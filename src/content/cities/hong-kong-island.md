Hong Kong Island is the original territory ceded to Britain in 1842 under the Treaty of Nanking. It runs on Hong Kong Time, UTC+8, year-round, the same as [Hong Kong](/hong-kong) and all of the SAR. No daylight saving since 1979.

The island holds the Central business district, the government headquarters, and some of Hong Kong's oldest and most densely developed neighborhoods. Victoria Peak overlooks the harbor, the skyline of Kowloon across the water, and on clear days, the New Territories beyond.

## The colonial clock and the harbour schedule

The daily schedule in colonial Hong Kong was tied to the harbour. Ships arrived and departed on tidal and commercial schedules. The noon gun at Causeway Bay, fired daily since the 1840s (reportedly as a punishment, then adopted as a tradition), became a time signal for ships in the harbour. The ceremony continues today, fired every day at noon by the Jardine Matheson Group.

This is one of the world's oldest continuously running public time signals.

## The vertical city's daily rhythm

Hong Kong Island's vertical density means that millions of people live within a few hundred meters of sea level, climb the escalators of the Mid-Levels, and move through the city on a compressed daily schedule. The morning rush on the MTR begins before 7am. Business lunches end by 2pm. Evening traffic peaks around 7pm.
