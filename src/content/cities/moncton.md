Moncton is New Brunswick's largest city and a bilingual hub where French Acadian culture meets English-speaking Canada. It runs on Atlantic Time, UTC-4 in winter and UTC-3 during summer daylight saving, the same as [Halifax](/halifax) in Nova Scotia and the Maritime provinces.

The city sits at the head of Petitcodiac River tidal bore, a natural phenomenon where incoming Bay of Fundy tides create a visible wave that travels upstream twice daily. The Bay of Fundy has the world's highest tidal range, up to 16 meters, and Moncton's identity is shaped partly by these extreme tidal rhythms, which operate on a lunar calendar rather than a civil one.

Atlantic Time puts Moncton one hour ahead of Eastern Time, which can create a mental gap for travelers from central Canada or the US. The one-hour advance means Moncton business days overlap well with early morning in London: a 9am Moncton meeting is already 1pm in the UK.

[Halifax](/halifax) shares this timezone. [9am in Moncton is 1pm in London](/when/9am-moncton/london) during European winter.
