Ljubljana is one of Europe's smallest capital cities, a compact and walkable place where the old town cascades down from a medieval castle and the Ljubljanica River runs through the center. It runs on Europe/Ljubljana time: UTC+1 in winter (CET), UTC+2 in summer (CEST).

At 46 degrees north, Ljubljana has meaningful seasonal light difference. Summer evenings during CEST extend past 9pm, and the outdoor café culture along the riverbanks takes full advantage.

## riverside cafes and the Ljubljanica at dusk The pedestrianized old town fills up after 7pm in summer and doesn't thin out until well after midnight. Winter afternoons dim before 4pm, and the city shifts to an indoor, candlelit character.

Slovenia gained independence from Yugoslavia in 1991, in the brief Ten-Day War. After that war, Ljubljana became the capital of an independent state for the first time in its modern history. The city has since organized itself around EU membership (since 2004) and a strong regional identity.

Central European Time is the natural choice for Ljubljana, bordering Italy to the west and Croatia and Austria on other sides, all on the same timezone.

[Kranj](/kranj) to the northwest keeps the same clock. [9am in Ljubljana is 8am in London](/when/9am-ljubljana/london) in winter, one hour ahead of GMT.
