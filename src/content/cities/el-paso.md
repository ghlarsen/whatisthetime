El Paso is Texas's westernmost major city, and it's the only significant Texas city on Mountain Time rather than Central Time. The IANA identifier `America/Denver` covers El Paso. While [Dallas](/dallas) and Houston run on Central Time, El Paso sits 750 miles west at a longitude that naturally falls in the Mountain timezone.

## a city more connected to New Mexico than Texas

The practical reason for Mountain Time in El Paso is its connection to [Ciudad Juárez](/ciudad-juarez), directly across the Rio Grande in Mexico, and to Albuquerque and Santa Fe in New Mexico. El Paso's economic and social connections run west toward New Mexico and the Mountain West, not east toward the rest of Texas. The two-hour time difference between El Paso and Houston is an accurate reflection of how disconnected the two cities are by culture, economy, and geography.

The El Paso-Ciudad Juárez border crossing is among the busiest in the world, and since 2022, both sides of the border keep the same clock (Ciudad Juárez switched to US Mountain Time rules to match El Paso). The alignment was a direct response to the friction caused when the clocks were mismatched for parts of the year.
