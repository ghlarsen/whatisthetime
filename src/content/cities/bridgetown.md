Bridgetown is the capital of Barbados, a small island nation in the southeastern Caribbean. It runs on America/Barbados: UTC-4, Atlantic Standard Time, year-round. Barbados has never observed daylight saving time in the modern era.

At 13 degrees north latitude, Barbados has predictable, consistent daylight. Sunrise falls between 5:30am and 6:30am through the year. Sunset arrives between 5:45pm and 6:45pm. The fixed UTC-4 clock aligns well with this natural cycle. There is no long summer evening to extend and no dark winter to compensate for.

## A British colonial port

Bridgetown was established by English settlers in 1628 and became one of the British Empire's most valuable Caribbean possessions. The sugar economy it drove made Barbados extraordinarily profitable for Britain and devastatingly brutal for the enslaved African people who worked the plantations. The Bridgetown waterfront, the Careenage, was the harbor where sugar was loaded onto ships bound for Britain.

The historic district of Bridgetown and its Garrison were inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2011. The garrison's time-related artifact, the cannon that was fired as a time signal for ships in the harbor, is part of the city's institutional relationship with accurate timekeeping. Ships needed to synchronize their chronometers, and harbor cannons were one method of broadcasting the noon signal.

## Caribbean timezone alignment

Barbados shares UTC-4 with several other Eastern Caribbean islands that do not observe DST, including [Brades](/brades) in Montserrat and [Castries](/castries) in Saint Lucia. The consistency simplifies regional transport and communication.

The [time conversion from Bridgetown to London](/when/9am-bridgetown/london) is four hours behind in winter, five hours behind in summer when the UK observes BST.
