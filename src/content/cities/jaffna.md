Jaffna is the largest city in northern Sri Lanka and the cultural capital of the Sri Lankan Tamil community. It runs on Sri Lanka Standard Time, UTC+5:30, year-round, the same as [Colombo](/colombo) and all of Sri Lanka. No daylight saving.

Sri Lanka shares the UTC+5:30 offset with India, though the two countries use different IANA identifiers: Asia/Colombo for Sri Lanka, Asia/Kolkata for India. Sri Lanka briefly used UTC+6 from 1996 to 2006 before returning to UTC+5:30 to realign with India and reduce the disruption from the half-hour difference.

## Jaffna and the Tamil calendar

The Tamil community uses the Tamil calendar alongside the Gregorian calendar. Tamil New Year, Puthandu, falls on April 14 or 15, aligned with the solar entry into Aries. This astronomical event, marking the sun's position rather than any civil calculation, is one of the most important dates in the Tamil year.

The Tamil calendar months are also used for festival scheduling, agricultural cycles, and auspicious dates for ceremonies. This solar time system runs in parallel with UTC+5:30.

## A city rebuilding after the civil war

Jaffna was heavily affected by the Sri Lankan Civil War, which ended in 2009. The city had been under rebel control for extended periods and suffered significant damage. Reconstruction has been substantial, and the city's temples, colleges, and markets are again centers of activity.
