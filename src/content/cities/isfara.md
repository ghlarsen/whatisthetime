Isfara is a city in northeastern Tajikistan, in the Fergana Valley near borders with Kyrgyzstan and Uzbekistan. It runs on Tajikistan Time, UTC+5, year-round. Tajikistan abolished daylight saving in 1991 at independence.

The Fergana Valley is one of Central Asia's most complex border regions. Isfara itself is close to an Uzbek exclave, Sokh. Cross-border movement in this region requires navigating not just political boundaries but timezone differences: Uzbekistan uses UTC+5 like Tajikistan, but Kyrgyzstan uses UTC+6, so moving between these neighbors can mean a clock change.

## The enclave problem

The Fergana Valley's Soviet-era borders created a patchwork of enclaves and exclaves that made daily life complicated after the republics became independent states. Farmers whose fields were in one country might live in another. Market towns that served multiple communities suddenly sat behind borders. Timezone differences added another layer of complexity to an already tangled geography.

## Silk Road history

Isfara sits along ancient Silk Road routes. The region was part of the Sogdian trade network that moved goods between China and the Mediterranean world. Central Asian merchants carried not just goods but calendar systems, currency, and time standards across vast distances.
