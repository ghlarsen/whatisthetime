Monrovia is the capital of [Liberia](/country/liberia) and one of the very few African capitals to run on GMT, UTC+0, year-round with no daylight saving. This alignment is relatively recent and historically interesting.

Until 1972, Liberia kept its own offset: UTC-0:44:30, a non-standard 44.5-minute offset derived from the mean solar time of Monrovia. This was one of the last sub-hour timezone offsets in the world, a relic of the era before international standardization. When Liberia joined the West African timezone community in 1972, it moved to GMT, which aligned it with Sierra Leone and Guinea to the north.

## American origins and a local solar time

Liberia was founded in 1822 by the American Colonization Society as a settlement for freed American slaves. Its founders brought American institutional patterns, including a constitution modeled on the US one, but the timezone they kept was based on local solar time rather than any American reference meridian.

At 6 degrees north, Monrovia has minimal daylight variation. The rainy season and dry season are the primary organizers of the year.

[9am in Monrovia is 9am in London](/when/9am-monrovia/london) year-round, one of the simplest conversions in West Africa.
