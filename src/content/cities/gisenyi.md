Gisenyi sits on the northern shore of Lake Kivu at the border with the Democratic Republic of Congo. It runs on Central Africa Time, UTC+2, the same as [Kigali](/kigali) and the same as [Cairo](/cairo). No daylight saving in Rwanda.

Directly across the border is Goma, DRC, which uses the same UTC+2 offset. The border crossing at Gisenyi-Goma is one of the busiest in Central Africa. Crossing it does not change the time.

## A border city's dual rhythm

Gisenyi is simultaneously a Rwandan resort town on Lake Kivu and a trading hub at one of Central Africa's most active borders. Tourism from Kigali brings visitors to the lake beaches on weekends. Cross-border trade runs on weekdays. The city operates two schedules in parallel, shaped by which economy it is serving at any moment.

The lakeside position moderates temperatures. Gisenyi sits at about 1,500 meters elevation. Nights are cool even near the equator. Mornings start early: markets open before 7am, boats move on the lake at dawn.

## Equatorial time at 1 degree South

At 1.7 degrees South latitude, day length barely changes across the year. Sunrise is around 6am and sunset around 6pm virtually every day of the year. UTC+2 puts solar noon at about 12:15pm, a near-perfect alignment.
