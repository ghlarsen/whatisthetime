Malabo is the capital of Equatorial Guinea and one of Africa's smallest capital cities. It sits on Bioko Island in the Gulf of Guinea, a volcanic island roughly 40 kilometers from the Cameroonian coast. The city runs on West Africa Time, UTC+1, with no daylight saving.

Equatorial Guinea is the only Spanish-speaking country in Africa, a legacy of Spanish colonization from 1778 until independence in 1968. The oil boom starting in the 1990s transformed one of Africa's poorest nations into one with among the highest GDP per capita on the continent, though wealth distribution remains extremely unequal.

## island geography and daylight

Bioko Island sits close to 3 degrees north latitude, meaning day length barely varies. The name "equatorial" in the country's name refers to its equatorial location, and the climate delivers on it: warm, humid, and stable year-round. No time change is needed because the sun provides essentially no seasonal signal.

[9am in Malabo is 8am in London](/when/9am-malabo/london) during European winter. The one-hour offset makes coordination with Madrid, Malabo's primary European connection, straightforward.
