Bengaluru, formerly Bangalore, is India's technology capital and home to the largest concentration of IT and software companies in South Asia. It runs on India Standard Time: UTC+5:30. The half-hour offset is one of the most distinctive features of Indian timekeeping. No other major economy uses a 30-minute offset as its primary standard for an entire country.

India has not observed daylight saving time since 1945. The subcontinent spans about 30 degrees of longitude, which would be enough to justify multiple timezones in most countries. India uses one. The result is that sunrise in the west (Gujarat, Rajasthan) and sunrise in the northeast (Assam, Arunachal Pradesh) can differ by nearly two hours, but both show the same time on the clock.

## Why Bengaluru became the tech city

The city's elevation, around 900 meters above sea level, gives it a climate cooler than most of peninsular India. When American and European tech companies set up Indian operations in the 1990s, this climate was one factor. Another was the concentration of engineering talent from institutions like the Indian Institute of Science, established here in 1909.

The UTC+5:30 offset turns out to be strategically useful for IT outsourcing. Bengaluru's business day has a few overlap hours with late-night operations in the United States and aligns with European morning shifts when Indian workers start early. The "follow the sun" model of software development, where teams in different timezones hand work off as their days end, was partly built around this offset.

## The clock in practice

Bengaluru's tech culture has borrowed some of the 24-hour scheduling norms of the global software industry. Call centers and development teams work night shifts calibrated to US time. But the city's traditional culture, its markets, temples, and old neighborhoods, keeps its own rhythm: early mornings, active evenings, and a respect for the slower pace of the Karnataka heartland surrounding the city.

The [time conversion from Bengaluru to London](/when/9am-bengaluru/london) puts London 4.5 hours behind in winter, 3.5 hours in summer when the UK observes BST. [Kolkata](/kolkata) is on the same clock but 2,100 kilometers east.
