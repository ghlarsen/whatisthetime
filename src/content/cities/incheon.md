Incheon is South Korea's third-largest city and the site of Incheon International Airport, consistently rated among the world's best airports and one of its busiest international hubs. It runs on Korea Standard Time, UTC+9, year-round. South Korea abandoned daylight saving in 1988, the year of the Seoul Olympics.

At UTC+9, Incheon shares its clock with [Seoul](/seoul), [Pyongyang](/hamhung), and [Tokyo](/tokyo). The three capitals run on the same offset despite their political differences.

## The airport between timezones

Incheon International Airport handles flights to and from every major timezone on Earth. The airport's function as a transfer hub, particularly for passengers flying between Europe and Southeast Asia or Australia, means that millions of travelers pass through Incheon experiencing temporary timezone displacement.

Korean Air and Asiana airlines use Incheon as their main base. Flight crews crossing from UTC+9 to UTC-5 and back again accumulate timezone debt that is managed through regulated rest periods and scheduling rules.

## The Incheon landing and the Korean War

Incheon was the site of General Douglas MacArthur's famous amphibious landing in September 1950, a strategically risky operation that turned the tide of the Korean War. The landing was planned with extreme precision, timed to the tides. MacArthur had less than three hours at high tide to get troops ashore. Tidal time, not the clock, was the governing constraint.
