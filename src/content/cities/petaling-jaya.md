Petaling Jaya was Malaysia's first planned township, built from the late 1950s to house workers from Kuala Lumpur and reduce overcrowding in the capital. Today PJ, as everyone calls it, is thoroughly merged with the greater KL metropolitan area: the LRT and MRT lines run through it, the morning commute flows in both directions, and the boundary between the two cities is meaningful only for tax purposes and local pride.

Malaysia uses Malaysia Time, UTC+8, year-round with no daylight saving. The IANA identifier is Asia/Kuala_Lumpur. Malaysia's clock was unified across the peninsula and Borneo states (Sabah and Sarawak) in 1982, when Sabah and Sarawak moved from UTC+8 to match the peninsula. This alignment, controversial at the time, simplified trade and communication across the country's two geographic parts.

At 3 degrees north of the equator, Petaling Jaya has essentially constant daylight year-round: sunrise around 7am, sunset around 7:15pm, every day. The concept of seasonal time adjustment is meaningless. The rhythm that matters is the monsoon cycle: the northeast monsoon from November through March brings heavy rain to the east coast, while PJ on the west coast gets its wetter periods from the southwest monsoon from May through September.

## tech hub in the greater KL area

PJ is Malaysia's tech and media hub, home to many corporate offices and studios. The working rhythm is determined by corporate and broadcast schedules more than any natural time cue.
