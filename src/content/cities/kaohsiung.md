Kaohsiung is Taiwan's second-largest city and its largest port, on the southern tip of the island. It runs on Taiwan Standard Time, UTC+8, year-round. Taiwan abolished daylight saving in 1979. The entire island uses a single timezone.

At UTC+8, Kaohsiung shares its clock with [Taipei](/taipei), [Hong Kong](/hong-kong), [Beijing](/beijing), and [Singapore](/singapore). The IANA identifier is Asia/Taipei.

## Taiwan's industrial port and the shipping clock

Kaohsiung Port is one of the busiest container ports in the world and a global hub for semiconductor and electronics exports. Container terminals operate 24 hours a day. Crane operators, logistics staff, and customs officials work rotating shifts synchronized to ship arrivals and departures.

The semiconductor supply chain that Kaohsiung feeds connects to factories and assembly plants in [Shanghai](/shanghai), [Shenzhen](/shenzhen), and [Seoul](/seoul) on one side and to distributors in [Rotterdam](/rotterdam) and [Los Angeles](/los-angeles) on the other. This supply chain operates on continuous global time, with UTC+8 as Kaohsiung's contribution.

## Subtropical south and the typhoon calendar

Kaohsiung sits at 22.6 degrees North, in Taiwan's subtropical south. The typhoon season, from roughly June to October, is the dominant temporal force on the city. When a typhoon approaches, the government issues warnings in stages and schools and businesses close according to a standardized typhoon response protocol. The typhoon schedule interrupts the normal clock more definitively than any daylight saving change could.
