Gaza runs on Palestine Standard Time, UTC+2, sharing its clock with [Jerusalem](/jerusalem) and [Amman](/amman) in winter. In summer, daylight saving moves the city to UTC+3. The timezone identifier in the IANA database is Asia/Hebron, the same used for the West Bank.

Palestine's daylight saving transitions have historically followed the Palestinian Authority's announcements rather than a fixed date rule, meaning the exact transition dates can vary year to year. This differs from neighboring Israel, which follows its own schedule under Asia/Jerusalem.

## One of the oldest inhabited cities on Earth

Gaza has been continuously inhabited for at least 4,000 years. Egyptians, Assyrians, Greeks, Romans, Byzantines, Arabs, Crusaders, Ottomans, and the British Mandate have each held the city and each brought their administrative calendars. Standard clock time arrived during the British Mandate period in the early twentieth century.

The city sits close to the Mediterranean at 31 degrees North latitude, giving it long summer days and relatively mild winters. Solar noon in Gaza falls around 11:45am in standard time, close to the clock.

## Daily life and the call to prayer

In Gaza, as across the Muslim world, the adhan marks time as reliably as any clock. Five prayer times, calculated from the sun's position, structure the day. Fajr before dawn, Dhuhr near midday, Asr in the afternoon, Maghrib at sunset, Isha after dark. This solar schedule runs in parallel with the civil clock, and most residents navigate both without thinking about it.
