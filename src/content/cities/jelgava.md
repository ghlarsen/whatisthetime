Jelgava is Latvia's fourth-largest city, about 40 kilometers southwest of [Riga](/riga). It runs on Eastern European Time, UTC+2 in winter and UTC+3 in summer. Latvia is an EU member and follows EU clock change conventions in late March and late October.

Latvia has been actively involved in EU discussions about abolishing daylight saving. The Latvian government, like several Baltic state governments, has expressed preference for permanent Eastern European Summer Time (UTC+3), arguing that earlier evenings in winter outweigh the cost of dark mornings.

## The Duchy of Courland

Jelgava (Mitau in German) was the capital of the Duchy of Courland and Semigallia, a semi-independent vassal state that existed from 1561 to 1795. The Duchy was remarkably active for its size: it established colonies in Tobago and on the Gambia River in West Africa in the seventeenth century.

The Jelgava Palace, designed by Bartolomeo Rastrelli, the same architect who built the Winter Palace in St. Petersburg, stands on an island in the Lielupe River. The palace clock tower was a landmark of civic time in the duchy's capital.

## Sharing a timezone with [Riga](/riga)

All of Latvia uses Europe/Riga. The clock is the same from the coast to the southern border.
