Dikhil is a regional capital in southwestern Djibouti, in the desert interior near the Ethiopian and Eritrean borders. Like all of [Djibouti](/djibouti), it uses East Africa Time, UTC+3, year-round, under the IANA identifier `Africa/Djibouti`. No daylight saving has ever been observed.

Djibouti sits between 11 and 12 degrees north, giving Dikhil a nearly equatorial daylight pattern. Sunrise runs from about 5:50am to 6:30am and sunset from 6:15pm to 6:55pm across the year. Minimal variation. UTC+3 suits the geography reasonably well: solar noon falls around 12:15pm, close to clock noon.

## Heat as the operating system

Dikhil is one of the hottest places in Djibouti, which is already among the hottest countries in the world. Temperatures regularly exceed 45°C in summer. The day's schedule is organized around heat above all else: work before 10am, shelter from 11am to 4pm, activity returning in the late afternoon. The clock is a formality; the sun's heat is the operating system.
