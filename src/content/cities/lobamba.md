Eswatini (formerly Swaziland) is a rare example of a dual-capital country: [Mbabane](/mbabane) is the administrative capital, while Lobamba is the royal and legislative capital, home to the Swazi Parliament and the royal palaces. Both cities run on Africa/Mbabane time: UTC+2, year-round, no daylight saving.

Lobamba sits in the Ezulwini Valley, the "Valley of Heaven," a stretch of low-lying land between the Mbabane highlands and the Malkerns region. The valley is Eswatini's ceremonial heartland. The Incwala ceremony (a first fruits ceremony in December/January) and Umhlanga (the Reed Dance in August/September) are held here, drawing thousands of participants from across the country. These ceremonies follow the lunar and agricultural calendar, not the civil clock.

At 26.5 degrees south, Lobamba has moderate seasonal light variation. June midwinter brings about 10.5 hours of daylight; December midsummer extends to nearly 14 hours. The Highveld climate brings crisp winter mornings and afternoon thunderstorms in summer.

Eswatini aligns its UTC+2 with South Africa, its dominant trading partner. The lack of daylight saving is consistent across southern Africa's major economies.

[9am in Lobamba is 7am in London](/when/9am-lobamba/london) in winter, two hours ahead of GMT.
