Kanton (also written Canton) is a small coral atoll in the Phoenix Islands group of Kiribati, in the central Pacific. It runs on UTC+13, one of the furthest-ahead timezone offsets on Earth.

This extreme offset is a political choice. Kanton and the Phoenix Islands were historically on the UTC-12 or UTC-11 side of the International Date Line. In 1995, Kiribati shifted the Phoenix and Line Islands to UTC+13 and UTC+14 to create a single calendar day across the entire country. This allowed business and administrative coordination without the confusion of some islands being a full day ahead of others.

## Jumping the date line

The 1995 Kiribati decision was geographically and cartographically significant. UTC+14, used by the Line Islands to the east (including Kiritimati/Christmas Island), is the furthest offset in the world. UTC+13 for Kanton was the secondary adjustment.

Before this change, Kanton was on the eastern (American) side of the date line. After, it moved to the western (Asian/Oceanian) side. The people on the island woke up on one date and found themselves on a different date the following morning.

## A largely uninhabited atoll

Kanton has a small population, primarily Kiribati administrative staff and workers. The atoll was used as a stopover point for early trans-Pacific aviation and was contested by the US and UK in the late colonial period.
