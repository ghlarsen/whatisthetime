New Amsterdam is Guyana's second city, sitting at the mouth of the Berbice River on the country's Atlantic coast. It's a small city by most measures but historically significant: it was the capital of the former Dutch colony of Berbice, one of three colonies (along with Essequibo and Demerara) that merged to form British Guiana in 1831 and eventually independent Guyana in 1966.

[Guyana](/country/guyana) runs on Guyana Time, UTC-4, year-round with no daylight saving. The country is unusual in South America for not using any DST adjustment. At 6 degrees north of the equator, daylight barely varies: sunrise and sunset stay within about 30 minutes of each other year-round, making the whole concept of seasonal time adjustments irrelevant.

New Amsterdam sits just 2 degrees from the equator in terms of latitude. The light is consistent, the temperature is consistently warm and humid, and the Atlantic trades bring some relief to the coastal heat. The Berbice River is tidal at this point, and the rhythm of the river traffic, the ferries, the fishing boats, responds to tides as much as clock time.

The city has a diverse population reflecting Guyana's remarkable ethnic mix: Indo-Guyanese, Afro-Guyanese, and smaller communities of Amerindians, Chinese, and Portuguese. Each community brings its own calendar observances to the city's rhythm.

[9am in New Amsterdam is 1pm in London](/when/9am-new-amsterdam/london), the same offset as [Georgetown](/georgetown).
