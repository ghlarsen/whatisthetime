Nyala is the capital of South Darfur state and one of Sudan's largest cities, though precise population figures are uncertain given the displacement and conflict that have defined the region since the early 2000s. The city sits at the eastern edge of the Sahel, where semi-arid grassland gradually transitions to desert conditions to the north.

[Sudan](/country/sudan) uses Central Africa Time, UTC+2, year-round with no daylight saving. Nyala shares this with [Khartoum](/khartoum) to the northeast. At 12 degrees north, the city's daylight ranges from about 11 to 13 hours across the year. The rainy season from July through September brings the region to life, with annual rainfall that makes agriculture possible in the surrounding areas.

Nyala functions as the main commercial hub for Darfur, a region roughly the size of France. Its markets handle goods for a vast rural hinterland that has no other comparable urban center nearby. The city's market economy connects nomadic pastoralists, settled farmers, and long-distance traders in networks that have existed for centuries along Saharan and Sahelian routes.

## conflict, displacement, and humanitarian operations

The conflict in Darfur that began in 2003 has made Nyala the base for one of the world's largest humanitarian operations. UN agencies, NGOs, and peacekeeping missions have operated from here, adding an international institutional time layer to the city's existing rhythms.
