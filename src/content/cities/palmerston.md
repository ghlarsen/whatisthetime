Palmerston is Darwin's planned satellite city, built from the 1980s to absorb the Northern Territory capital's growth. It sits 20 kilometers southeast of Darwin, close enough that the two effectively function as one urban area. The road connecting them handles heavy commuter traffic in both directions.

The Northern Territory uses Australia/Darwin: UTC+9:30, year-round, no daylight saving. This makes Palmerston, like Darwin, one of the few places in the world with a permanent half-hour offset. The NT joins South Australia in using 9:30, splitting the difference between the Eastern states (UTC+10) and Western Australia (UTC+8). The Northern Territory has consistently voted against implementing daylight saving: residents argue the extended summer evenings would simply mean more heat.

At 12.5 degrees south, Palmerston barely registers seasonal light variation. Sunrise and sunset stay within about 45 minutes of the same time year-round. The climate operates on the tropical wet/dry cycle: the wet season from October through April brings monsoon rains, flooding, and humidity; the dry season from May through September is clear, warm, and comfortable.

Palmerston has a large Defence Force presence nearby and houses many military families. The rhythms of RAAF Base Darwin and the nearby Robertson Barracks give part of the city a schedule that runs on military time.
