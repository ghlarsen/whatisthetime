Abobo is not a separate city so much as a vast northern commune of the greater Abidjan metropolitan area, home to over a million people. It is one of the most densely populated urban areas in West Africa. The clock here is the same as in [Abidjan](/abidjan) proper: UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, permanent, no seasonal adjustment.

What makes Abobo notable in the context of time is its sheer scale. Markets here operate at enormous volume, and the early morning hours, 5am to 8am, are peak commercial hours. The rhythm of work starts before sunrise and tapers by early afternoon, then resumes in the evening. Solar time and clock time happen to align at this latitude. Sunrise is consistently between 6am and 6:30am year-round, which makes UTC+0 feel natural rather than arbitrary.

[Ivory Coast](/country/ivory-coast) sits between roughly 4 and 10 degrees north latitude. The variation in day length between seasons is modest compared to Europe or North America. Sunrise and sunset don't shift dramatically, which means the stable, non-adjusting clock feels like it fits.

[9am in Abobo is 9am in Accra](/when/9am-abobo/accra), the same timezone shared across much of coastal West Africa.
