Bangui sits on the north bank of the Ubangi River, which forms the border with the Democratic Republic of Congo to the south. The city is the capital of the Central African Republic, a country that sits almost exactly at the geographic center of Africa. Despite this central position, it is one of the continent's least connected capitals: no railways, limited road infrastructure, and political instability that has disrupted normal life for much of its post-independence history.

The clock is West Africa Time, UTC+1. The IANA identifier is Africa/Bangui. No daylight saving.

[Bégoua](/begoua), a large suburban area north of the capital, shares the same timezone and is effectively part of the greater Bangui urban area.

At 4.4 degrees north latitude, Bangui has minimal seasonal daylight variation. The equatorial forest climate means two rainy seasons per year and heat and humidity throughout. The daily rhythm follows the heat: morning activity, an afternoon slowdown, evening recovery.

## The river and the market

The Ubangi River is both the city's southern border and its connection to the broader Congo Basin transport network. River traffic, though reduced from historical levels, still moves goods and people. The city's market, the Marché Central, is one of the most active commercial points in Central Africa.
