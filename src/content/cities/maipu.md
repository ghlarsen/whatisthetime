Maipú is a municipality within the greater Santiago metropolitan area, home to over half a million people and part of Chile's capital region. It follows Chile Standard Time alongside [Santiago](/santiago), running UTC-4 in winter (southern hemisphere) and UTC-3 during daylight saving summer months.

## Chile's unpredictable DST and the narrow Andes strip

Chile's daylight saving rules are among the most unpredictable in South America. The government has adjusted DST dates repeatedly over the years, sometimes canceling transitions entirely, other times shifting them by weeks. The earthquake of 2010 disrupted one planned transition. Energy crises have influenced others. Programmers working with Chilean time data keep meticulous notes.

Chile runs as a narrow strip along the Andes, and Maipú sits in the broad central valley where Santiago spreads. At 33 degrees south, day length varies meaningfully: about 9.5 hours in June, 14.5 hours in December. The DST adjustment is genuinely useful here, capturing evening light during the summer growing season.

[9am in Maipú is 1pm in London](/when/9am-maipu/london) during Chilean summer when DST is active and the UK is on GMT. The offset moves depending on which hemisphere's summer you're in.
