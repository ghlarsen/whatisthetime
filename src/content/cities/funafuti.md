Funafuti is the capital of Tuvalu, a Pacific island nation of nine atolls and around 11,000 people. The IANA timezone `Pacific/Funafuti` is set to UTC+12 year-round. At this offset, Funafuti is among the first places in the world to greet each new calendar day, just after the Line Islands of Kiribati at UTC+14 and Samoa at UTC+13.

Tuvalu faces an existential threat from rising sea levels. The atolls, the highest point of which is only about 4 meters above sea level, are increasingly affected by storm surges and saltwater intrusion. The government has pursued climate advocacy at the international level and begun planning for potential relocation of the population. Tuvalu is among the nations that could literally cease to exist as habitable territory within decades.

In this context, Funafuti's timezone is among the least pressing concerns about its future. UTC+12 is geographically and historically reasonable: Tuvalu has maintained this offset since at least the 1970s. For now, Funafuti starts its days early by the world's clock. The question is how many more it has.
