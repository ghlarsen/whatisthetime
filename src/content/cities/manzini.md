Manzini is the largest city in Eswatini (formerly Swaziland) and the country's commercial and industrial heart, even though [Mbabane](/mbabane) serves as the administrative capital. Both cities run on South Africa Standard Time, UTC+2, no daylight saving, the same clock as the South African cities and regions that border the small mountain kingdom.

Eswatini is one of the world's last absolute monarchies, ruled by King Mswati III since 1986. The country is almost entirely surrounded by South Africa (with a short border with Mozambique). That geographic encirclement makes timezone alignment with South Africa not just convenient but essentially inevitable.

At 26 degrees south latitude, Manzini sees meaningful seasonal daylight shifts: about 10.5 hours in June, 13.5 hours in December. The agricultural calendar, particularly for sugarcane which is Eswatini's largest export crop, responds to these seasonal rhythms more than to civil clock adjustments.

[Mbabane](/mbabane) shares this timezone. [9am in Manzini is 7am in London](/when/9am-manzini/london) during European winter.
