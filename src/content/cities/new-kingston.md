New Kingston is the modern commercial district of Jamaica's capital, built in the 1960s and 70s on land that was once a horse racing track. The tower blocks, hotels, and corporate offices here run on a clock that has not changed in decades: Jamaica uses Eastern Standard Time, UTC-5, year-round, with no daylight saving.

[Jamaica](/country/jamaica) abolished daylight saving time in 1983 and has not returned to it. The IANA identifier America/Jamaica reflects this permanent UTC-5 position. In North American summer, when New York and Miami shift to EDT (UTC-4), Jamaica finds itself on the same clock as Central Standard Time zones. In North American winter, Jamaica aligns with New York's Eastern Standard. The alignment shifts twice a year without Jamaica moving at all.

At 18 degrees north, Jamaica's daylight variation is modest. Summer days run to about 13 hours; winter days shorten to just over 11. The consistent tropical climate makes seasonal adjustments feel unnecessary. The reggae hour is not defined by the clock.

## finance district and the dollar clock

New Kingston specifically operates on international finance time: its banks and offices calibrate their hours against New York trading hours, which makes the permanent UTC-5 alignment logical. The business of Jamaica's financial sector is largely denominated in US dollars and priced off US benchmarks.
