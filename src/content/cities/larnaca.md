Larnaca is the arrival and departure point for most visitors to Cyprus, home to the island's main international airport. It runs on Asia/Nicosia time: Eastern European Time, UTC+2 in winter, UTC+3 during daylight saving. Cyprus is an EU member and follows EU DST transitions.

The airport at Larnaca serves as an unusual time gateway. Travelers from western Europe arrive having gained two hours from London time in winter, or matching it in the early weeks of DST transition when the clocks are sometimes offset. The jet lag is usually mild compared to intercontinental flights, but the two-hour difference from the UK is enough to shift the feeling of arrival time.

At 35 degrees north, Larnaca has meaningful seasonal light variation. Summer evenings during CEST (UTC+3) stretch past 8pm. Winter afternoons end by 5pm. Cyprus has a Mediterranean climate where the sunlight is reliable almost year-round, making daylight saving more about social alignment than energy savings.

The Hala Sultan Tekke mosque, sitting at the edge of the salt lake beside the airport, is one of the most important Islamic sites in the region. Flamingos winter on the lake each year, arriving in October and leaving in March, their schedule governed entirely by their own calendar.

[Limassol](/limassol) to the west keeps the same time. [9am in Larnaca is 7am in London](/when/9am-larnaca/london) in winter, two hours ahead.
