Guédiawaye is one of the most densely populated areas in West Africa, a suburban city immediately north of [Dakar](/dakar). It runs on UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. Senegal does not observe daylight saving.

At UTC+0, Guédiawaye shares its clock with [London](/london) in winter, [Monrovia](/monrovia), and [Abidjan](/abidjan). When London shifts to BST in summer, Guédiawaye stays at UTC+0, meaning Dakar briefly runs an hour behind London.

## Dakar's urban sprawl

Guédiawaye is administratively separate from Dakar but functions as part of the Dakar metropolitan area. The city grew rapidly through the late twentieth century as rural migration transformed the Cap-Vert peninsula. Population estimates vary, but the city has over 300,000 residents packed into a small footprint.

Daily commutes into Dakar are long. The ndeye, traditional peanut sellers, the thiéboudienne vendors, the tailors in their workshops: all start their commercial day early, often before 7am.

## Solar time at 15 degrees North

Guédiawaye sits at 14.8 degrees North latitude. Day length variation across the year is modest: about 2.5 hours between the longest and shortest days. UTC+0 puts solar noon at around 12:45pm due to the city's 17 degrees West longitude. This makes the working day start and end a bit later in solar terms than the clock suggests.
