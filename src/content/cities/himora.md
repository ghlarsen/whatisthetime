Himora is a town in Eritrea's Gash-Barka region, near the border with Ethiopia. It runs on East Africa Time, UTC+3, the same as [Asmara](/asmara) and all of Eritrea. No daylight saving.

Eritrea and Ethiopia share the UTC+3 offset. The border between them, which was the site of a devastating war from 1998 to 2000, does not create a timezone change. Travelers crossing between the two countries stay on the same clock.

## Eritrea's independent clock

Eritrea became independent from Ethiopia in 1993 after a thirty-year independence war. The new nation inherited East Africa Time from the Ethiopian administrative structure and kept it. The IANA database uses Africa/Asmara as the identifier for Eritrea's timezone.

Before 2010, the IANA identifier was Africa/Asmera (an older transliteration). The spelling was corrected in the 2010 database release, a small example of how geographic naming decisions ripple into technical infrastructure.

## The western lowlands

Himora sits in Eritrea's hotter, lower-lying western region, quite different from the cool highland climate of Asmara. Agricultural and pastoral rhythms dominate. The rainy season, concentrated in July and August, structures the year. Markets and movement follow the seasonal pattern more than the clock.
