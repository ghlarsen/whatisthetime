Jayapura is the capital of Papua province, in the far east of Indonesia on the island of New Guinea. It runs on Eastern Indonesia Time (WIT), UTC+9, year-round. No daylight saving.

UTC+9 puts Jayapura on the same clock as [Tokyo](/tokyo), [Seoul](/seoul), and [Pyongyang](/hamhung), despite being in Southeast Asia near the equator. Indonesia spans three timezones: UTC+7 in the west (Java, Sumatra), UTC+8 in the center (Bali, Kalimantan, Sulawesi), and UTC+9 in the east (Papua, Maluku).

## On the border of West Papua and Papua New Guinea

Jayapura sits near the border between Indonesian Papua and Papua New Guinea, which uses UTC+10. Crossing the border means a one-hour time change.

The city was known as Hollandia under Dutch colonial rule. When Indonesia incorporated West New Guinea in 1963, the city was renamed multiple times before settling on Jayapura.

## A city between cultures

Jayapura is home to both indigenous Papuan communities and migrants from other parts of Indonesia, particularly Java and Sulawesi. This demographic mix brings multiple cultural time systems into proximity: Javanese traditional time-keeping, Papuan seasonal and ceremonial calendars, and the civil UTC+9 clock all coexist.
