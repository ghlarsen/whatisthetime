Atakpamé is a city in central Togo, sitting in the country's middle highlands zone about halfway between the coastal capital Lomé and the northern border. It serves as the commercial and administrative center of the Plateaux region, known for agriculture and the surrounding hills.

[Togo](/country/togo) runs on UTC+0, the same as [Accra](/accra) in neighboring Ghana. The IANA identifier is Africa/Lome. No daylight saving.

Togo's use of UTC+0 is consistent with much of coastal West Africa. The country sits between roughly 0 and 1.5 degrees east longitude, which makes UTC+0 a reasonable solar alignment. The sun rises at roughly 6am and sets at roughly 6pm year-round, with only modest seasonal variation. The clock fits the day.

Atakpamé is a market city for the surrounding agricultural region, known for cotton and food crop production. Market days draw people in from the surrounding villages and valleys. The rhythm here is more agricultural than industrial: early mornings for the market, slower midday hours, evening social activity.

The city's position in the highland zone gives it a slightly cooler and wetter character than the coastal south or the semi-arid north. The harmattan dry season wind from the Sahara arrives in November and February, bringing haze and dry air. This seasonal shift is felt in the daily rhythm even if the clock doesn't change.
