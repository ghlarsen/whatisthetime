Maradi is Niger's largest city and commercial center, sitting in the south of the country near the Nigerian border. It runs on West Africa Time, UTC+1, year-round. [Niger](/country/niger) applies no daylight saving.

The city is at the heart of Niger's peanut and grain trade, and market rhythms here are more tied to the agricultural calendar and the movements of traders from Nigeria to the south than to clock-based scheduling. The Maradi market is one of the most important in the Sahel region.

## Sahel seasons and the heat pause

At 13 degrees north latitude, Maradi sits in the Sahel zone where the hot dry season and the short rainy season are the primary organizers of the year. Day length shifts from about 11 hours in December to 13 hours in June. The midday heat is intense for most of the year, and the working day often pauses in the early afternoon regardless of what the clock says.

[Niamey](/niamey), the national capital, shares this timezone. [9am in Maradi is 8am in London](/when/9am-maradi/london) during European winter, a one-hour gap.
