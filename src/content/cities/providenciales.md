Providenciales, known locally as Provo, is where most of the Turks and Caicos Islands' tourism happens. The island has some of the most photographed beaches in the Caribbean: Grace Bay's sand is genuinely the color it appears in resort photographs, a pale white that turns the shallow water an improbable turquoise.

The [Turks and Caicos Islands](/country/turks-and-caicos-islands) use Eastern Time, UTC-5 in winter (EST) and UTC-4 in summer (EDT). This makes Providenciales one of the few Caribbean territories that observes daylight saving time, aligning its clock with the US East Coast year-round. The practical reason is clear: the majority of visitors come from the eastern US and Canada.

At 21 degrees north, the daylight variation is modest. Summer evenings extend to around 7:30pm. Winter days end closer to 6pm. The consistency of Caribbean light is one of the things that makes the islands feel timeless in the resort brochure sense, and genuinely peaceful in the real sense.

Grand Turk, the administrative capital, is a different island 150 kilometers to the southeast. It uses the same timezone but has a very different character: quieter, more residential, less developed, with a historic salt industry in the salt ponds.

[9am in Providenciales is 2pm in London](/when/9am-providenciales/london) in winter.
