Ashgabat holds a Guinness World Record: the highest concentration of white marble-clad buildings in the world. The city has been almost entirely rebuilt since a catastrophic 1948 earthquake, and most of its current appearance dates to the post-Soviet construction projects of the 1990s and 2000s, when the first president created a city of gold-domed palaces, vast empty boulevards, and monuments to his own cult of personality.

The clock is Turkmenistan Time, UTC+5. The IANA identifier is Asia/Ashgabat. No daylight saving.

Turkmenistan is one of the world's most closed countries. Reliable information about daily life in Ashgabat is difficult to obtain. What is clear from the clock: UTC+5 puts the city in alignment with [Tashkent](/tashkent) to the northeast, though the two capitals have very different characters.

At 37.9 degrees north, Ashgabat sits in a harsh climate: hot summers (regularly above 40 degrees Celsius), cold winters, and very little rainfall in the Karakum Desert that surrounds the city on three sides. The Kopetdag mountain range rises dramatically to the south, forming the border with Iran.

The city's white boulevards and golden domes are largely empty of pedestrian traffic in the midday heat. Life happens in the early morning and evening, with a long midday pause when the temperature makes outdoor activity dangerous.
