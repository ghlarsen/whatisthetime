Mont-Dore is a commune immediately south of [Noumea](/noumea), New Caledonia's capital, and part of the broader urban area. It runs on New Caledonia Time, UTC+11, year-round with no daylight saving. [New Caledonia](/country/new-caledonia) is a French special collectivity in the southwest Pacific.

The territory voted in three independence referendums between 2018 and 2021, each returning a majority for remaining with France, though the final vote was boycotted by Kanak independence parties. The political status question shapes New Caledonia's relationship with French institutions, including the administrative timezone, but the civil clock itself is simply UTC+11.

## nickel, referendums, and Pacific time

New Caledonia is home to some of the world's largest nickel deposits, and the mining industry runs on industrial shifts that operate around the clock. The UTC+11 offset places New Caledonia close in time to eastern Australia: [Sydney](/sydney) is one hour behind during AEST, making coordination with Australian mining services straightforward.
