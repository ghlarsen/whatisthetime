Hlaingthaya Township is a large industrial township on the western outskirts of [Yangon](/yangon), Myanmar's commercial capital. It runs on Myanmar Time, UTC+6:30, the same as all of Myanmar. No daylight saving.

UTC+6:30 is one of the world's relatively rare 30-minute offset timezones. Myanmar chose it during the British colonial period, when the offset was calculated from Rangoon's longitude, and kept it through independence and multiple governments.

## An industrial satellite

Hlaingthaya is one of Yangon's largest townships by population, home to garment factories, light industry, and dense residential areas that house factory workers. The industrial character means shift schedules and factory time discipline are central to daily life. Workers commute across the Hlaing River from Yangon proper.

Myanmar's garment industry, a major exporter to Europe and North America, means Hlaingthaya's factories operate on international delivery schedules while workers live by UTC+6:30.

## Coordinating UTC+6:30 with the world

At UTC+6:30, Myanmar sits between the Indian Subcontinent (UTC+5:30), Bangladesh (UTC+6), and Thailand (UTC+7). The offset means Myanmar is out of sync with all its immediate neighbors, which creates constant timezone arithmetic for cross-border trade.
