Ensenada, 100 kilometers south of Tijuana on the Pacific coast of Baja California, uses `America/Tijuana`: Pacific Standard Time (UTC-8) in winter and Pacific Daylight Time (UTC-7) in summer, following the same DST schedule as California. Baja California Norte is the one state in Mexico that observes US-synchronized daylight saving, because its economy and social connections are tied to Southern California rather than to the rest of Mexico.

## wine country on California time

Ensenada is the center of Mexico's wine country, with the Valle de Guadalupe wine region just east of the city producing internationally recognized wines. The harvest season (late August through October) shapes the town's rhythms in ways no clock policy affects: grapes ripen when they ripen.

The Pacific Time alignment means Ensenada and San Diego share the same clock year-round. A drive from [Tijuana](/tijuana) to Ensenada along the coastal toll road stays in one timezone throughout. But continuing south to Mexicali brings a clock change: Mexicali is in the Mountain Time zone. Baja California's timezone creates a narrow Pacific corridor running south along the coast, cut off from the rest of Mexico's timezone blocks by geography and politics.
