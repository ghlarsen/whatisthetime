Callao is Peru's principal seaport and a constitutional province that borders Lima. The two cities form a continuous urban area. Callao runs on America/Lima: UTC-5, Peru Time, year-round. Peru does not observe daylight saving time.

The UTC-5 offset places Callao on the same clock as [Bogotá](/bogota) to the north. At 12 degrees south latitude, Callao has moderate seasonal daylight variation: June sunrise around 6:30am, sunset around 5:45pm. December sunrise around 5:45am, sunset around 6:30pm. The fixed clock maps reasonably onto these seasonal shifts.

## The port of Lima

Callao is the Pacific gateway for Lima and, by extension, for Peru's landlocked interior. The port handles minerals, agricultural exports, and manufactured goods. Container shipping from Callao reaches China, the United States, and European markets. The port's schedules operate on UTC, coordinating with ships and shipping lines that keep universal maritime time.

The city was a major colonial port. Spanish galleons loaded silver from the Potosí mines here for transport to Spain. Pirate attacks on Callao were a persistent problem in the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries, leading to the construction of the Real Felipe fortress, which still stands and is one of the best-preserved colonial fortifications in South America.

## Lima's twin

Callao and Lima have separate legal status as constitutional provinces but are functionally one city. Millions of people commute between them daily. The Metropolitan Lima transit system links them. The same cloud patterns, the same garúa (coastal fog) characteristic of Peru's central coast, covers both.

The garúa season, roughly June through October, brings cool mist and low clouds from the Humboldt Current. Sunrise is often invisible behind cloud cover for months. The fixed UTC-5 clock remains constant while the sky cycles through fog and occasional clarity.

The [time conversion from Callao to London](/when/9am-callao/london) puts London five hours ahead in winter, six in summer when the UK observes BST.
