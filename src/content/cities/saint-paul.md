Saint-Paul is Réunion's second-largest commune, stretching along the western coast and inland toward the Mafate cirque. The town of Saint-Paul itself is at sea level, but the commune includes mountain communities at considerable altitude. The Friday market in Saint-Paul, held at the seafront, is one of the island's most popular: local produce, vanilla, spices, rum, flowers.

[Réunion](/country/reunion) runs on UTC+4, Réunion Time, year-round without daylight saving. Saint-Paul shares this clock with [Saint-Denis](/saint-denis) to the north and the rest of the island. The `Indian/Reunion` timezone covers the whole department.

The western coast of Réunion faces the sunset. The Hermitage and Saint-Gilles beaches here are the island's main beach tourism zone: the lagoon is protected by a barrier reef, the water calm and clear, facing west for the evening light. At 4pm, the mountains behind the coast cast their shadows over the beach while the ocean still catches the sun.

## the western beaches and Mafate above

The Mafate cirque above Saint-Paul is the most isolated of Réunion's three cirques. The hiking trails from the western coast ascend to it steeply. The communities inside Mafate have no road access; supplies arrive by helicopter or on foot. The rhythm of those communities is entirely different from the beach towns below.
