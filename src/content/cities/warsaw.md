Warsaw was systematically destroyed. After the 1944 Warsaw Uprising, German forces spent months methodically demolishing the city building by building, burning libraries, blowing up the Royal Castle, executing the population. When the Red Army entered in January 1945, roughly 85% of the city was rubble.

What exists today is a reconstruction, built from historical photographs, paintings, and architectural records. The Old Town that tourists visit was painstakingly recreated from scratch after the war. The Royal Castle, destroyed stone by stone, was rebuilt and reopened in 1984. Warsaw is UNESCO-listed not for its age but for what its restoration represents.

[Poland](/country/poland) keeps Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter and UTC+2 during summer DST. Warsaw sits at 52 degrees north, with meaningful seasonal daylight variation: June sunsets past 9pm, December darkness before 4pm.

The Jewish quarter of Warsaw, once home to the largest Jewish community in Europe, was liquidated in the Holocaust. The POLIN Museum of the History of Polish Jews, opened in 2013 on the site of the former ghetto, chronicles a thousand years of Jewish life in Poland with specific attention to dates and places.

[9am in Warsaw is 8am in London](/when/9am-warsaw/london). [Wrocław](/wroc-aw) to the southwest shares the same Polish timezone.
