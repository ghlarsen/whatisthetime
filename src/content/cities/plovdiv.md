Plovdiv has been continuously inhabited for at least 6,000 years. The Thracians, the Macedonians under Philip II, the Romans who called it Philippopolis, the Byzantines, the Ottomans, and finally Bulgaria all claimed these six hills above the Maritsa River. The city predates most of Europe's nations by thousands of years.

[Bulgaria](/country/bulgaria) uses Eastern European Time, UTC+2 in winter and UTC+3 during summer daylight saving. Plovdiv shares this schedule with [Sofia](/sofia) to the west, and with [Athens](/athens) and [Bucharest](/bucharest) across the wider Balkan region.

The old town climbs across three of Plovdiv's hills, a maze of National Revival architecture from the 18th and 19th centuries. In the morning, light catches the colorful facades before it reaches the lower modern city. The Roman amphitheater, still used for concerts, sits embedded in the hillside: an outdoor stage where the sun sets directly behind the stage in summer.

At 42 degrees north, summer evenings extend past 9pm. The outdoor cafes in the old town and along Kapana, the arts quarter, fill from early evening and stay busy until late. Winter brings short days, gray skies, and fog rolling in from the Maritsa valley.

[9am in Plovdiv is 7am in London](/when/9am-plovdiv/london) in winter.
