Johor Bahru is the capital of Johor state and Malaysia's southernmost major city, separated from Singapore by the Johor Strait and connected by two causeways. It runs on Malaysia Standard Time, UTC+8, year-round. No daylight saving.

Singapore also uses UTC+8, so crossing between Johor Bahru and Singapore involves no timezone change. This matters enormously: hundreds of thousands of people commute between the two cities daily, making it one of the world's largest cross-border commuter flows.

## Why UTC+8 and not UTC+7

Malaysia's geographic position at about 104 degrees East longitude suggests UTC+7 as the natural solar time. Malaysia runs an hour ahead of solar time. This was a deliberate decision made in 1982, when Malaysia unified its two halves (peninsular Malaysia and Sabah/Sarawak in Borneo) to UTC+8 to improve coordination with Singapore and align with the East Asian economic zone.

The social effect: sunrise in Johor Bahru falls close to 7am, and sunset around 7pm, year-round. Natural light bookends the working day neatly.

## The daily crossing

The Causeway and the Second Link bridge collectively handle over 300,000 daily crossings between Johor Bahru and Singapore. Workers who earn Singapore dollars but live in Johor for lower costs wake at 5am to queue for morning crossing. This cross-border life means the working day starts early for a significant portion of the city's population.
