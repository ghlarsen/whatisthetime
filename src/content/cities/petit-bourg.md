Petit-Bourg is one of Guadeloupe's municipalities on the larger island of Basse-Terre, sitting on the eastern coast facing Grande-Terre across the narrow Rivière Salée. The municipality includes coastal lowlands and rises into the rainforest interior toward the Basse-Terre mountains, where La Soufrière volcano reaches 1,467 meters. The landscape shifts dramatically within a short distance.

[Guadeloupe](/country/guadeloupe) is a French overseas department and uses Atlantic Standard Time, UTC-4, year-round with no daylight saving. The IANA identifier is America/Guadeloupe. As a French territory, Guadeloupe uses the euro and follows French law, but it keeps a Caribbean clock rather than aligning with European time.

At 16 degrees north, Petit-Bourg's daylight barely varies: around 11 to 13 hours across the year. The rhythm is shaped by the wet and dry season cycle of the Caribbean: the dry season (February through June) and the wet season (July through December, with the hurricane risk from August through October).

Agriculture in the Petit-Bourg area includes banana cultivation, which is one of Guadeloupe's main export products. The banana harvest has a continuous rather than seasonal rhythm, with fruit ready year-round in the right conditions.
