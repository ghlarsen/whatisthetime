Luanda sits just south of the equator on Angola's Atlantic coast, and at this latitude the sun keeps a reliable schedule. Sunrise around 6am, sunset around 6pm, year-round variation of less than an hour. West Africa Time, UTC+1, is the zone for [Angola](/country/angola), and the clocks never change.

The city is one of Africa's most expensive, a consequence of the oil industry that reshaped the economy after the 27-year civil war that ended in 2002. The working day often stretches into the evening, partly because the equatorial light offers no strong signal that day is done.

[Lubango](/lubango), Angola's southern highland city, shares this same clock, making coordination across the country uncomplicated. Both cities also align with [Lagos](/lagos) and Kinshasa, which run UTC+1 as well, though the Congo basin cities use different IANA identifiers.

[9am in Luanda is 8am in London](/when/9am-luanda/london) during European winter, a clean one-hour offset useful for the many oil and resource companies that maintain offices in both cities.
