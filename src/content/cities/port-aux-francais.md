Kerguelen Island sits in the Southern Ocean, roughly equidistant between Antarctica, southern Africa, and southern Australia. There are no scheduled commercial flights. Ships arrive several times a year. Port-aux-Français, the French research station at the island's main harbor, is home to between 50 and 120 scientists and support staff, depending on season.

The [French Southern Territories](/country/french-southern-territories) use UTC+5 for Kerguelen, a practical choice rather than a geographic one. The island sits at roughly 69 degrees east longitude, which would suggest UTC+4:36 by strict solar calculation. The UTC+5 offset keeps the station's time reasonably close to local solar time while using a round number.

At 49 degrees south latitude, Kerguelen's daylight variation is significant. December brings nearly 17 hours of daylight. June brings fewer than 8. The Southern Ocean context means that this daylight comes with wind: Kerguelen averages 150 days of gale-force winds per year. The island was named "Desolation Island" by early English sailors, and the name fits.

Cats brought to the island as pets in the 19th century went feral and devastated the local rabbit population (which were also introduced). Eradication programs have been ongoing. The island runs on a specific kind of ecological time as well as UTC+5.
