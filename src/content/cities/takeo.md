Takeo is the capital of Takeo Province in southern [Cambodia](/country/cambodia), in a flat agricultural region near the Vietnamese border. It runs on Indochina Time (UTC+7) all year, the same fixed offset as the rest of Cambodia and the Indochina peninsula.

The province is historically significant: the Phnom Da archaeological site, about 30 kilometers from Takeo town, contains temple ruins from the pre-Angkorean Funan and Chenla kingdoms, some of the earliest Cambodian stone architecture. The city sits on older time in the sense that the region has been continuously settled and agriculturally active for over two millennia.

## monsoon calendar over the clock

Rice cultivation dominates the Takeo lowlands. The agricultural calendar, dictated by the Mekong system's flooding cycle and monsoon rains, shapes daily life more directly than the clock. Planting and harvest times shift the whole community's schedule regardless of what UTC+7 says.

Takeo town itself is a quiet provincial capital, much smaller than Phnom Penh or Siem Reap. Its daily rhythm runs on market morning activity and a slower afternoon heat, a pattern common across the Cambodian lowlands.

[Siem Reap](/siem-reap) to the northwest shares the same Indochina Time offset.

[9am in Takeo is 2am in London](/when/9am-takeo/london) in winter, 3am during British Summer Time.
