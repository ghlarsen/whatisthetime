Eagle River is a community north of Anchorage in the Matanuska-Susitna area, effectively a suburb of Alaska's largest city. It uses `America/Anchorage`, UTC-9 in winter (Alaska Standard Time) and UTC-8 in summer (Alaska Daylight Time). Alaska observes standard US DST dates.

At about 61 degrees north, Eagle River has some of the most dramatic daylight variation in the US outside of actual Arctic territory. Near the summer solstice, the sun barely dips below the horizon for about 4 hours; it never gets fully dark. In December, the sun is up for less than 5 hours. The daylight saving shift in spring and fall is felt acutely at these latitudes because the change in morning light is so stark.

Alaska Time (AKST) was set as UTC-9 rather than the UTC-10 that Alaska's longitude would suggest. The choice was made to keep Alaska within one timezone zone and to maintain reasonable business hours overlap with the US West Coast (UTC-8/UTC-7). [Anchorage](/anchorage) and Eagle River, within a few kilometers of each other, run identically on this political rather than solar compromise.
