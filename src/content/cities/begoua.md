Bégoua is a densely populated district immediately north of [Bangui](/bangui), the Central African Republic's capital. It functions as an extension of the capital's urban area rather than a separate city, but its size and population make it significant in the country's urban hierarchy.

The clock is West Africa Time, UTC+1. The IANA identifier is Africa/Bangui. No daylight saving. All of the Central African Republic runs on this single clock.

The Central African Republic is named for its geographic position but is in many practical ways one of the continent's most isolated countries. Landlocked, with limited infrastructure, and affected by ongoing political instability, the country's clock is one of the few uncomplicated aspects of its governance.

At roughly 4.4 degrees north latitude, Bégoua and the Bangui region have near-constant day length throughout the year: about 12 hours of daylight. The equatorial climate brings two rainy seasons per year. The daily rhythm here follows the heat and rain patterns more than any clock-based schedule.

## Security and the fabric of daily life

The northern suburb of Bangui has been affected by the same security challenges as the capital itself. Periodic unrest has disrupted normal commerce and movement patterns. But the market activity, the daily fabric of life, continues in the gaps between disruptions.
