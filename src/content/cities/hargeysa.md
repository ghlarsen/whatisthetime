Hargeysa is the capital of Somaliland, the self-declared republic that broke away from Somalia in 1991 and has operated with its own government, currency, and institutions since. It runs on East Africa Time, UTC+3, the same as [Mogadishu](/mogadishu), [Nairobi](/nairobi), and [Addis Ababa](/addis-ababa). No daylight saving.

Somaliland is not internationally recognized as an independent state, but it functions as one. The Hargeisa Clock Tower in the city center is a symbol of the city's aspirations: a monument to local civic identity.

## A city that rebuilt itself

Hargeysa was largely destroyed in 1988 during the Somali government's brutal suppression of the Somali National Movement. A monument in the city center, a MiG jet mounted on a plinth, marks the airstrike. The city was rebuilt almost entirely from scratch after 1991.

That rebuilding included establishing consistent civil administration, including clocks, schedules, and business hours that function independently of Mogadishu's chaos to the south.

## Pastoral and urban time

Somaliland's economy mixes urban commerce with the livestock trade. Camels, sheep, and goats are exported through the port of Berbera. The livestock trade follows a seasonal calendar tied to rain patterns and grazing conditions, a time system older than any civil timezone.
