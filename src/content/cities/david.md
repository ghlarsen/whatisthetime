David, capital of Panama's Chiriquí province, runs on UTC-5 year-round under `America/Panama`. Panama adopted Eastern Standard Time as a permanent arrangement after the US Canal Zone influence in the 20th century, and the country has never observed daylight saving time. The practical benefit is stability: the Panama Canal operates 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, and its scheduling coordinates with global shipping that runs on fixed UTC offsets.

David is the agricultural capital of western Panama, close to the Costa Rican border and the Barú Volcano. Coffee from the Boquete highlands above David is among the most expensive in the world. The coffee harvest runs November through March, and the farmers working the volcanic slopes start their days before 5am regardless of what the clock says. UTC-5 happens to put sunrise at around 5:30am at the winter solstice in David, a reasonable alignment.

The border with Costa Rica is a short drive north. Costa Rica runs on UTC-6 year-round, one hour behind David. The time difference creates small but real friction for the cross-border commerce in produce and labor that flows between the two countries daily.
