İzmir is Turkey's third-largest city and the major port of the Aegean coast. It runs on UTC+3 year-round, the same as [Istanbul](/istanbul) and all of Turkey. Since Turkey's 2016 decision to abolish winter clock changes, the offset has been fixed.

At 38.4 degrees North on the Aegean coast, İzmir has a Mediterranean climate: hot dry summers, mild winters. The city faces Greece across the Aegean. [Athens](/athens) runs on UTC+2 in winter and UTC+3 in summer, meaning it matches İzmir's UTC+3 in summer but diverges by an hour in winter. Ferry services between the two countries must account for this seasonal difference.

## Ancient Smyrna and modern İzmir

The city is built on the site of ancient Smyrna, one of the great cities of the ancient Greek world and a claimed birthplace of Homer. The city's layered history, Greek, Roman, Byzantine, Ottoman, and Turkish Republican, means multiple historical calendars have structured life here.

The modern city was substantially rebuilt after a catastrophic fire in 1922. The new waterfront, the kordon, became the city's social center: a promenade where the day ends with evening walks and seafood dinners running late into the night.

## Aegean rhythms

İzmir's Mediterranean character makes it Turkey's most distinctly leisured city. Long lunches, late evenings, summer schedules that run past midnight: the social clock runs later than the formal working day. UTC+3 in summer means the sun sets well after 8pm, supporting an outdoor evening culture.
