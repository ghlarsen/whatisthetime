Homs is Syria's third-largest city, sitting at the crossroads of ancient trade routes in central Syria. It runs on UTC+3, year-round. Syria had observed daylight saving until 2022, when it was abolished.

Homs shares the Asia/Damascus timezone with the Syrian capital. At UTC+3, the city is in the same timezone as [Riyadh](/riyadh), [Baghdad](/baghdad), and [Nairobi](/nairobi), though the geographic and cultural context is entirely different.

## The city of the revolution and reconstruction

Homs was at the center of the Syrian Civil War that began in 2011. The old city, particularly the Bab Amr neighborhood, was besieged and largely destroyed between 2011 and 2013. Rebuilding has been slow and partial. The Homs that exists today is both a continuation of one of Syria's oldest cities and a city dramatically altered by years of conflict.

Time took on a different meaning in Homs during those years: not the timezone on the clock, but the lived experience of siege conditions, displacement, and survival.

## An ancient crossroads

Homs (ancient Emesa) was a significant city under the Romans and a center of early Christianity. The city's position between Damascus and Aleppo made it a natural waypoint on trade and military routes. Multiple civilizations have set their calendars and schedules here.
