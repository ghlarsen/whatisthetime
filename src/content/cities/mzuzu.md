Mzuzu sits at around 1,250 meters elevation in Malawi's Northern Region, cool enough to be the country's main tea-growing center and the hub for the surrounding highland districts. The Viphya Plateau to the south carries the largest manmade forest in Africa. The city exists in that particular African highland atmosphere: bright days, cool nights, and a freshness to the air that the lowlands near Lake Malawi don't have.

[Malawi](/country/malawi) runs on Central Africa Time, UTC+2, no daylight saving. Mzuzu shares this with [Blantyre](/blantyre) in the south. At around 11 degrees south, seasonal light variation is modest: roughly 11 to 13 hours of daylight depending on the time of year, with sunrise consistently between 5:30am and 6:30am.

The city is a market and service hub for the surrounding rural districts. The weekly market days pull in buyers and sellers from hours away. Transport connections to Blantyre and to Lilongwe pass through or near Mzuzu, and the city functions as a northern waypoint on the M1 highway that runs the length of the country.

## university, markets, and highland rhythm

Mzuzu also has Mzuzu University, one of Malawi's main institutions of higher education, which gives the city a younger demographic mix and an academic rhythm that structures part of its year around the academic calendar.
