Nagoya sits almost exactly at the midpoint of Japan's main island, which is also almost exactly at the midpoint of the country's industrial output. Toyota's headquarters and main manufacturing facilities are nearby in Toyota City. The Port of Nagoya is Japan's highest-tonnage port. The Shinkansen Tokaido line, the world's first high-speed rail line when it opened in 1964, connects Nagoya to [Tokyo](/tokyo) in about 80 minutes at 285 km/h. The trains run on the second.

[Japan](/country/japan) uses a single timezone, Japan Standard Time at UTC+9, with no daylight saving. Nagoya's longitude sits very close to the 135-degree meridian that was chosen as Japan's standard meridian in 1888, making it one of the cities closest to "true" solar noon alignment with JST.

Sunrise in Nagoya ranges from about 4:40am in late June to around 7am in late December. The Tokai region, at 35 degrees north, experiences clear four-season progression. Spring sakura viewing at Nagoya Castle is a major cultural event with precise timing: the bloom forecast is taken seriously, and peak viewing windows are often just a few days.

Nagoya has a reputation within Japan as a practical, business-focused city, less given to the cultural performance of Tokyo or Kyoto. The pace reflects that: early starts, disciplined schedules, the Shinkansen running exactly on time.

[9am in Nagoya is 12am in London](/when/9am-nagoya/london), the same as [Tokyo](/tokyo) and [Osaka](/osaka).
