Paysandú sits on the western bank of the Uruguay River, directly facing the Argentine city of Colón across the water. The Puente General Artigas connects the two cities, and cross-border commerce, family ties, and tourism move freely over it. The two cities are in different countries with different currencies and different timezones only for part of the year: Uruguay uses UTC-3 year-round, while Argentina uses UTC-3 year-round as well. They match exactly.

[Uruguay](/country/uruguay) uses Uruguay Standard Time, UTC-3, year-round, with no daylight saving since 2015. Uruguay had used DST for many years before deciding the energy savings were insufficient to justify the disruption. The permanent UTC-3 simplifies scheduling and aligns with Argentina's permanent offset.

At 32 degrees south, Paysandú experiences real seasonal variation: summer days in December approach 14 hours of light, winter days in June shorten to under 10. The Uruguay River moderates the temperature somewhat, and the riparian landscape is greener and more humid than the interior.

Paysandú is known for its leather industry and its beef production from the surrounding ranching lands. The city has a slower, more pastoral character than Montevideo on the coast, and the rhythm of the agricultural year, the cattle movements, the grain harvest, shapes the economic calendar.

[9am in Paysandú is 12pm in London](/when/9am-paysandu/london), the same as [Montevideo](/montevideo).
