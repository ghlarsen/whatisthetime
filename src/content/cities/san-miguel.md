San Miguel sits in eastern [El Salvador](/country/el-salvador), close to the Honduran border, in the shadow of the Chaparrastique volcano. The city runs on Central Standard Time (UTC-6), the same offset as [San Salvador](/san-salvador) to the west, and it stays there all year. El Salvador abolished daylight saving time in 1987 and has not looked back.

At roughly 13 degrees north latitude, daylight variation through the year is modest. The longest days in June see around 12 hours and 45 minutes of light. The shortest in December drop only to about 11 hours and 30 minutes. When sunlight shifts by little more than an hour between seasons, there is limited incentive to move the clocks.

## church bells and the midday pause

The city's twice-daily rhythm used to be anchored by the church bells of the Cathedral of Our Lady of Peace. Now it runs on phone alerts and market opening times, but the pattern remains: early rising, a quiet pause through the hottest midday hours, and activity resuming in the cooler late afternoon.

[9am in San Miguel is 3pm in London](/when/9am-san-miguel/london) during northern hemisphere summer, and 4pm during winter when the UK shifts off daylight saving while El Salvador stays put.
