Charlotte Amalie is the capital of the United States Virgin Islands, located on the island of St. Thomas. It runs on America/St_Thomas: UTC-4, Atlantic Standard Time, year-round. The US Virgin Islands, despite being a US territory, does not observe daylight saving time, unlike the mainland United States.

This creates a seasonal relationship with the US mainland: in winter, the Eastern US is UTC-5 and Charlotte Amalie is UTC-4, so the USVI is one hour ahead. In summer, when the Eastern US springs forward to UTC-4, it matches the USVI. The alignment shifts with the seasons.

## St. Thomas harbor

Charlotte Amalie's harbor is one of the Caribbean's finest natural anchorages and has been a trading hub since the Danish West India Company established the colony in the 1670s. The Danes built Christiansfort (now Fort Christian, the oldest standing building in the USVI) and developed the harbor as a free port. St. Thomas became one of the busiest slave trading ports in the Caribbean.

The United States purchased the territory from Denmark in 1917 for $25 million, primarily to prevent Germany from acquiring it during World War One. The harbor continues to handle significant cruise ship traffic: Charlotte Amalie is one of the Caribbean's busiest cruise ports.

## Duty-free commerce

St. Thomas is a major duty-free shopping destination. The Main Street district in Charlotte Amalie is dense with jewelry, electronics, and luxury goods stores serving cruise passengers. The shopping rhythm is dictated by ship arrival and departure times: shops fill when a ship docks and empty when it sails.
