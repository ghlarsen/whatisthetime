Kharkiv is Ukraine's second-largest city, just 40 kilometers from the Russian border, and a major industrial and academic center that emerged from Soviet urban planning. It runs on Eastern European Time, the same timezone as [Kyiv](/kyiv): UTC+2 in winter, UTC+3 during summer daylight saving.

## from Soviet capital to Eastern European Time

The city was the capital of Soviet Ukraine from 1917 to 1934, when the capital moved to Kyiv. That early centrality meant Kharkiv developed a dense infrastructure of universities and research institutions during a period of intense industrial investment. Time there was Moscow Time during most of the Soviet period.

After Ukrainian independence in 1991, Kharkiv shifted to Eastern European Time, diverging from Moscow Time (UTC+3) in winter and matching it in summer. The difference is an hour in the coldest months, nothing in the warmest, a subtle but symbolically important distinction from the neighboring country.

At 50 degrees north, Kharkiv has meaningful seasonal light variation. December days run about 8 hours. June days run nearly 16. The harshest winter cold arrives around late January, long after the shortest day.

[9am in Kharkiv is 7am in London](/when/9am-kharkiv/london) in winter, matching the standard Eastern European offset.
