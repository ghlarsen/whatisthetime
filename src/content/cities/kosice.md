Košice is Slovakia's second city, the main urban center of the country's eastern half, sitting in a basin ringed by low hills close to the Hungarian and Ukrainian borders. It runs on Europe/Bratislava time: UTC+1 in winter (CET), UTC+2 in summer (CEST). DST transitions follow the EU schedule.

## easternmost Gothic cathedral and EU clock debates

The city has been central European for most of its history, moving between Hungarian, Habsburg, Czechoslovak, and finally Slovak administration. It was the first city in which the Košice Government Program was announced in 1945, laying out the postwar Czechoslovak political framework. The St. Elisabeth Cathedral, the easternmost Gothic cathedral in Central Europe, has dominated the main square since the 15th century.

At 48 degrees north, Košice has moderately significant seasonal light variation. June evenings stretch past 9pm. December afternoons end before 4pm. The city experiences more continental climate influence than western Slovakia, with colder winters and hotter summers.

EU member Slovakia observes the EU-standard DST transitions. The debate about abolishing daylight saving across Europe has been active, with the European Parliament voting in 2019 to end it. Implementation has stalled, leaving Košice continuing to change clocks twice a year.

[9am in Košice is 8am in London](/when/9am-kosice/london) in winter, one hour ahead of GMT.
