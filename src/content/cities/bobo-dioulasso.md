Bobo-Dioulasso is Burkina Faso's second-largest city and its cultural capital. It runs on Africa/Ouagadougou: UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. Burkina Faso does not observe daylight saving time. The country sits between 10 and 15 degrees north latitude, well within the tropics, where daylight variation is modest and solar noon stays close to 12pm throughout the year.

The name Bobo-Dioulasso means roughly "the home of the Bobo and Dioula people" in the local languages. The city is a place where multiple ethnic groups have historically coexisted, and it remains a center of cultural production and commerce for the broader West African region.

## West Africa's music city

Bobo-Dioulasso has a strong musical tradition. The balafon, a wooden xylophone central to West African music, is strongly associated with the region. The city has produced numerous prominent musicians and is known throughout the region for its cultural festivals and performance traditions. The SIAO, the International Arts and Crafts Fair, is held every two years in Ouagadougou but draws participants for whom Bobo-Dioulasso is a cultural anchor.

The railroad connecting Bobo-Dioulasso to Abidjan on the coast was historically central to the city's commercial importance. Trade goods moving between the landlocked interior and the Atlantic coast passed through here.

## UTC+0 across the Sahel

Burkina Faso's UTC+0 is shared with most of its neighbors: Mali, Niger, Ghana, and Ivory Coast all use UTC+0 or UTC+1. This regional consistency simplifies the cross-border trade that sustains the Sahel economy. [Ouagadougou](/ouagadougou), the capital, is on the same clock.

The [time difference between Bobo-Dioulasso and London](/when/9am-bobo-dioulasso/london) is zero in winter, one hour behind when the UK observes BST.
