Tbilisi sits in a valley where the Mtkvari River cuts through the South Caucasus mountains. The city is genuinely old, founded according to legend in the 5th century by King Vakhtang I of Iberia, who supposedly followed a wounded pheasant to a hot spring. The sulfuric baths still operate in the old town. The city hasn't entirely forgotten its origins.

[Georgia](/country/georgia) runs on UTC+4, permanently, with no daylight saving. This was not always the case. Georgia experimented with DST multiple times after independence from the Soviet Union, finally abandoning it in 2005 and settling into a stable, year-round UTC+4. The decision brought consistency after years of clock adjustments that confused residents and created administrative headaches.

## long dinners and elastic evening hours

The time creates a peculiar daily rhythm. At 41 degrees north, sunrise in June comes before 6am, giving the city genuinely long summer mornings. The famous Georgian hospitality, centered on long dinners (supras) that begin late and end later, runs on an elastic relationship with evening hours. The formal clock time matters less than the gathering itself.

[9am in Tbilisi is 5am in London](/when/9am-tbilisi/london). [Yerevan](/yerevan) in Armenia shares the UTC+4 clock.
