Brikama is the largest city in The Gambia by population, sitting in the Western Region just south of the capital Banjul. It runs on Africa/Banjul: UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. The Gambia does not observe daylight saving time.

The Gambia is one of Africa's smallest and most unusually shaped countries: a narrow strip of land averaging about 50 kilometers wide, stretching 350 kilometers inland along both banks of the Gambia River, entirely surrounded by Senegal except for its Atlantic coast. Despite this unusual geography, the entire country uses a single timezone: UTC+0.

## Population without capital status

Brikama is larger than Banjul, the official capital, but lacks the capital designation. This is partly historical: Banjul was the colonial administrative center, occupying a small island at the river mouth that was easier to defend and govern in the British colonial era. The urban sprawl of Greater Banjul has absorbed Brikama into an effective metropolitan area, but the two cities maintain separate administrative identities.

The city is known for its craft markets, particularly kora music instruments. Brikama has a strong tradition of griot musicians who maintain the oral histories and musical traditions of the Mandinka people.

## Senegal's timezone border

Senegal, which surrounds The Gambia, also uses UTC+0 (though the IANA identifier is Africa/Dakar). Crossing from Gambia into Senegal requires no timezone adjustment. The borders here are legacies of the colonial division between British and French West Africa, drawn along political rather than natural or cultural lines.

The [time difference between Brikama and London](/when/9am-brikama/london) is zero in winter, one hour behind when the UK observes BST. [Bununka Kunda](/bununka-kunda) is further up the Gambia River on the same clock.
