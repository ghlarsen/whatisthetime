Camayenne is a commune within the greater Conakry metropolitan area, the capital of Guinea. It runs on Africa/Conakry: UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. Guinea does not observe daylight saving time. The country sits on West Africa's Atlantic coast, and its longitude of approximately 13 to 15 degrees west would technically place it in UTC-1 territory, but the entire region uses UTC+0 for practical consistency.

## The Conakry peninsula

Conakry, the capital, occupies the Kaloum Peninsula projecting into the Atlantic. Camayenne and other communes extend northward along the coast and onto the adjacent mainland. The city is built on one of the world's densest peninsulas, with limited land and significant overcrowding in the city core.

Guinea's Atlantic coast position, combined with its proximity to the equator (around 10 degrees north), gives it consistent daylight year-round. Sunrise around 6:30am, sunset around 6:45pm. The weather distinction here is wet season versus dry season, not light versus dark.

## Bauxite and iron ore

Guinea holds some of the world's largest deposits of bauxite (used to make aluminum) and significant iron ore reserves. The country's mining economy connects it to global commodity markets that operate on UTC and New York, London, and Tokyo trading hours. Mining companies with operations in Guinea manage this timezone navigation constantly.

The UTC+0 offset makes this relatively straightforward: London is the same time in winter, one hour ahead in summer.

The [time difference between Camayenne and London](/when/9am-camayenne/london) is zero in winter, one hour behind when the UK observes BST.
