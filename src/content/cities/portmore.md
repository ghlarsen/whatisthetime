Portmore is technically separate from Kingston, but the two cities form one continuous urban sprawl across the Liguanea Plain. Portmore sits on a peninsula between Kingston Harbour and the Portmore Lagoon, connected to Kingston by the Causeway. Most of the people who live in Portmore work in Kingston. The morning commute across the Causeway is one of the defining daily rhythms of the region.

[Jamaica](/country/jamaica) runs on Eastern Standard Time, UTC-5, year-round with no daylight saving. This is the same offset as New York in winter. In summer, when the US East Coast advances to UTC-4, Jamaica stays behind by an hour. The island made this choice and has not revised it.

At 18 degrees north, Jamaica's daylight variation is modest. Sunrise in Kingston and Portmore comes between 5:45am and 6:30am. Sunset falls between 6pm and 7pm. The days feel consistent throughout the year, which is one of the pleasures of low-latitude living.

The Old Harbour Bay fishing community near Portmore heads out before dawn, as fishing communities everywhere tend to do. The fish market opens when the boats return, around 7am or 8am, and the rhythms of the sea assert themselves over any civil schedule.

[9am in Portmore is 2pm in London](/when/9am-portmore/london) in winter.
