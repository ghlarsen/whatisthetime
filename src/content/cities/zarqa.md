Zarqa is [Jordan](/country/jordan)'s second-largest city, sitting about 20 kilometers northeast of Amman in a flat industrial zone. The city grew rapidly in the 20th century around the oil refinery established there in the 1950s and a large military base, attracting Palestinian refugees and economic migrants who built a dense, workmanlike city.

Jordan keeps Arabia Standard Time, UTC+3 in winter, but observes summer daylight saving time moving to UTC+2. This is the opposite of most DST conventions: Jordan moves its clocks back in summer, creating UTC+2, rather than forward. The country has had an irregular DST history, with the government adjusting the dates and occasionally skipping the change in years when the economic disruption seemed unnecessary.

## industrial city and irregular DST history

At 32 degrees north, Zarqa gets meaningful but not extreme daylight variation. The summers are hot and dry; the winters mild by Northern European standards. The industrial character of the city means its pace is set by factory shifts and refinery schedules as much as by natural light.

Zarqa is often mentioned in regional security contexts: it was the hometown of Abu Musab al-Zarqawi, the founder of al-Qaeda in Iraq. The city's dense, economically marginal neighborhoods became well-known to security researchers after 2003 for reasons that have nothing to do with timekeeping.

[9am in Zarqa is 6am in London](/when/9am-zarqa/london). [Amman](/amman) nearby shares the same Jordanian timezone.
