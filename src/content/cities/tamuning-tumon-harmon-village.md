The combined designation of Tamuning-Tumon-Harmon Village covers [Guam](/country/guam)'s main tourism corridor: Tumon Bay, the island's primary beach resort area, flanked by Tamuning's commercial district and Harmon village. The area runs on Chamorro Standard Time (UTC+10) all year, with no daylight saving.

Tumon Bay is the visual and commercial heart of Guam's tourism industry. The crescent of hotels, beach clubs, and duty-free shopping that lines the bay serves primarily Japanese, Korean, and Chinese visitors for whom Guam is a short-haul tropical destination. The resort rhythm here is shaped by flight arrivals, check-in times, and the pattern of tours and beach activities, all organized within the fixed UTC+10 frame.

At UTC+10, Tumon Bay sees each new calendar day significantly before the Japanese and Korean visitors' home cities (both at UTC+9). The single hour difference is small, but it means that technically, guests arriving from Tokyo or Seoul in the evening are arriving into the next calendar day local time.

## wartime history and resort present

Guam was heavily fortified by Japan during World War II and liberated by US forces in August 1944, a campaign that took three weeks. The war is present in the landscape: Liberation Day is a public holiday, and the Guam War Survivors' Memorial is near the airport.

[Tamuning](/tamuning) nearby shares the same Chamorro Standard Time.

[9am here is 11pm in London](/when/9am-tamuning-tumon-harmon-village/london) the previous day in winter, 10pm during British Summer Time.
