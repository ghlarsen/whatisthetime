Mbuji-Mayi is one of the largest cities in Africa and the diamond mining capital of the [Democratic Republic of the Congo](/country/democratic-republic-of-the-congo), sitting in the Kasai-Oriental region in the center of the country. It runs on Central Africa Time, UTC+2, the eastern DRC timezone that differs by one hour from Kinshasa in the west.

## artisanal mining and daylight schedules

The diamond mining industry here is one of the world's largest, though much of it is artisanal small-scale mining. Hundreds of thousands of people work the alluvial diamond fields around the city, many informally. Mining schedules are shaped by daylight more than clock time: the workday in open-cast artisanal mines begins at dawn and ends when the light fails.

Mbuji-Mayi sits at about 6 degrees south latitude, near the equator, where daylight barely shifts across the year. The UTC+2 offset places it in the Central Africa Time zone shared with [Lubumbashi](/lubumbashi) to the southeast and the East African countries further east.

[9am in Mbuji-Mayi is 7am in London](/when/9am-mbuji-mayi/london) during European winter.
