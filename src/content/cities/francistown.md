Francistown, Botswana's second-largest city, uses `Africa/Gaborone`, UTC+2, year-round. Botswana has never observed daylight saving time. At 21 degrees south, the city sits in the subtropical zone where daylight variation is moderate (about 10.5 hours at winter solstice and 13.5 hours at summer solstice). The stable year-round clock suits the city's role as a regional commercial center.

Francistown was founded during the 1860s gold rush along the Tati River, making it one of southern Africa's oldest gold-mining settlements. Today it's a trade and transit hub, strategically located at the junction of roads running north to [Zambia](/zambia) and Zimbabwe, south to [Gaborone](/gaborone), and east toward Zimbabwe's Bulawayo. Cross-border trade, trucking, and logistics define its commercial identity.

All of Botswana, [Zimbabwe](/zimbabwe), and [Zambia](/zambia) are on UTC+2, though Zimbabwe calls it CAT and Zambia calls it CAT as well. The shared offset makes cross-border business straightforward in this corner of southern Africa. The clocks don't shift; the goods do.
