Sunch'ŏn is an industrial city in South Pyongan Province of [North Korea](/country/north-korea), known for its chemical fertilizer and vinalon fiber industries. The city keeps Pyongyang Time (UTC+9), matching South Korea and Japan across the border.

North Korea's timezone history is politically charged. In 2015, the country announced it was creating "Pyongyang Time" at UTC+8:30, advancing its clocks by 30 minutes to distinguish itself from Japan, which had imposed UTC+9 during its colonial rule of Korea. The announcement was framed as a liberation from Japanese temporal imperialism.

Then in 2018, ahead of the inter-Korean summit between Kim Jong-un and South Korean President Moon Jae-in, North Korea announced it would return to UTC+9 to synchronize with the South. The clock moved back 30 minutes as a symbolic gesture of rapprochement. Sunch'ŏn and all of North Korea shifted simultaneously.

## diplomatic theater and the clock

It is a rare example of a timezone being changed for explicitly diplomatic reasons. The 30 minutes existed briefly as an expression of political identity, then dissolved in a moment of diplomatic theater.

At 39 degrees north, Sunch'ŏn has real seasonal light variation: about 15 hours in June and about 9.5 hours in December.

[9am in Sunch'ŏn is 12am in London](/when/9am-sunch-on/london) in winter, 1am during British Summer Time.
