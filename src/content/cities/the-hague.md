The Hague is an anomaly. It is the seat of government of [The Netherlands](/country/the-netherlands) without being its capital. It hosts the International Court of Justice, the International Criminal Court, and dozens of other international bodies. The city's identity is built around international law, diplomacy, and the careful recording of events, dates, and times that legal proceedings demand.

The Hague keeps Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter and UTC+2 during summer DST, the same clock as [Amsterdam](/amsterdam) 50 kilometers north. The Netherlands famously ran on Amsterdam Time, UTC+0:19:32, until 1940, when the German occupation imposed Central European Time. After the war, the Netherlands kept CET despite the geographic oddity of being at roughly the same longitude as the UK.

## international justice and unambiguous timestamps

For a city full of international tribunals, timezone conversions are a daily operational matter. Hearings at the International Court of Justice list times carefully. Evidence from distant jurisdictions must be timestamped with timezone clarity. The machinery of international justice depends on unambiguous time.

The beach resort of Scheveningen, technically part of The Hague, runs on the same clock. The North Sea is indifferent to time zones.

[9am in The Hague is 8am in London](/when/9am-the-hague/london). [Rotterdam](/rotterdam) nearby shares the same clock.
