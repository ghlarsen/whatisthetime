Hrodna (also spelled Grodno) is in the far west of Belarus, at the borders with Poland and Lithuania. It runs on UTC+3 year-round, Belarus's permanent Further Eastern European Time, adopted in 2011.

This creates a striking situation: Hrodna is geographically further west than many Polish cities, yet its clock is two hours ahead of Warsaw in winter. Cross the border from Hrodna to Białystok, Poland, and the clock jumps back two hours in winter, one hour in summer.

## A multiethnic city on Europe's edge

Hrodna has historically been home to Belarusians, Poles, Jews, Lithuanians, and Tatars. The city passed between the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth, Russia, Germany (briefly in WWI), Poland (in the interwar period), and the Soviet Union. Each political era brought its own administrative timezone.

The Jewish community of Hrodna was largely destroyed in the Holocaust. The city's Jewish heritage is marked by a small museum and memorial.
