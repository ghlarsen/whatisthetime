West Island is the main administrative settlement of the Cocos (Keeling) Islands, an Australian external territory in the Indian Ocean about halfway between Australia and Sri Lanka. The territory consists of two atolls and about 600 residents, the majority of whom are Cocos Malay, descendants of workers brought to the islands in the 19th century to work coconut plantations.

The islands keep Cocos Islands Time, UTC+6:30, one of the world's rarer half-hour offset timezones. The [Cocos Islands](/country/cocos-islands) arrived at this offset through geographic pragmatism: the islands sit between UTC+6 (Bangladesh) and UTC+7 (Indonesia), and the half-hour split roughly corresponds to the islands' longitude.

The Cocos Malay community maintains a distinct cultural identity, language (a Malay creole), and calendar of Islamic festivals that runs in parallel to the civil one. Home Island, across the lagoon from West Island, is where the Cocos Malay community primarily lives.

The territory is administered from Christmas Island and ultimately from Canberra. For practical communication, the difference between Australian Eastern Time (UTC+8 or UTC+11) and the local UTC+6:30 is a constant factor in scheduling.

[9am on West Island is 2:30am in London](/when/9am-west-island/london). The nearest comparable timezone is [Yangon](/yangon) in Myanmar, also at UTC+6:30.
