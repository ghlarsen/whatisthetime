Cali is Colombia's third-largest city and the cultural capital of the Cauca Valley. Known globally as the salsa capital of the world, it runs on America/Bogota: UTC-5, Colombia Time, year-round. Colombia has never observed daylight saving time.

At roughly 3 degrees north latitude, Cali sits close enough to the equator that daylight is essentially constant: sunrise around 6am, sunset around 6:15pm throughout the year. There is no summer with late evenings and no winter with early darkness. The fixed UTC-5 clock matches this equatorial reality.

## The salsa clock

Cali's salsa music culture operates on a genuinely different clock from the rest of the city's formal life. The salsa schools (academias) are active from morning to late at night. Social dance events (chontaduros and salsedromes) start at night and run until 4am or 5am. The professional salsa competition circuit, the Cali style distinct from New York or LA style salsa, has made the city internationally famous among dancers.

This late-night culture coexists with Cali's industrial and commercial economy. The city is Colombia's main sugar-producing region and hosts chemical and pharmaceutical industries. The two rhythms, the industrial day shift and the dancing night shift, occupy the same clock without much friction.

## Cali's equatorial climate

At 1,000 meters elevation, Cali has a mild "eternal spring" climate similar to [Bogotá](/bogota), though warmer and drier. Two rainy seasons pass through the year, but temperatures remain between 16 and 28 degrees Celsius year-round. The absence of seasonal temperature variation, combined with consistent daylight, creates a clock environment where the seasons are defined by rain and festivals rather than light.

The [time conversion from Cali to London](/when/9am-cali/london) puts London five hours ahead in winter, six in summer when the UK observes BST.
