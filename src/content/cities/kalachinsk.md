Kalachinsk is a small city in Omsk Oblast, on the West Siberian Plain in Russia. It runs on Omsk Time, UTC+6, year-round. Russia abolished daylight saving in 2014, and Kalachinsk has kept a fixed clock since.

At UTC+6, Kalachinsk is 3 hours ahead of [Moscow](/moscow) and shares its offset with [Dhaka](/dhaka) and [Almaty](/almaty). The Trans-Siberian Railway passes through the Omsk region, and train schedules have historically been printed in Moscow time, creating a persistent gap between official railway time and local civil time.

## The West Siberian agricultural plain

The area around Kalachinsk is part of the West Siberian Plain, some of the most productive agricultural land in Siberia. The growing season is short but intense, concentrated in June and July. All farming activity compresses into a narrow window: planting must follow snowmelt, harvest must precede the first autumn frosts.

This agricultural urgency creates a seasonal time pressure that no clock governs. Farmers do not consult UTC+6 to decide when to harvest; they watch the weather and the grain.
