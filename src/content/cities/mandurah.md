Mandurah is a coastal city and the second-largest urban area in Western Australia, sitting about 75 kilometers south of [Perth](/perth) along the Indian Ocean. It runs on Australian Western Standard Time, UTC+8, the fixed clock of [Australia's](/country/australia) western state that applies year-round with no daylight saving.

## four referendums and a firm no

Western Australia held referendums on adopting daylight saving in 1974, 1984, 1992, and 2009. Every vote rejected it. The 2009 vote came after a three-year DST trial and still returned a decisive no, with 55% opposed. The reasons given include disruption to children's routines, farmers' schedules, and the already very late evenings in the long summer days. Perth and Mandurah sit at 32 degrees south, where December evenings don't naturally get dark until after 8pm.

Mandurah is known for its canals, the Peel-Harvey estuary system, and its dolphin population. The fishing and tourism economy pays attention to tides and daylight: those rhythms are meaningful in ways civil clock shifts would only complicate.

[Perth](/perth) to the north shares this clock. [9am in Mandurah is 1am in London](/when/9am-mandurah/london) during European winter, an eight-hour gap.
