Adelaide keeps a half-hour offset from its neighbors, and it has done so since 1899. South Australia runs on Central Standard Time, UTC+9:30 in winter, UTC+10:30 during daylight saving. This puts Adelaide perpetually 30 minutes off from Sydney and Melbourne, 30 minutes ahead of Darwin. For anyone scheduling calls between Australian cities, Adelaide is the wrinkle in the plan.

## Why the half-hour exists

The 30-minute offset dates to the early days of railway standardization in Australia. South Australian authorities chose a meridian-based time that fell between the eastern states and Western Australia. The logic was geographically sound: Adelaide sits roughly along the 138.5 degree east meridian, halfway between the 150-degree meridian underpinning eastern time and the 120-degree meridian underpinning Western Australian time. The half-hour compromise has survived ever since.

[Adelaide Hills](/adelaide-hills) to the east of the city shares the same timezone. The region rises quickly into cooler highland country but the clock doesn't change at the city boundary.

South Australia does observe daylight saving, unlike neighboring [Northern Territory](/country/australia). This means for part of the year Adelaide is only 30 minutes behind Sydney; for the rest, it drops to 1.5 hours behind.

[9am in Adelaide is 11:30pm in London](/when/9am-adelaide/london) the previous day during Australian summer.
