Fomboni is the capital of Mohéli (Mwali), the smallest island of the Comoros archipelago. The IANA timezone `Indian/Comoro` covers the whole archipelago: UTC+3, East Africa Time, year-round. No daylight saving has ever been observed.

The Comoros sit at 11-13 degrees south, close to Madagascar's northwestern coast. UTC+3 aligns the islands with the East African mainland, their primary trading and cultural partners. Solar noon in Fomboni falls around 12:05pm, making the clock a near-perfect solar match.

Mohéli is the least developed of the Comoros islands, with limited infrastructure and a small economy based on subsistence agriculture and fishing. Fomboni is a quiet town by any standard. The call to prayer from the town's mosque marks the day's structure more reliably than any clock; the fishing boats leave before dawn whenever the sea permits. The island's rhythm is natural and immediate, connected to tide, weather, and the ylang-ylang harvest rather than to UTC arithmetic.
