Nzérékoré sits in the deep forest zone of southeastern Guinea, at a three-country junction where Guinea, Liberia, and Côte d'Ivoire meet. The forest here is part of the Upper Guinea forest belt, one of West Africa's most biodiverse regions and a source of both timber and, historically, significant mineral wealth. The city is the regional capital for the Forested Guinea region.

[Guinea](/country/guinea) runs on Greenwich Mean Time, UTC+0, year-round with no daylight saving. The IANA identifier is Africa/Conakry, though Nzérékoré sits hundreds of kilometers from the coast and the capital. At 7.7 degrees north of the equator, Nzérékoré's daylight barely varies: sunrise around 6am to 6:30am, sunset around 6pm to 6:30pm year-round.

The forest climate shapes the daily schedule. The rainy season from May through October brings intense and prolonged rainfall that can make roads impassable. The dry season from November through April is drier but can bring haze from the harmattan. Most outdoor activity concentrates in the morning hours before midday heat and humidity peak.

## forest networks and the Ebola outbreak

The Ebola epidemic that began in the forest villages near Nzérékoré in late 2013 and spread to cause the largest outbreak in history was traced to a community in Guéckédou district nearby. The region's remoteness and cross-border forest networks shaped how the epidemic initially spread.
