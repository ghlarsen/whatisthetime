Bouaké is Ivory Coast's second-largest city and the commercial capital of the country's interior. It runs on Africa/Abidjan: UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. Ivory Coast does not observe daylight saving time.

The city sits in central Ivory Coast at about 7 degrees north latitude. The climate is tropical, with two wet seasons, and daylight is consistent throughout the year. The sun rises around 6am and sets between 6 and 7pm. The UTC+0 timezone aligns closely with solar noon for this longitude, making it an accurate clock in the most basic solar sense.

## Post-conflict recovery

Bouaké was the center of the rebel-held north during Ivory Coast's civil conflict from 2002 to 2011. The Forces Nouvelles insurgency held the city for nearly a decade, creating a political divide between the rebel north and the government-controlled south. During this period, Bouaké functioned under a parallel administration with its own rules and rhythms.

The peace process and reunification of the country brought Bouaké back under national governance. Post-conflict reconstruction investment has been significant, and the city has largely rebuilt its infrastructure. The Bouaké market, one of the largest in West Africa, has resumed its role as a regional trade hub connecting the Sahel to the coast.

## West Africa's shared clock

Bouaké shares Africa/Abidjan time with [Abidjan](/abidjan), with Ghana, with Mali, and with several other West African countries. The regional consistency makes cross-border commerce straightforward from a scheduling perspective.

The [time difference between Bouaké and London](/when/9am-bouake/london) is zero in winter, one hour behind when the UK observes BST.
