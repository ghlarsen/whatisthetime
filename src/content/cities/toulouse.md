Toulouse is called La Ville Rose for the warm pink brick of its older buildings. It sits in southwestern [France](/country/france), closer to Madrid than to Paris, and its climate and pace feel distinctly southern. The Canal du Midi runs through the region, and the Garonne River cuts through the city center.

France keeps Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter and UTC+2 during summer DST, a timezone that is geographically aggressive for Toulouse. The city sits at about 1 degree east longitude, almost exactly on the Greenwich meridian, which puts it solidly in the UK's natural timezone. France's use of CET is a historical artifact of German occupation in 1940, which imposed Central European Time on occupied France. Post-war France kept it.

The practical result is that solar noon in Toulouse falls around 1:30pm in winter, and sunsets in summer push past 10pm. French meal times, already late by northern European standards, accommodate this. Dinner at 8pm is considered early.

Toulouse is also the home of Airbus headquarters and the center of European aerospace. The engineers and executives coordinating with Hamburg, Madrid, and Toulouse across European timezone overlap have a practical framework that the shared CET makes simpler.

[9am in Toulouse is 8am in London](/when/9am-toulouse/london). [Bordeaux](/bordeaux) to the northwest and [Montpellier](/montpellier) to the east share the same Paris timezone.
