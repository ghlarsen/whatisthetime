Favoriten is the 10th Bezirk of Vienna, the largest by population in the Austrian capital. It shares `Europe/Vienna` with the rest of Austria: CET (UTC+1) in winter and CEST (UTC+2) in summer, following EU DST rules. The transitions happen on the last Sunday of March and October.

## a working-class district at the Balkans rail gateway

Historically a working-class district built around the Vienna Südbahnhof (now Wien Hauptbahnhof), Favoriten's rhythms were shaped by factory shifts and railway schedules. The rail connections that run south toward Graz, Ljubljana, and the Balkans still define the neighborhood's character as a place where people arrive from elsewhere and build lives.

Vienna sits at 48.2 degrees north, giving Favoriten meaningful daylight variation: about 15.5 hours of daylight at the summer solstice and 8.5 hours at the winter solstice. The long summer evenings fill the park at Reumannplatz and the street cafes on Favoritenstrasse. The short winter days compress the commercial hours and push social life indoors. CET/CEST is the shared framework that keeps Favoriten on the same schedule as [Vienna's](/vienna) concert halls and government ministries, even if its daily rhythms feel distinctly different.
