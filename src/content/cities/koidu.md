Koidu is the commercial center of Kono District, the heart of Sierra Leone's alluvial diamond fields. The city runs on Africa/Freetown time: GMT, UTC+0, all year. No daylight saving.

Like [Kenema](/kenema) further southwest, Koidu's economy is shaped by diamond trading and artisanal mining. The sorting houses and dealer offices keep early hours, when visibility is best for stone evaluation. Late morning and afternoon heat pushes outdoor work to its limits.

Sierra Leone's civil war (1991-2002) centered partly on control of the Kono diamond fields. "Blood diamonds" from this district became internationally known. The post-war period has brought more formalized mining regulations, though the informal economy remains large. Time in Koidu has always been measured against the value of what comes out of the ground rather than what the clock says.

At 8 degrees north, Koidu receives close to 12 hours of daylight year-round. The harmattan season from November to February brings dry, dusty air from the Sahara, reducing visibility and changing the character of the workday.

[Noon in Koidu is noon in London in winter](/when/12pm-koidu/london), GMT matching GMT exactly.
