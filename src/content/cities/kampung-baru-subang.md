Kampung Baru Subang is a town in Selangor, Malaysia, part of the Klang Valley megalopolis that stretches between Kuala Lumpur and the coast. It runs on Malaysia Standard Time, UTC+8, year-round. No daylight saving.

Like all of Malaysia, Kampung Baru Subang runs an hour ahead of solar time. The country chose UTC+8 in 1982 for its alignment with Singapore and the East Asian economic zone. Solar noon falls at about 1:20pm in the Klang Valley, noticeably late but accepted as the natural rhythm.

## The Klang Valley urban sprawl

Kampung Baru Subang is part of Malaysia's most densely urbanized corridor. Subang Jaya, Shah Alam, Petaling Jaya, and numerous other townships blend into a continuous urban fabric. The Sultan Abdul Aziz Shah Airport (Subang Airport) served as Kuala Lumpur's main airport until KLIA opened in 1998 and remains active for budget and chartered flights.

The town's rhythm is suburban commuter: morning departures by 7am to beat the highway traffic, evening returns varying by road conditions. The Klang Valley highway system connects these suburbs, and traffic timing governs daily schedules more practically than the clock.
