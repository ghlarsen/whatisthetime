Belo Horizonte was purpose-built as the capital of Minas Gerais state, inaugurated in 1897 as one of Brazil's first planned modern cities. Its grid streets and wide boulevards were drawn before the first resident arrived. The clock it runs on, Brasília Time at UTC-3, it shares with [São Paulo](/sao-paulo) and [Rio de Janeiro](/rio-de-janeiro), the two cities that anchor southeastern Brazil's economic and cultural weight.

Brazil's timezone history is tangled. For years the country observed daylight saving time from October to February, pushing clocks to UTC-2 for the southern summer. In 2019, President Bolsonaro abolished DST by decree, ending decades of biannual clock changes. Belo Horizonte, along with the rest of the country's southeast, has been on a fixed UTC-3 ever since.

## A city with opinions about clocks

The DST abolition was genuinely controversial in Minas Gerais. The agricultural sector, which drives much of the state's economy, had long complained that DST disrupted livestock and harvest schedules. Businesses in the financial sector, aligned with [São Paulo](/sao-paulo)'s markets, preferred the alignment with international trading hours that the extra summer daylight provided. The 2019 decree sided with the agricultural argument.

Belo Horizonte sits at roughly 20 degrees south latitude. Sunrise in winter falls around 6:30am, in summer around 5:30am. The long summer mornings the city gets naturally provide light early enough that DST's extra evening hour was always more about commercial alignment than practical need.

## Daily life and the mineiro clock

The people of Belo Horizonte, the mineiros, have a reputation across Brazil for moving at a deliberate pace: warm, social, unhurried. The city's bar culture is among the most famous in Brazil. Boteco bars stay open late, and weekday evenings routinely stretch past midnight. The fixed UTC-3 clock means sunset in summer arrives comfortably before 7pm, giving long evenings to the outdoor life the city is known for.

The [time difference between Belo Horizonte and London](/when/9am-belo-horizonte/london) is three hours in winter, four in summer when the UK observes BST.
