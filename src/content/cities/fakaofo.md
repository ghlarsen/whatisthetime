Fakaofo is one of Tokelau's three coral atolls, home to about 500 people. It uses `Pacific/Fakaofo`, UTC+13, year-round. Tokelau is a New Zealand territory, and in 2011, the territory made a timezone leap that has no equal in modern history: on December 29, 2011, Tokelau moved from UTC-11 to UTC+13, skipping December 30 entirely. The entire date ceased to exist on those atolls.

## skipping a day to sync with New Zealand

The reason was practical. Tokelau is administered from New Zealand and receives most of its supplies and communications from Samoa. Until 2011, Tokelau and New Zealand were 23 hours apart in the same direction, meaning that despite being close neighbors, they were almost a full day out of sync. By crossing the date line, the territory brought itself within 1 hour of New Zealand and within the same business week as its key partners.

Fakaofo sits at 9 degrees south and is barely above sea level, vulnerable to storm surges and sea-level rise. The atoll's residents, who number in the hundreds, have limited infrastructure and no airstrip. Supply ships bring food and fuel. When the calendar date shifted in 2011, it felt significant in an administrative sense; the residents had more pressing concerns about whether the sea would continue rising.
