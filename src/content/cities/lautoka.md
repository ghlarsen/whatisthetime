Lautoka is Fiji's second city, built around the sugar industry. The Lautoka Mill is the largest sugar mill in the Southern Hemisphere, and the city's entire layout, its straight streets, the railway that still moves cane during harvest season, reflects its industrial origin. The city runs on Pacific/Fiji time: UTC+12 in winter (May-October), UTC+13 during Fiji's daylight saving period (roughly November-January).

Fiji is one of the few Pacific Island nations that observes daylight saving. The government introduced it in 1998 to align Fiji's business hours more effectively with Australian eastern trading partners. The DST dates are set each year by government decree rather than following a fixed calendar, which can create uncertainty.

At 17 degrees south, Lautoka has moderate seasonal daylight variation. The sugar harvest season (June-November) coincides roughly with the southern hemisphere's cooler, drier months. The harvest determines the city's working rhythm more than any clock adjustment: the mills run day and night during crushing season, and the city adjusts around them.

UTC+12 places Fiji at the same clock as New Zealand in winter. During Fiji's DST (UTC+13), Fiji is actually one hour ahead of New Zealand's UTC+12, putting it among the furthest ahead clocks on earth.

[9am in Lautoka is 9pm in London the previous evening](/when/9am-lautoka/london) in Fijian winter, twelve hours ahead.
