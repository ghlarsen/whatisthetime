Sector 6 covers the western edge of Bucharest, [Romania](/country/romania)'s capital, including the Henri Coandă International Airport and large residential neighborhoods built largely during the communist era. Like the rest of the city, it runs on Eastern European Time: UTC+2 in winter, UTC+3 in summer.

The airport's presence makes Sector 6 a transit zone where timezone awareness matters. Flights arrive and depart on coordinated universal time, while passengers and staff operate on Eastern European Time. The gap between the two depends on the season: two hours in winter, three in summer.

Romania advances clocks on the last Sunday of March and returns on the last Sunday of October, in alignment with EU DST rules. Discussions about ending DST have circulated in the EU for years, with Romania engaged in those debates, but the changes have not been implemented.

Bucharest's western sector has developed significant commercial areas alongside its residential character, with major shopping centers and office parks serving the commuter populations.

[Sector 3](/sector-3) across the city on the eastern side keeps the same clock.

[9am in Sector 6 is 7am in London](/when/9am-sector-6/london) in winter, 6am during summer when both observe DST simultaneously.
