Gabú sits in eastern Guinea-Bissau, close to the borders with Guinea and Senegal. It uses `Africa/Bissau`, UTC+0, GMT, year-round. Guinea-Bissau has never observed daylight saving time. The country runs on the same clock as [Dakar](/dakar), [Conakry](/conakry), and [Freetown](/freetown), a shared GMT belt along the West African Atlantic coast and its hinterland.

At 12 degrees north, Gabú has sunrise between about 6:25am and 7:10am and sunset between 6:35pm and 7:15pm, with minimal seasonal variation. GMT is geographically reasonable: the town sits near 14.2 degrees west longitude, where UTC-1 might be slightly more solar-accurate, but the alignment with neighboring countries makes the shared clock practical.

Gabú is a market town at the crossroads of trade routes between Senegal, Guinea, and the Guinean coast. The town's weekly market is the largest in eastern Guinea-Bissau, drawing traders from all three countries. All three share the same UTC+0 clock, which simplifies the cross-border commerce considerably compared to regions where timezone boundaries cut across trade routes.
