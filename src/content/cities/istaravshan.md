Istaravshan is one of the oldest cities in Central Asia, in northern Tajikistan on the ancient Silk Road. It runs on Tajikistan Time, UTC+5, year-round, the same as [Dushanbe](/dushanbe) and all of Tajikistan.

The city was known as Cyropolis or Kyreschata in ancient sources, founded or refounded by Cyrus the Great of Persia around 539 BCE. Alexander the Great destroyed it, and it was rebuilt. The city has been Ura-Tyube under various rulers and was renamed Istaravshan after Tajikistan's independence.

## A bazaar city's market time

Istaravshan's historic center includes a traditional covered bazaar, one of the oldest in Central Asia. Bazaar time runs on market logic: vendors open early to capture morning buyers, trade peaks midmorning, and the bazaar quiets in the heat of the afternoon before reviving briefly in the evening. This rhythm predates any civil timezone by millennia.

Silk embroidery, traditional clothing, and metalwork are specialties of Istaravshan's craftsmen, who have passed their skills through apprenticeship systems that operate on their own schedule of years and mastery rather than clock time.

## The UTC+5 alignment

At UTC+5, Istaravshan shares its offset with [Islamabad](/islamabad), [Karachi](/karachi), and [Tashkent](/tashkent). When it is noon in Istaravshan, it is 7am in [London](/london).
