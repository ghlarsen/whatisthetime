Gbarnga is the capital of Bong County and one of Liberia's most significant interior towns. It runs on UTC+0, the same as [Monrovia](/monrovia) and the same as [London](/london) in winter. Liberia has no daylight saving time.

The city became widely known internationally during the Liberian Civil Wars of the 1990s, when it served as a base for Charles Taylor's National Patriotic Front. That history left marks on the physical city, and the years since have been a slow reconstruction.

## Interior Liberia's rhythm

Gbarnga sits in the forested interior, far from the coast. The market town rhythm here is governed more by weekly market days and the agricultural season than by the clock. Bong County's mining history, centered on the Bong Mines to the west, brought shift schedules and industrial time discipline to some residents, but the wider town moves to older patterns.

At 7 degrees North latitude, day length varies only modestly. Sunrise around 6:30am and sunset around 6:30pm year-round means UTC+0 works reasonably well as a solar time. Solar noon falls around 12:10pm.
