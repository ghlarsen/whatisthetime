Maputsoe sits on the Caledon River in northern [Lesotho](/country/lesotho), facing the South African town of Ficksburg directly across the water. A bridge connects them. Both towns run on UTC+2, South Africa Standard Time, a convenient alignment for the constant cross-border movement of people, goods, and workers.

Lesotho is entirely surrounded by South Africa, and its timezone follows accordingly. South Africa Standard Time, UTC+2, applies year-round with no daylight saving. The same clock that runs in Johannesburg and Cape Town runs in Maputsoe.

## textiles, trade agreements, and factory shifts

The town is primarily known as a manufacturing center, with textile factories established here in part due to trade agreements that gave Lesotho favorable access to US markets under AGOA (African Growth and Opportunity Act). Factory shift workers in Maputsoe keep industrial time aligned with supply chains running through South African ports.

[Maseru](/maseru), the national capital, shares this timezone. [9am in Maputsoe is 7am in London](/when/9am-maputsoe/london) during European winter.
