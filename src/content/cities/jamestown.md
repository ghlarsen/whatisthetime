Jamestown is the only town of Saint Helena, a British Overseas Territory in the South Atlantic Ocean, roughly 1,900 kilometers west of Angola. It runs on UTC+0, Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. No daylight saving.

Saint Helena is famous as Napoleon Bonaparte's place of exile. After his defeat at Waterloo in 1815, Napoleon was transported to this remote island, arriving in October 1815 and dying there in May 1821. The island was chosen precisely because of its isolation: no escape was possible.

## The most isolated capital on Earth

Before the RMS St Helena, the supply ship that serviced the island for decades, the only way to reach Jamestown was by sea, a journey of several days from the nearest mainland. The island opened its first airport in 2016, though the strong winds make landings challenging.

This isolation shaped Jamestown's relationship with time. The island knew the date from ship arrivals. News traveled weeks behind events. The idea of synchronizing with the outside world was aspirational rather than immediate.

## Napoleon's clock

Napoleon on Saint Helena was fastidious about time. He kept a precise daily schedule: rising early, working, receiving visitors, riding, dining. His clocks were set by the ship's chronometers that had brought him from Europe. Staying on schedule was one of the few things he could control.

The house he lived in, Longwood House, is now a French property maintained by the French government.
