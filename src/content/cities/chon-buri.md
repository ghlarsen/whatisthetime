Chon Buri is the capital of Chon Buri Province on Thailand's Eastern Seaboard, about 80 kilometers southeast of Bangkok. It runs on Asia/Bangkok: UTC+7, Indochina Time, year-round. Thailand has not observed daylight saving time since 1920.

At 13 degrees north, Chon Buri has consistent tropical daylight year-round. Sunrise falls between 5:45am and 6:30am. Sunset between 6pm and 7pm. The fixed UTC+7 aligns solar noon close to 12:30pm at this longitude.

## The Eastern Economic Corridor

Chon Buri Province is the heart of Thailand's Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC), one of Southeast Asia's most ambitious industrial development zones. The province hosts the Map Ta Phut industrial estate, one of Asia's largest petrochemical complexes, along with automotive assembly plants, electronics manufacturers, and the Laem Chabang deep-sea port, Thailand's main container terminal.

This industrial concentration means Chon Buri operates on international supply chain schedules. The port coordinates with shipping lines running on UTC. Factories producing electronics, vehicles, and chemicals for global markets keep schedules tied to delivery windows in Europe, North America, and Northeast Asia.

## Pattaya's 24-hour rhythm

Pattaya, within Chon Buri Province along the coast, is one of Southeast Asia's most active beach resort cities, known for a tourism and entertainment economy that runs around the clock. The beach resorts, bars, and restaurants on the beachfront operate on a 24-hour service model during peak season. The contrast between Pattaya's nighttime economy and the industrial estates' morning shifts is one of the sharper time culture contrasts within a single province.

The [time conversion from Chon Buri to London](/when/9am-chon-buri/london) puts London seven hours behind in winter, six in summer when the UK observes BST.
