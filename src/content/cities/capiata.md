Capiatá is one of the most populous municipalities in Greater Asunción, Paraguay's capital region. It runs on America/Asuncion: UTC-4 in winter (April to October), UTC-3 in summer (October to April). Paraguay observes daylight saving time, making it one of the few South American countries that consistently does so. DST begins the first Sunday in October and ends the last Sunday in March.

Paraguay's DST creates a distinctive situation: during its summer (Southern Hemisphere summer, October to March), Paraguay is at UTC-3, the same as [Buenos Aires](/buenos-aires). During its winter, it drops to UTC-4. Argentina does not observe DST, so the two countries sharing a long border move in and out of timezone alignment twice a year.

## A suburb without its own fame

Capiatá is a residential and industrial suburb that has grown substantially as Asunción's metropolitan area expanded. Like many peri-urban areas in South America, it developed rapidly in the late twentieth century as internal migration from rural Paraguay filled the capital region. The city has its own municipality but is functionally integrated into Greater Asunción's urban system.

## Paraguay's timezone logic

Paraguay borders Brazil to the north and east (which uses multiple timezones including UTC-3 and UTC-4) and Argentina and Bolivia to the south and west. The DST observation allows Paraguay to maintain better alignment with the Brazilian markets that drive much of its commerce during the Southern Hemisphere summer, which is also the peak agricultural and commercial season.

The [time conversion from Capiatá to London](/when/9am-capiata/london) varies: four hours behind in winter, three in summer.
