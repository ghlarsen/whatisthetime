Celje is Slovenia's third-largest city, located in the Savinja Valley in eastern Slovenia. It runs on Europe/Ljubljana: Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter, UTC+2 in summer, following EU DST schedules. The rules are identical to those of most Central European countries.

Celje sits at 46 degrees north latitude. Summer sunsets arrive around 9pm. December afternoons are dark before 4pm. The Alpine and sub-Alpine landscape around the city amplifies the seasonal light changes: winter days in the valley feel particularly short when the surrounding hills cut off direct sunlight even before the horizon.

## A medieval powerhouse

Celje was the center of the County of Celje, a medieval state that at its peak in the early fifteenth century controlled territory across Slovenia, Croatia, and parts of Austria and Hungary. The counts of Celje were among the most powerful nobles in Central Europe and nearly achieved royal status before the line died out in 1456. The ruins of their castle still dominate the hill above the modern city.

The city's Latin motto, "Semper Maior" (Always Greater), dates from this period of ambition. The historic buildings in the old town reflect its medieval commercial importance as a trade junction between the Alpine regions and the Pannonian plain.

## Industrial heritage

Celje was also a center of industrial development in the Yugoslav era. Enameled goods, electrical products, and chemical manufacturing made the city economically significant. The industrial legacy has left environmental cleanup challenges, but also a working-class cultural identity distinct from Ljubljana's administrative and university culture.
