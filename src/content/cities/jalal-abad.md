Jalal-Abad is a city in southwestern Kyrgyzstan, in the Fergana Valley, near the borders with Uzbekistan and Tajikistan. It runs on Kyrgyzstan Time, UTC+6, year-round. Kyrgyzstan abolished daylight saving in 2005.

The Fergana Valley is one of Central Asia's most complex border regions. Jalal-Abad sits near multiple border crossings. Uzbekistan, directly across the border, uses UTC+5, meaning a short trip into Uzbekistan means losing an hour.

## The Osh-Jalal-Abad axis

Jalal-Abad and [Osh](/osh) are the two major cities of southern Kyrgyzstan, a region with a large Uzbek minority population. The area saw serious ethnic violence in 2010. The intermingling of Kyrgyz and Uzbek communities, across a timezone boundary, makes the clock difference felt daily by families with relatives on both sides.

## Walnut forests and the harvest calendar

Jalal-Abad is famous for its ancient walnut forests, among the largest in the world, which UNESCO has recognized as part of a biodiversity hotspot. The walnut harvest in autumn is a community event that brings families to the forest for weeks. This seasonal calendar, tied to the nut harvest, structures the year for many rural residents more than any civil schedule.
