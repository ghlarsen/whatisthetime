Mar del Plata is Argentina's premier beach resort, sitting on the Atlantic coast 400 kilometers south of Buenos Aires. It runs on Argentina Time, UTC-3, the same fixed offset as [Buenos Aires](/buenos-aires) and most of [Argentina](/country/argentina), with no daylight saving.

Argentina's current fixed timezone is a direct result of its confused DST history. The country experimented with daylight saving multiple times across the twentieth century, with different provinces sometimes observing it differently. In 2008, the national government eliminated DST entirely, fixing the country at UTC-3.

## a city that lives for summer

Mar del Plata's temporal identity is defined by its summer season. January and February bring millions of porteños (Buenos Aires residents) to its beaches. The summer schedule runs late: dinner after 10pm, clubs open past midnight, the beach in the early morning before the heat. The long southern summer evenings, where it stays light until 8pm without any clock adjustment, support this culture naturally.

Off-season, the city quiets dramatically. The same streets that are jammed in January are empty in July.

[9am in Mar del Plata is 12pm in London](/when/9am-mar-del-plata/london) during European winter. Argentina's UTC-3 puts it three hours behind London on standard time.
