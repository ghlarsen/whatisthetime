Taichung sits in the center of [Taiwan](/country/taiwan), in the wide coastal plain between the central mountain range and the Taiwan Strait. It runs on National Standard Time (UTC+8) all year. Taiwan suspended daylight saving time in 1979 and has not observed it since.

The city is known for its relatively mild climate compared to Taipei's heat and humidity, its cultural institutions including the National Museum of Natural Science, and its role as a manufacturing base for the island's machine tool and bicycle industries. Taiwan is a global leader in bicycle manufacturing, and Taichung is its center, home to Giant and Merida among others.

At 24 degrees north, Taichung has moderate seasonal variation: about 13.5 hours of daylight in June, about 10.5 in December. The fixed UTC+8 means the clock aligns well with solar time for most of the island.

Taiwan's timezone designation in the IANA database is `Asia/Taipei`, reflecting the political sensitivities around Taiwan's status. The offset (UTC+8) is the same as China, but Taiwan's relationship with China Standard Time is not one of alignment: it is purely coincidental numerical agreement.

[Taipei](/taipei) to the north keeps the same National Standard Time.

[9am in Taichung is 1am in London](/when/9am-taichung/london) in winter, 2am during British Summer Time.
