Adamstown is the capital of Pitcairn Island, and saying it is a small town is an understatement. Fewer than 50 people live here, descendants of the HMS Bounty mutineers and their Tahitian companions who settled the island in 1790. There are no hotels. There is no airport. The only way in is by ship.

The island runs on UTC-8, year-round, no daylight saving. This offset is unusual. Pitcairn is geographically in the central Pacific, roughly in line with the International Date Line, but it uses UTC-8 rather than a positive UTC offset. This makes Adamstown one of the few places on Earth where the clock and the sun are genuinely misaligned in an administrative sense.

The IANA identifier is Pacific/Pitcairn. There are no other cities in that timezone group. Adamstown is, in timezone database terms, alone.

Time has a different quality on an island with under 50 residents. Supply ships arrive roughly every three months. There is no rush hour. The rhythms of the settlement are tied to boat schedules, fishing tides, and the amateur radio transmissions the islanders use to communicate with the outside world.

[9am in Adamstown is 5pm in London](/when/9am-adamstown/london), an 8-hour gap that reflects just how far removed this tiny capital is from the centers of global time.
