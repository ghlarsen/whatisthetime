Atikokan is a small mining town in northwestern Ontario with a population of around 3,000 people, and it has something no other Canadian municipality can claim: its own IANA timezone entry. America/Atikokan exists specifically because Atikokan observes Eastern Standard Time, UTC-5, year-round without daylight saving.

When the rest of Ontario springs forward in March and moves to UTC-4, Atikokan stays at UTC-5. For most of the year, Atikokan is on the same clock as Chicago and Winnipeg, which are in the Central Time zone. During North American summer, Atikokan is in practice in the same time as Chicago, even though it is formally classified under Eastern Time.

The town sits in the Canadian Shield near the Quetico Provincial Park wilderness area. It was founded around an iron ore mine that closed in the 1970s. The town has since pivoted to forestry, tourism, and a wood pellet manufacturing industry.

## How Atikokan got its own timezone

Atikokan is geographically in the Central time zone's territory, but historically aligned with Eastern Time due to its administrative connections to Ontario. When Ontario adopted DST, Atikokan chose not to follow. The result: its own timezone entry in the IANA database, making it one of the smallest places with that distinction.
