Russeifa is one of Jordan's larger industrial cities, located in the Balqa Governorate north of Amman, in the phosphate-rich zone that forms a significant part of Jordan's export economy. The city is close enough to the capital to function as an extended Amman suburb while maintaining its own industrial character.

[Jordan](/country/jordan) uses Arabia Standard Time but with an important difference from neighboring countries: Jordan observes daylight saving time. The clocks move to UTC+3 in summer (EEST) and back to UTC+2 in winter (EET). Saudi Arabia and most Gulf states do not change their clocks. This creates seasonal offset differences between Jordan and its neighbors.

At 32 degrees north, Russeifa has a Mediterranean-influenced climate with hot dry summers and cooler, occasionally rainy winters. The Jordan Valley nearby has a different microclimate: lower elevation, hotter in summer, mild in winter.

## phosphate industry and industrial time

The phosphate mines around Russeifa and the broader Zarqa governorate are visible from the main road: terraced cuts in the desert hillside, conveyor belts, processing facilities. Jordan is one of the world's leading phosphate exporters. The mines run on industrial time, which pays no particular attention to the clock on the wall.

[9am in Russeifa is 7am in London](/when/9am-russeifa/london) in winter.
