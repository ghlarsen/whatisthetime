Borama is a city in Somaliland's Awdal region, near the borders with Ethiopia and Djibouti. It runs on Africa/Mogadishu: UTC+3, East Africa Time, year-round. No daylight saving time is observed. Somaliland, which has governed itself separately from Somalia since 1991, uses the same timezone as the rest of Somalia and the broader East Africa region.

At about 10 degrees north latitude, Borama has consistent tropical daylight. The sun rises around 6am and sets around 6:30pm, with only small seasonal variations.

## Border trade hub

Borama's position near two international borders gives it commercial significance beyond its population size. The Ethiopia-Somaliland border at Doqoshiis (near Tog Wajaale) is a busy crossing for trade goods. Ethiopian merchants and Somaliland traders exchange goods that circulate through the broader Horn of Africa economy. Cattle and livestock, which are Somaliland's primary exports, often transit through the Awdal region.

The city has historically been home to an educated class. The Amoud University, founded in 1998, was one of the first universities established in Somaliland after the civil war, and it has become a modest but functioning institution of higher education.

## Khat and the morning clock

Khat, the mild stimulant leaf chewed widely in the Horn of Africa and Yemen, arrives fresh every morning from Ethiopia by truck. The delivery schedule drives a specific morning time rhythm in Borama: the khat truck arrives, the markets open, and social activity around the fresh khat begins. This daily cycle, pegged to agricultural harvest times in highland Ethiopia, operates within but somewhat independently of the UTC+3 clock.

The [time difference between Borama and London](/when/9am-borama/london) is three hours in winter, two in summer when the UK observes BST. [Berbera](/berbera) to the east is on the same clock.
