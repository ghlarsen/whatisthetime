Kananga is the capital of Kasaï-Central province in the Democratic Republic of Congo, on the central Kasaï Plateau. It runs on Central Africa Time, UTC+2, year-round. No daylight saving.

The DRC is one of Africa's largest countries and uses two timezones: UTC+1 for the western province around Kinshasa (Africa/Kinshasa), and UTC+2 for the rest of the country (Africa/Lubumbashi). Kananga falls in the UTC+2 zone.

## The diamond region

Kananga sits near the Kasaï diamond-mining region, one of the most significant in the world. The diamond economy brings a layer of industrial time discipline: mine operations, security schedules, and export logistics run on precise schedules that connect Kananga to the global diamond trade centered in [Antwerp](/antwerp) and [Dubai](/dubai).

## Civil conflict and disrupted time

The Kasaï region experienced significant conflict and displacement from 2016 to 2018, when militia violence and government counteroperations displaced hundreds of thousands of people. Civil administration, market operations, and social routines were disrupted. Rebuilding has required re-establishing the basic structures that organize civil time: market days, school schedules, government office hours.

At 5.9 degrees South latitude, day length varies modestly. Solar noon falls at about 12:15pm at UTC+2, a reasonable alignment for the region's longitude.
