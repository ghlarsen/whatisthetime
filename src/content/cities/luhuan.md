Luhuan is a district within [Macao](/macau), the former Portuguese territory that returned to Chinese administration in 1999. Like the rest of Macao, it runs on China Standard Time, UTC+8, no daylight saving.

Macao sits at the western edge of the Pearl River Delta, geographically a few degrees west of where UTC+8 would be the natural solar time. The sun rises and sets noticeably later by the clock than geography alone would suggest. But China's single nationwide timezone, running from the western deserts to the eastern coast, means every part of the country, including Macao's special administrative region, reads the same time.

## China's single timezone meets Macao's casino economy

The city's casino economy runs twenty-four hours, which makes timezone distinctions less relevant than the table games schedule. High-rollers arriving from [Hong Kong](/hong-kong), just across the water and sharing the same clock, move between cities without adjusting their watches.

[9am in Luhuan is 1am in London](/when/9am-luhuan/london). The significant offset reflects Macao's position on the far side of Eurasia from Europe.
