Sena Madureira sits in the state of Acre in far western [Brazil](/country/brazil), on the Iaco River in the depths of the Amazon basin. It runs on Acre Time (UTC-5), the westernmost timezone in Brazil. Acre is geographically close to Peru and Bolivia, and its UTC-5 offset places it two hours behind Brasília Time (UTC-3).

Acre has its own distinctive timezone story. The state used to be on UTC-5 historically, moved to UTC-4 in the early 2000s in an attempt to align with the Brazilian east, then returned to UTC-5 in 2008 after residents and businesses found the alignment more disruptive than the difference. The population simply preferred to match local solar time.

Brazil abolished daylight saving time in 2019. Acre, which was already exempt from DST as part of the western timezone zone, was not directly affected, but the abolition removed the complicated seasonal arithmetic that made Acre sometimes two hours behind Brasília and sometimes the same.

Sena Madureira is a small Amazonian town, connected to the state capital Rio Branco by road and river. The rhythm of life here follows the river, the forest, and the seasons of the Amazon more than any clock.

[9am in Sena Madureira is 2pm in London](/when/9am-sena-madureira/london) in winter, 3pm during British Summer Time.
