Burgas is Bulgaria's main Black Sea port and the gateway to the country's summer resort coast. It runs on Europe/Sofia: UTC+2 in winter (Eastern European Time), UTC+3 in summer (Eastern European Summer Time), following EU DST schedules.

Burgas sits at 42 degrees north on the Black Sea coast. The city has a maritime climate moderated by the sea, with milder winters than the Bulgarian interior and warm, dry summers. In June, sunset arrives after 9pm. In December, darkness falls before 5pm.

## Black Sea gateway

Bulgaria's resort coast, with its famous beach resorts at Sunny Beach (Slanchev Bryag), Nesebar, and Sozopol, depends on Burgas as its main transport hub. International charter flights bring tourists from Northern and Western Europe throughout the summer season. The airport at Burgas becomes one of the busiest in southeastern Europe during July and August.

The Black Sea has always been a zone of cultural and commercial exchange. Ancient Greek colonies established settlements on this coast before 600 BCE. The archaeological sites around Sozopol and the UNESCO-listed old town of Nesebar are evidence of millennia of continuous habitation.

## Eastern EU timezone

Burgas is at the eastern edge of the European Union's eastern timezone zone. Turkey, directly to the south and east, uses UTC+3 year-round (no DST). This means that in winter, Burgas on UTC+2 is one hour behind the Turkish coast visible across the Black Sea. In summer, both use UTC+3. The EU's DST schedule creates this seasonal timezone flip with immediate neighbors.

The [time conversion from Burgas to London](/when/9am-burgas/london) puts Burgas two hours ahead in winter, one ahead in summer. [Bucharest](/bucharest) is on the same clock.
