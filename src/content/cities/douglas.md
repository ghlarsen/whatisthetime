Douglas, the Isle of Man's capital, runs on `Europe/Isle_of_Man`, which tracks GMT (UTC+0) in winter and British Summer Time (BST, UTC+1) in summer. The transitions follow the same dates as the UK. Despite this, the Isle of Man has its own IANA entry rather than sharing `Europe/London`. The island is a British Crown Dependency, not part of the United Kingdom, with its own government (Tynwald, one of the oldest parliaments in the world) and its own legislative independence, but it has always followed UK time conventions.

At 54 degrees north, Douglas has meaningful daylight variation. Summer solstice brings sunrise around 4:30am and sunset after 10pm. The famous Isle of Man TT motorcycle races, held in May-June, take advantage of the long late-spring evenings. Riders go out in conditions that would be impossible a few months earlier.

Douglas is a financial center, with the island's favorable tax laws attracting international banking and insurance business. The working day in Douglas runs parallel to London's, aligned by a shared timezone. Morning calls to Frankfurt and Luxembourg are two hours behind; calls to New York are five to six hours behind depending on DST. The Isle of Man's clock is London's clock, even if the island itself is not London.
