Chipata is the capital of Zambia's Eastern Province, sitting near the Malawi border in the fertile Luangwa Valley watershed. It runs on Africa/Lusaka: UTC+2, Central Africa Time, year-round. No daylight saving time is observed.

Chipata is at roughly 13 degrees south latitude, on the plateau above the Luangwa Valley. The plateau elevation (1,200 meters) gives it a more temperate climate than the hot valley floor. Daylight varies moderately through the year: June (winter) brings shorter days with sunset before 6pm; December (summer) extends daylight past 6:30pm.

## The border and the crossing

The Malawi border crossing at Mwami, just east of Chipata, is one of Zambia's busiest land borders. Malawi uses UTC+2 as well, so crossing the border requires no timezone adjustment. The regional consistency across Zambia, Malawi, and Zimbabwe simplifies commerce for the traders who cross these borders regularly.

The Great East Road connecting Lusaka to Chipata and onward to Malawi passes through Chipata as the main eastern trade artery. Cotton, tobacco, and smallholder agricultural goods move along this road to markets in Lusaka and to export through Dar es Salaam.

## South Luangwa

Chipata is the primary access point for South Luangwa National Park, one of Africa's finest wildlife reserves. The park is known for its high density of leopards and for its tradition of walking safaris. Safari schedules are governed by light: early morning game drives start before 6am to catch the golden light and active wildlife; afternoon drives extend until last light around 6pm.

The [time difference between Chipata and London](/when/9am-chipata/london) is two hours in winter, one in summer when the UK observes BST. [Blantyre](/blantyre) in Malawi is on the same clock.
