Madīnat Ḩamad is one of Bahrain's planned residential cities, built in the 1980s as part of the kingdom's urban development program. Like all of [Bahrain](/country/bahrain), it runs on Arabia Standard Time, UTC+3, with no daylight saving.

## Gulf alignment and the King Fahd Causeway clock

Bahrain is a small archipelago in the Persian Gulf, and its timezone sits at UTC+3 despite a geographic longitude that would put natural solar noon somewhat earlier. The offset reflects regional alignment with Saudi Arabia and the Gulf states rather than strict geographic calculation.

The island kingdom is connected to Saudi Arabia by the King Fahd Causeway, and the shared timezone makes commercial and social movement across that link seamless. Bahrain's financial sector and Formula 1 circuit both operate on the same clock as their primary Gulf partners.

[Manama](/manama), Bahrain's capital, shares this timezone. [9am in Madīnat Ḩamad is 6am in London](/when/9am-madinat-hamad/london) in European winter. The three-hour difference is stable year-round, which makes scheduling consistent for businesses working across the Gulf and Europe.
