Lesotho is entirely surrounded by South Africa, and its time follows accordingly. [Mafeteng](/country/lesotho) runs on South Africa Standard Time, UTC+2, the same as [Maseru](/maseru) to the north and every South African city around it. There is no daylight saving.

Mafeteng is a district capital in the southern lowlands, sitting at about 1,700 meters, modest altitude by Lesotho standards where much of the country sits above 2,000 meters. The name in Sesotho means "the place of Fete's people," referring to the son of a nineteenth-century Basotho chief.

The town is primarily agricultural, and the farming calendar is shaped by the southern hemisphere seasons: planting in October, harvest in April. The clock doesn't adjust for seasons here, but the daily work schedule, especially outdoor labor, adapts to sunrise and sunset which shift by about two hours across the year at this latitude.

[Maseru](/maseru) shares the same timezone. [9am in Mafeteng is 7am in London](/when/9am-mafeteng/london) during European winter.
