Calliaqua is a coastal town on the southern end of Saint Vincent, in the nation of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. It runs on America/St_Vincent: UTC-4, Atlantic Standard Time, year-round. No daylight saving time is observed.

Saint Vincent and the Grenadines is a small island nation in the Eastern Caribbean, with Saint Vincent as the main island and a chain of smaller islands and cays extending south toward Grenada. The country uses a fixed UTC-4 offset that is standard across the Eastern Caribbean islands.

## Near the Soufrière

Calliaqua sits in the relatively safer southern part of Saint Vincent, away from the La Soufrière volcano in the north. La Soufrière erupted dramatically in April 2021, forcing the evacuation of tens of thousands of people from the northern zones. Calliaqua and the south became receiving areas for evacuees. The eruption was the worst in four decades and caused significant damage to the island's agriculture and infrastructure.

Natural events like volcanic eruptions and hurricanes define time in the Eastern Caribbean in a way that goes beyond clocks. The hurricane season, June through November, imposes a calendar of risk and preparation on island life.

## Caribbean time

UTC-4 is the standard for much of the Eastern Caribbean. [Bridgetown](/bridgetown) in Barbados, [Castries](/castries) in Saint Lucia, and [Brades](/brades) in Montserrat all use the same offset. The consistency simplifies transport, communication, and commerce across the island chain.

At 13 degrees north, Calliaqua has predictable, consistent daylight. Sunrise falls between 5:30am and 6:30am throughout the year. The equatorial proximity keeps the seasons defined by rain and hurricanes rather than light.

The [time difference between Calliaqua and London](/when/9am-calliaqua/london) is four hours behind in winter, five in summer when the UK observes BST.
