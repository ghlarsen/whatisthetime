Al Rayyan is Qatar's second-largest municipality and effectively the western suburban and industrial continuation of Doha. The city gained global visibility during the 2022 FIFA World Cup when several matches were played at Ahmad Bin Ali Stadium, located here. That tournament was notable partly because of Qatar's timezone: with kick-off times set for evening in Qatar (UTC+3), European viewers were watching games starting at 8pm, 10pm, or even midnight their time.

The clock is Arabia Standard Time, UTC+3. The IANA identifier is Asia/Qatar. No daylight saving. Qatar has kept a stable clock for decades, part of a Gulf-wide pattern of fixed time.

Al Rayyan's landscape is characteristically Qatari: low-rise commercial strips, residential compounds, industrial zones, and the occasional dramatic architecture of a sports facility or government building. The city is automobile-dependent, with distances and heat making walking impractical for much of the year.

[Al Mansourah](/al-mansurah) and [Al Matar al Atiq](/al-matar-al-atiq) in greater Doha share the same timezone, as does all of Qatar.

The 2022 World Cup required significant logistical coordination between Qatar's UTC+3 and the broadcasting schedules of over 200 countries. The unusual scheduling of the tournament in November-December rather than the traditional June-July was itself partly a concession to Qatar's extreme summer heat.

[9am in Al Rayyan is 6am in London](/when/9am-ar-rayyan/london) during European winter.
