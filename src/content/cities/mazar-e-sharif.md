Mazār-e Sharīf is the largest city in northern Afghanistan, sitting near the Uzbekistan border and historically a major trade and cultural center. It runs on Afghanistan Time, UTC+4:30, the unusual half-hour offset that applies across all of [Afghanistan](/country/afghanistan) with no daylight saving.

The city is the site of the Shrine of Hazrat Ali (Blue Mosque), one of the holiest sites in Islam for Shia Muslims and a major pilgrimage destination. The daily schedule here, like in all Afghan cities, is structured as much by the five prayer times as by the civil clock. The adhan from the Blue Mosque sets the day's rhythm.

Afghanistan's UTC+4:30 offset sits between Iran (UTC+3:30) and Pakistan (UTC+5), a half-hour increment that reflects historical timekeeping rather than alignment with neighbors. The offset has remained unchanged since its adoption, making Afghanistan consistently offset from its neighbors' whole-hour clocks.

The city has been contested territory across multiple modern wars, including the Soviet-Afghan war, the civil war of the 1990s, the NATO-era conflict, and the Taliban's return to power in 2021.
