Guayaquil is Ecuador's largest city and its main port, sitting at the mouth of the Guayas River near the Pacific coast. It runs on Ecuador Time, UTC-5, year-round. No daylight saving. Ecuador sits straddling the equator, and the minimal variation in day length across the year makes clock changes unnecessary and arbitrary.

Note that the Galapagos Islands, Ecuador's famous archipelago 1,000 kilometers offshore, use a different timezone: UTC-6. The mainland and the islands diverge by an hour.

## Port time and Pacific trade

Guayaquil's port is one of the most active on South America's Pacific coast, handling Ecuador's exports of shrimp, bananas, and cacao. Port operations run continuously. Container terminals, crane schedules, and customs processing all operate on international shipping time. The port's clock is synchronized to UTC for international vessel scheduling even as the city runs on UTC-5.

## At the equator, every day is the same length

Guayaquil sits at 2 degrees South latitude. Day length is nearly constant year-round: about 12 hours and 5 minutes of daylight every day. Solar noon falls at around 12:15pm by the clock, very close to UTC-5's standard position. This is as natural a timezone alignment as exists anywhere on Earth.
