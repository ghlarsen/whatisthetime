Taipa is one of the main islands of [Macao](/country/macao), connected to the Macao peninsula by bridges and to Cotai (the reclaimed land between Taipa and Coloane) by the Cotai Strip. The island runs on China Standard Time (UTC+8) all year, the same offset as the rest of the territory and neighboring mainland China.

Taipa was once a quiet fishing and agriculture island, known for its pipa manufacturing (a Chinese lute), its temples, and a colonial Portugese village. Much of that village survives, a UNESCO-listed area of yellow and white colonial houses on a quiet hill above the new development.

## Cotai Strip and the continuous casino clock

The new development is considerable. The Cotai Strip, built on reclaimed land adjacent to Taipa, is one of the most concentrated zones of casino resort development in the world, modeled in part on Las Vegas but on a scale that rivals or exceeds it. The gaming floors operate continuously, indifferent to the UTC+8 clock's relationship with sunrise or sunset.

Taipa Houses-Museum preserves five restored colonial villas from the early twentieth century. They represent a different version of Macao time: slower, smaller, marked by fishing tides and ferry schedules.

[Sé](/se) on the Macao peninsula shares the same China Standard Time.

[9am in Taipa is 1am in London](/when/9am-taipa/london) in winter, 2am during British Summer Time.
