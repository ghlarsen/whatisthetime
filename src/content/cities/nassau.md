Nassau sits on New Providence Island, 290 kilometers east of Miami across the Straits of Florida. It's close enough that you can see the lights of cruise ships departing both ways. The [Bahamas](/country/bahamas) uses Eastern Time, UTC-5 in winter and UTC-4 in summer, which aligns it exactly with [New York City](/new-york-city) and Miami. Of all the Caribbean nations, the Bahamas has perhaps the most tightly integrated daily schedule with the United States.

The timezone alignment reflects economic reality. The Bahamas' two largest industries are tourism and financial services, both heavily driven by US visitors and US capital. The cruise ships that dock in Nassau carry mostly American passengers who don't want to recalibrate their phones. The banks serving offshore accounts run on schedules that need to overlap with New York trading hours.

Nassau's natural daylight at 25 degrees north doesn't differ dramatically from Miami's. Summer days approach 13.5 hours of light; winter days shorten to under 10.5. The daylight saving switch, applied in line with the US schedule, shifts the evening hour but doesn't alter the character of the city.

Cable Beach and the Paradise Island strip run on tourist time, which means late. Downtown Nassau follows the banking and government schedule, which means early. The two rhythms coexist in the same timezone but in their own temporal worlds.

[9am in Nassau is 2pm in London](/when/9am-nassau/london).
