Türkmenabat (formerly Charjew or Chardzhou) sits where the Trans-Caspian Railway crosses the Amu Darya River, making it a strategic transit point in a landlocked country. The Amu Darya, the ancient Oxus of classical geography, forms the border with Uzbekistan here and has defined regional trade and agriculture for millennia.

[Turkmenistan](/country/turkmenistan) keeps Turkmenistan Time, UTC+5, year-round with no daylight saving. The country closed itself off from the outside world significantly under Saparmurat Niyazov (Turkmenbashi) and has remained among the most isolated states in Central Asia. The consistent UTC+5 clock is one of the few things about Turkmenistan that doesn't require special explanation.

Türkmenabat's position at 39 degrees north gives it hot summers and cold winters, a continental interior climate far from any moderating ocean. The Karakum Desert covers most of Turkmenistan, and the heat in summer is serious. Work and commerce shift earlier in the day during the hottest months.

The city is the capital of Lebap Province, which borders both Uzbekistan and Afghanistan. Cross-border trade patterns create informal temporal alignments with Uzbek market schedules, even where the official clocks differ by a small margin.

[9am in Türkmenabat is 4am in London](/when/9am-turkmenabat/london). [Ashgabat](/ashgabat), the capital, keeps the same Turkmenistan Time.
