Port of Spain's most important time is the 48 hours before Ash Wednesday. The Trinidad Carnival, which traces its roots to the French colonial period and was reshaped by freed Africans after emancipation, is one of the great unifying events of Caribbean culture. For those two days, civil time largely yields to Carnival time: J'ouvert at 4am, parade bands entering the Savannah at sunrise, the Last Lap on Tuesday evening.

[Trinidad and Tobago](/country/trinidad-and-tobago) runs on Atlantic Standard Time, UTC-4, year-round. No daylight saving. Port of Spain shares this clock with [San Fernando](/san-fernando) to the south and maintains the same offset as Puerto Rico to the northwest.

## natural Trinidad: birds and wetlands

Trinidad sits just 11 kilometers off the Venezuelan coast, which makes it geographically South American in character. The forests hold oilbirds and hummingbirds. The Caroni Swamp is home to scarlet ibis, Trinidad's national bird, which return to roost at sunset in flocks that turn the mangroves red.

The Gulf of Paria, the shallow sea between Trinidad and Venezuela, catches the sunset directly behind the city. Sunset in Port of Spain, around 6:15pm year-round, is a reliable daily event worth watching from the waterfront.
