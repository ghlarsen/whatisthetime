Ponce calls itself La Perla del Sur, the Pearl of the South, and the people of Ponce will make sure you know it. The city has always had a distinct identity from San Juan to the north: more traditional, more formal, architecturally richer in some ways, facing the Caribbean rather than the Atlantic.

[Puerto Rico](/country/puerto-rico) runs on Atlantic Standard Time, UTC-4, year-round with no daylight saving. Ponce and [San Juan](/san-juan) share the same clock, as does the entire island. In winter, this means Puerto Rico is an hour behind New York. In summer, when the East Coast springs forward to UTC-4, Puerto Rico and New York briefly align.

Ponce sits on the southern coast, sheltered from the Atlantic trade winds by the central mountain range. The climate here is drier than San Juan and hotter. The afternoon light in Ponce is intense and direct. The famous Ponce fire station, the Parque de Bombas, in red and black stripes at the center of the plaza, has been a landmark since 1882.

The city's annual Carnaval de Ponce, held in February, operates on its own suspended schedule: masks, music, and color that run through the night and into the next afternoon, where civil time becomes mostly theoretical.
