Killarney is a small settlement in the Bahamas, following America/Nassau time: UTC-5 in winter (Eastern Standard Time), UTC-4 in summer (Eastern Daylight Time). The Bahamas aligns its daylight saving transitions with the United States, making coordination with Florida, just 80 kilometers to the northwest, straightforward.

## Caribbean island, Florida business hours

The name Killarney reflects the Irish heritage of some early Bahamian settlers, though the place itself is distinctly Caribbean. The connection to Florida goes beyond name: the Bahamas' proximity to Miami means that economic rhythms, flight schedules, and business hours are closely tied to the Eastern Time Zone. Coordinating with [Nassau](/nassau) and other Bahamian settlements is frictionless for Florida-based businesses.

At 25 degrees north, the Bahamas has moderate seasonal daylight variation. Summer evenings extend past 8pm during EDT. Winter days end around 5:30pm on EST. The islands' latitude means year-round warmth regardless of where the clock sits.

The tourism industry that drives much of the Bahamian economy runs on a schedule set partly by cruise ship arrivals, which operate on their own time regardless of local clocks, and partly by flight schedules from North American cities.

[9am in Killarney is 2pm in London](/when/9am-killarney/london) in winter, five hours behind GMT.
