Managua is Nicaragua's capital and largest city, sitting on the southern shore of Lake Xolotlán. It runs on Central Standard Time, UTC-6, year-round. [Nicaragua](/country/nicaragua) abolished daylight saving time in 1998 after a failed attempt to reintroduce it that same year, and has kept a fixed offset since.

The abolition came after practical chaos. Nicaragua had observed DST in 1998, with the government repeatedly changing the dates due to political considerations. When the back-and-forth created scheduling problems across the country, the government simply eliminated DST entirely. The fixed UTC-6 has been stable ever since.

Managua is one of the few Latin American capitals on a lake, and the lakeside geography shapes the city's social rhythms. Afternoons are hot. The lake breeze picks up in the late afternoon. Evenings cool enough for outdoor socializing happen after 6pm, and that pattern holds year-round at 12 degrees north latitude where daylight barely shifts.

[Masaya](/masaya), just south of the capital, shares this timezone. [9am in Managua is 3pm in London](/when/9am-managua/london) during European winter, a six-hour gap.
