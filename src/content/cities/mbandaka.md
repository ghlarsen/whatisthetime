Mbandaka sits at almost precisely 0 degrees latitude on the Congo River in Équateur Province of the [Democratic Republic of the Congo](/country/democratic-republic-of-the-congo). It runs on West Africa Time, UTC+1, year-round. At the equator, sunrise and sunset fall at approximately 6am and 6pm every day of the year. The clock never needs to change because the sun keeps the same schedule.

## river port at the equator

The city is one of the DRC's significant river ports. The Congo River at this point is enormous, and river transport remains the primary connection between Mbandaka and [Kinshasa](/masina) over 600 kilometers to the southwest. Cargo barges take weeks to make the round trip.

Mbandaka was formerly known as Coquilhatville during Belgian colonial rule. It sits in the heart of the Congo Basin rainforest, one of the world's largest intact tropical forests, where the equatorial light is filtered through dense canopy.

[9am in Mbandaka is 8am in London](/when/9am-mbandaka/london) during European winter, a one-hour gap identical to all West Africa Time cities.
