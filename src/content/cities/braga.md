Braga is one of Portugal's oldest cities, with roots going back to the Roman settlement of Bracara Augusta, which was briefly the capital of the Roman province of Hispania Tarraconensis. Today it is a vibrant university city in Portugal's Minho region. It runs on Europe/Lisbon: UTC+0 in winter (Western European Time), UTC+1 in summer (Western European Summer Time), observing DST in line with EU schedules.

Portugal's timezone is slightly unusual geographically. Braga sits at about 41 degrees north and roughly 8.5 degrees west longitude. Geographically, this location would naturally align with UTC-1. But Portugal uses UTC+0 year-round, meaning solar noon in Braga in winter arrives around 1:15pm rather than noon. In summer with DST, solar noon is closer to 2:15pm. Evenings feel long because they are: the sun sets well past 9pm in June.

## The ecclesiastical capital

Braga has been Portugal's Catholic ecclesiastical capital for centuries. The Archbishop of Braga holds one of the oldest continuous see positions in the Iberian Peninsula. The cathedral, begun in the eleventh century, is the oldest in Portugal. The city's religious calendar, with its Holy Week processions, was for centuries as important as the civil calendar in organizing Braga's public life.

Bom Jesus do Monte, the famous baroque stairway sanctuary overlooking the city, is still a pilgrimage destination. The annual pilgrimage schedule is one of the most significant temporal rhythms in the city's year.

## University city and modern rhythm

Braga hosts the University of Minho, which transformed the city from a conservative religious center into one of Portugal's most dynamic university towns. Student life has injected a different clock: late nights, early morning lectures, the academic calendar.

The [time conversion from Braga to London](/when/9am-braga/london) is the same time in winter (both UTC+0) and the same in summer (both UTC+1). [Lisbon](/lisbon) shares the same clock.
