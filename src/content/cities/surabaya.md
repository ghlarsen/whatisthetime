Surabaya is [Indonesia](/country/indonesia)'s second-largest city and the capital of East Java, a port city and commercial center on the Madura Strait. Despite being in East Java, Surabaya uses Western Indonesia Time (UTC+7), the same timezone as Jakarta to the west, because all of Java falls within this zone.

Indonesia's three timezone system, Western (UTC+7), Central (UTC+8), and Eastern (UTC+9), divides the archipelago's vast extent into manageable segments. For Surabaya at roughly 112 degrees east longitude, UTC+7 means solar noon falls at around 11:30am, about half an hour early by the clock. This is a minor misalignment compared to the far western or eastern extremes of the timezone.

Surabaya is one of Southeast Asia's largest ports and a manufacturing hub for east Java's industrial output. The city's naval history is significant: the Battle of Surabaya in November 1945, when Indonesian independence fighters resisted British and Dutch forces, is commemorated on November 10 as Heroes' Day (Hari Pahlawan). Time here carries that weight.

## monsoon rhythms and commercial port

At 7 degrees south, Surabaya is near the equator, with minimal daylight variation. The city's rhythm is shaped by the monsoon seasons and the flow of commercial shipping more than by any seasonal clock.

[Jakarta](/jakarta) to the west shares the same Western Indonesia Time.

[9am in Surabaya is 2am in London](/when/9am-surabaya/london) in winter, 3am during British Summer Time.
