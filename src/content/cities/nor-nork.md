Nor Nork is one of the administrative districts of Yerevan, Armenia's capital, built during the Soviet period as a mass housing expansion for the growing city. The district sits on a plateau east of the city center with views toward Mount Ararat on clear days, the iconic peak that lies just across the border in Turkey but defines Armenian national identity.

[Armenia](/country/armenia) uses Armenia Time, UTC+4, with summer daylight saving shifting clocks to UTC+5. The DST transition follows seasonal rules, moving forward in spring and back in autumn. At 40 degrees north, Yerevan and its districts experience meaningful seasonal light variation: summer days push toward 14.5 hours, winter days shorten to under 9.5 hours.

Nor Nork's Soviet-era architecture is characteristic: large apartment blocks, wide avenues, a grid that prioritized cars and collective space over the organic street patterns of older neighborhoods. The district houses a large portion of Yerevan's population and is thoroughly integrated into the city's daily rhythm. The morning minibus routes that run downtown carry workers whose schedules are set by the capital's government and business offices.

## Ararat on the horizon

Mount Ararat's visibility is a kind of informal clock for residents: clear mornings when the peak appears are different in quality from overcast days. The mountain has been in Turkey since 1921 but remains on Armenia's national symbols.
