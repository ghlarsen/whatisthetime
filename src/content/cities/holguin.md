Holguín is Cuba's fourth-largest city, in the eastern part of the island. It runs on Cuba Standard Time, UTC-5 in winter, shifting to UTC-4 in summer when Cuba observes daylight saving. The transition dates are announced annually by the Cuban government.

Holguín shares the America/Havana timezone with the capital. The entire island of Cuba uses one timezone, and daylight saving is applied uniformly.

## Birthplace of Columbus's landing and of Fidel Castro

Christopher Columbus landed somewhere on Cuba's northeastern coast in 1492, near modern Holguín province. Fidel Castro was born in Birán, in Holguín province, in 1926. The region carries both the start of the colonial era and the start of the revolutionary era within its borders.

The commercial city grew around sugar and later around nickel mining, one of Cuba's most valuable natural resources. The Che Guevara nickel plant in nearby Moa is one of the largest nickel processing facilities in the world.

## Eastern Cuba's rhythm

Holguín sits at about 20.9 degrees North. The solar day is moderate: around 13.5 hours at midsummer and 10.5 hours at midwinter. UTC-5 puts solar noon at about 12:15pm, a good natural alignment for eastern Cuba's longitude.
