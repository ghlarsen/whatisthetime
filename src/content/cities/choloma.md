Choloma is one of Honduras's fastest-growing cities, located in the Sula Valley near San Pedro Sula. It runs on America/Tegucigalpa: UTC-6, year-round. Honduras does not observe daylight saving time.

The city has grown from a small town to a major urban center in a few decades, driven almost entirely by the maquiladora (export assembly) industry. Apparel factories making clothing for US and European brands employ hundreds of thousands of workers in the Sula Valley. Choloma has an unusually young and dense workforce population compared to other Honduran cities.

## Factory time

The maquiladora factories in Choloma operate on strict schedules tied to production quotas and shipping deadlines. Three-shift operations running 24 hours, along with two-shift systems, are common. The workers, predominantly women, navigate early morning shifts starting at 6am, midday shifts, and night shifts that run until after midnight.

The shipping schedules connecting Choloma's factories to container ports at Puerto Cortés, then to US distribution centers, are governed by the global logistics chain that operates on UTC. The factories at one end and the Walmart distribution centers at the other end run on different clocks, mediated by logistics software that handles the conversion.

## San Pedro Sula metro

Choloma is effectively a suburb of San Pedro Sula, Honduras's main commercial and industrial city. The two form a continuous urban area. San Pedro Sula has one of the highest murder rates in the world for cities not in active armed conflict, a situation driven by gang activity related to the drug trade and by deep structural poverty. This context shapes the daily security calculations of Choloma's factory workers.

The [time conversion from Choloma to London](/when/9am-choloma/london) puts London six hours ahead in winter, seven in summer when the UK observes BST.
