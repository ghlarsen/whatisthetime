Osijek is the capital of Slavonia, the flat agricultural eastern region of Croatia that extends toward the Danube and the borders with Hungary and Serbia. The city sits on the Drava River, and its Tvrđa, a Baroque fortress town built by the Habsburgs in the 18th century, is one of the best-preserved examples of Central European fortress architecture in the region.

[Croatia](/country/croatia) follows Central European Time, UTC+1 in winter and UTC+2 in summer (CEST), with EU standard daylight saving transitions. Osijek shares this with [Zagreb](/zagreb) to the west. At 45.5 degrees north on the flat Pannonian Plain, the city's seasonal light variation is significant: summer days approach 15.5 hours, winter days shorten to under 8.5.

## paprika harvest and the agricultural year

Slavonia's economy is agricultural, and the pace in Osijek reflects the surrounding countryside. The grain harvest in summer, the paprika harvest in autumn (Slavonia is Croatia's paprika country), and the pig slaughter in winter are seasonal markers that structure the year for families connected to farming even within the city.

The Drava floods occasionally and the threat of flooding is a seasonal event that focuses attention on the river in spring. The fish restaurants along the Drava serve the freshwater catches that the river and the surrounding waterways produce.
