Sharjah borders Dubai to the north and south, sharing the same Gulf Standard Time (UTC+4) as the rest of the [United Arab Emirates](/country/united-arab-emirates). The fixed offset applies year-round without daylight saving adjustment, a stable clock across a region where summers are too hot for outdoor work anyway.

While Dubai is known for finance and tourism, Sharjah has cultivated a reputation as the UAE's cultural emirate. It has stricter social regulations than Dubai: alcohol is prohibited, and public behavior codes are more conservative. The emirate has invested heavily in museums, arts facilities, and heritage preservation, hosting the Sharjah Biennial and operating several significant cultural institutions.

Sharjah International Airport handles significant traffic independently of Dubai's airports, and the emirate's port on the east coast (on the Gulf of Oman rather than the Arabian Gulf) gives it access to both the Arabian Gulf and Indian Ocean shipping routes.

## summer heat and the indoor working day

At 25 degrees north, Sharjah has about 13.5 hours of daylight in June and about 10.5 in December. The summer heat, pushing toward 45 degrees Celsius, drives virtually all commercial and social activity indoors during midday hours from roughly May through September, creating a de facto shift in when the productive day actually happens.

[Seeb](/seeb) in Oman across the Gulf shares the same UTC+4 offset.

[9am in Sharjah is 5am in London](/when/9am-sharjah/london) in winter, 6am during British Summer Time.
