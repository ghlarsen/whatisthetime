Guangzhou is one of China's largest cities, the capital of Guangdong province, and the heart of the Pearl River Delta manufacturing economy. It runs on China Standard Time, UTC+8, the single timezone that governs all of China's vast territory.

For Guangzhou, at 113 degrees East longitude, UTC+8 is a reasonable fit. The standard meridian for UTC+8 is 120 degrees East, which passes through [Shanghai](/shanghai). Guangzhou is 7 degrees west of that, meaning solar noon falls around 12:28pm, a slight but manageable offset.

## The Canton trade legacy

Guangzhou, historically known as Canton in Western records, was China's gateway to international trade for centuries. From the eighteenth century, the Canton System restricted foreign trade to this single port, and Western merchants lived in the Thirteen Factories district outside the city walls.

Coordinating trade between Canton and London, a 12-to-14-hour time difference depending on the season, was a practical problem that traders solved with correspondence, accounting for sailing time, and careful calendaring. The Pearl River Delta's trade orientation meant that time coordination with the outside world was always part of doing business here.

## The Canton Fair and the factory clock

The Canton Fair, held twice yearly, is the world's largest trade fair. For one month in spring and one month in autumn, buyers from around the world come to Guangzhou. The fair's schedule, synchronized with international visitors from [Europe](/), the Americas, and the Middle East, requires constant timezone management.
