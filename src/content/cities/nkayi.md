Nkayi sits in the Bouenza region of the Republic of the Congo, about 350 kilometers south of Brazzaville. The city's economy is anchored by SARIS-Congo, the sugar company that operates one of Central Africa's larger sugar complexes here. The sugarcane fields, the refinery, and the company infrastructure that surrounds them define much of the city's physical and economic landscape.

[Republic of the Congo](/country/republic-of-the-congo) uses West Africa Time, UTC+1, year-round. No daylight saving. The IANA identifier is Africa/Brazzaville. At around 4 degrees south of the equator, daylight in Nkayi barely varies through the year. Sunrise comes between 5:30am and 6am, sunset between 6pm and 6:30pm, with minimal change across seasons.

The sugar complex runs on industrial time: the processing season, the harvest schedule, the shift rotations at the refinery. Agricultural time and industrial time layer over each other. The harvest of sugarcane happens when the cane is ready, not on a fixed calendar date, and the refinery's rhythm adjusts accordingly.

## the Congo-Ocean Railway and colonial cost

The Niari Valley, where Nkayi sits, was connected to the coast by the Congo-Ocean Railway, one of the most costly construction projects in colonial African history, built between 1921 and 1934 at enormous human cost.
