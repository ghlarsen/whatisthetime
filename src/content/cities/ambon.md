Ambon sits in the heart of the Maluku Islands, the original Spice Islands that drove European colonial ambition for centuries. Nutmeg and cloves from this archipelago were so valuable in the 16th and 17th centuries that European powers went to war over them. The Dutch East India Company made Ambon its eastern headquarters and the city became the pivot of a global trade system.

Today Ambon is the capital of Maluku province and runs on Eastern Indonesia Time, UTC+9. The IANA identifier is Asia/Jayapura, named for the capital of Papua province to the east. Indonesia spans three timezones: Western (UTC+7, Java), Central (UTC+8, Bali), and Eastern (UTC+9, Maluku and Papua). Ambon is in the eastern zone.

Indonesia does not observe daylight saving time, so UTC+9 is the permanent clock here. This puts Ambon in timezone alignment with [Tokyo](/tokyo) and [Seoul](/seoul), though it is geographically far to the south.

The city sits on an island surrounded by one of Southeast Asia's richest marine environments. The rhythm here is shaped by the sea, fishing and diving, the morning market, the afternoon heat. The spice trade that once made this city globally significant has been supplanted by fisheries, but the maritime character remains.

[9am in Ambon is 1am in London](/when/9am-ambon/london) during European winter.
