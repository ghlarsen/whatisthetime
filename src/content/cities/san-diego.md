San Diego is the westernmost major city in the continental United States. It runs on Pacific Time, UTC-8 in winter (PST) and UTC-7 during daylight saving (PDT), the same clock as [San Francisco](/san-francisco) and Los Angeles to the north. The Pacific coast from Mexico to Canada synchronizes on this single timezone.

[United States](/country/united-states) Pacific Time is set by the longitude that runs through the Pacific Coast. San Diego, at 32 degrees north, gets the most temperate version of this timezone: warm year-round, minimal seasonal variation in temperature, morning marine layer burning off by noon, clear evenings.

The Tijuana border, 30 kilometers south, is where Pacific Time meets a different Mexican Baja California timezone that actually tracks the same rules. San Diego and [Rosarito](/rosarito) are on identical clocks, but crossing the border is still a full international transition.

The US Navy's Pacific Fleet is headquartered in San Diego, meaning the city runs on naval time as a deep layer beneath the civilian schedule. The 24-hour military clock, the watch schedules, the tide tables that govern harbor movements: these run in parallel with the civilian time.

At 32 degrees north, San Diego's sunset ranges from around 4:45pm in December to 8pm in June. The beach culture is year-round, but the long summer evenings are when Ocean Beach and Mission Beach fully assert themselves.

[9am in San Diego is 5pm in London](/when/9am-san-diego/london) in winter.
