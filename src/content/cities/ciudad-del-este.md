Ciudad del Este perches at the junction of three countries, connected to Foz do Iguaçu in Brazil by the Friendship Bridge and to Puerto Iguazú in Argentina a few kilometers south. Three countries, three clocks. Paraguay runs on PYT (UTC-4 in winter, UTC-3 in summer with daylight saving), Brazil's border state of Paraná runs on BRT (UTC-3), and Argentina keeps its own rhythm. Anyone crossing the Friendship Bridge for morning shopping adjusts their watch by an hour.

Paraguay observes daylight saving time, shifting clocks forward in September and back in March, which puts [Asunción](/asuncion) and Ciudad del Este temporarily in sync with São Paulo part of the year and out of sync the rest. For the thousands of traders and shoppers who cross daily, this arithmetic is second nature.

## Commerce bends time at the triple border

The city's pulse is commercial. The Mercado de los Mejores is packed before 8am. Shops on Avenida San Blas open earlier than the official business hours suggest, because the real customers are coming from Brazil, where the clock already reads an hour later. Time in Ciudad del Este bends toward where the money is moving.
