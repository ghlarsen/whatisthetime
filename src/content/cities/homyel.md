Homyel', also spelled Gomel, is Belarus's second-largest city, in the southeastern part of the country near the borders with Russia and Ukraine. It runs on UTC+3 year-round, on Further Eastern European Time (FET), the designation for the permanent summer time Belarus adopted in 2011.

In 2011, Belarus followed Russia's experiment with permanent daylight saving. Russia later reversed course in 2014, returning to winter time. Belarus kept summer time permanently. The result: in winter, when [Warsaw](/warsaw) is at UTC+1, [Kiev](/kyiv) at UTC+2, and [Moscow](/moscow) at UTC+3, Homyel' is also at UTC+3, matching Moscow but running two hours ahead of its nearest EU neighbor Poland.

## Chernobyl's neighbor

Homyel' is the largest city in the area most affected by the 1986 Chernobyl nuclear disaster. The Chernobyl Exclusion Zone is partially in Ukraine and partially in Belarus, and Homyel' Oblast received significant radioactive contamination from the fallout. The disaster altered the region's relationship with time in a profound way: evacuations, long-term health monitoring, contamination management, and generational health effects created a different kind of temporal reckoning alongside the civil clock.
