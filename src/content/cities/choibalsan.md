Choibalsan is the capital of Dornod Province in eastern Mongolia, sitting near the Chinese and Russian borders. It runs on Asia/Ulaanbaatar: UTC+8, Mongolia Standard Time. Mongolia observes DST from late March to late September, moving to UTC+9 in summer, though practice has been inconsistent in recent years.

The city is named after Khorloogiin Choibalsan, the Mongolian communist leader who ruled from the 1930s until his death in 1952. Choibalsan oversaw purges that killed tens of thousands of Mongolians, including virtually the entire Buddhist clergy, under Stalin's direction. The city bearing his name in the Mongolian People's Republic era has not been renamed since democratization.

## The Mongolian-Manchurian steppe

Choibalsan sits in the broad eastern Mongolian steppe, near the historic Khalkhin Gol river where a major Soviet-Mongolian victory over Japanese forces occurred in 1939. This battle, barely known in Western history, convinced Japan not to attack the Soviet Union from the east during World War Two, a decision with enormous consequences for the war's outcome.

The Khalkhin Gol battlefield is about 130 kilometers east of Choibalsan. The steppe surrounding the city is the traditional territory of Mongolian nomadic herders whose movement patterns follow seasonal pasture cycles, not clock schedules.

## China border proximity

Choibalsan is close to the Chinese border, and the border town of Erenhot (Erlian) in Inner Mongolia is accessible by road. China uses UTC+8 throughout, including Inner Mongolia. Mongolia at UTC+8 (standard) and China at UTC+8 are on the same clock in this region.

The [time conversion from Choibalsan to London](/when/9am-choibalsan/london) puts London eight hours behind in winter (UTC+8 standard), seven in summer if Mongolia is observing DST.
