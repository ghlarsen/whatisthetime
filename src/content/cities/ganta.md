Ganta sits in Nimba County near Liberia's border with Guinea and Ivory Coast. It is one of Liberia's most important interior trading towns, a crossroads where goods and people move between three countries. The clock it keeps is UTC+0, the same as [Monrovia](/monrovia), the capital, and the same as [London](/london) in winter.

Liberia is unusual in West Africa: most of its neighbors run on UTC+0 or UTC+1, but Liberia's path to a standard time was its own. The country held an unofficial "Monrovia time" of UTC-0:44:30 until 1972, when it finally aligned with GMT. That half-hour offset, based on the longitude of Monrovia, was one of the last non-standard timezone offsets in the world to disappear.

## Border town rhythms

Ganta's position at the junction of three countries means its market operates on multiple informal schedules. Traders from [Abidjan](/abidjan) and Conakry arrive on different days. Cross-border traffic peaks in the early morning. The formal clock matters less here than the rhythm of the weekly market and the movement of trucks.

The town also sits near iron ore regions that have attracted mining investment, bringing more structured work schedules and shift patterns that push against the older solar rhythm.
