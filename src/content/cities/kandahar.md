Kandahār is Afghanistan's second-largest city and the spiritual and political heartland of the Pashtun south. It runs on Afghanistan Time, UTC+4:30, year-round. No daylight saving. The entire country shares this offset.

Kandahār sits at about 1,010 meters above sea level in the Arghandab River valley. The city's longitude, 65.7 degrees East, puts UTC+4:30 at a natural solar noon of around 12:07pm, a near-perfect alignment.

## The Pashtun calendar

The Pashtun community maintains its own traditional Pashto calendar alongside the civil Solar Hijri calendar. Agricultural seasons, the opium poppy harvest cycle, and tribal gathering seasons structure time in the rural areas around the city.

The city's strategic position on trade routes between Central Asia, South Asia, and Iran has made it a crossroads for millennia. Alexander the Great established or refounded a city here; the Mughal Empire passed through; the British fought two wars here in the nineteenth century.

## The poppy harvest and the informal economy

The Kandahār region has been the heart of Afghanistan's opium production for decades. The harvest cycle, in spring, connects the city's rural economy to global drug markets. This informal economy runs on its own schedule, tied to the agricultural calendar and the movement of traders, not to UTC+4:30.
