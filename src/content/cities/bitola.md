Bitola runs on Central European Time: UTC+1 in winter, UTC+2 in summer, following the EU DST schedule. The IANA identifier is Europe/Skopje, the same as North Macedonia's capital. The country has one timezone throughout.

Bitola sits in the southwestern corner of North Macedonia, near the border with Greece, at an elevation of around 600 meters in the Pelagonia plain. At 41 degrees north, its daylight variation is moderate. Summer sunsets arrive around 8:30pm. Winter brings darkness before 5pm.

## The city of consuls

During the late Ottoman period, Bitola, then known as Monastir, was one of the most diplomatically active cities in the Balkans. The Ottoman Empire's administrative structure made it the capital of the Monastir Vilayet, a regional government unit covering a large part of the western Balkans. European powers posted consulates here to monitor Ottoman affairs and protect their commercial interests.

At its peak in the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, Bitola hosted over twenty consulates. The elegant consulate buildings along the Shirok Sokak (Broad Street) are still standing, giving the city a cosmopolitan architectural character unusual for a city its size. Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, who would later found modern Turkey, attended military school in Bitola in the 1890s. There is a museum dedicated to him in the city.

## Post-Ottoman continuity

When the Balkan Wars ended Ottoman rule in 1912, Bitola became part of Serbia and then Yugoslavia. The consulates closed. The city's strategic importance diminished. But the physical legacy of that diplomatic era remains visible in every baroque and art nouveau building on the main street.
