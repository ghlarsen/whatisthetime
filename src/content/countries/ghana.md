Ghana uses Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) (UTC+0). No daylight saving time. IANA: `Africa/Accra`.

**Key facts about time in Ghana**
- Timezone: Greenwich Mean Time (GMT)
- UTC offset: +00:00
- DST: No
- IANA identifier: `Africa/Accra`
- Capital: Accra

Most countries at UTC+0 are offset somewhat from the Prime Meridian but use it for practical regional alignment. Ghana is different: the Prime Meridian (0 degrees longitude) actually passes through eastern Ghana, running roughly through the city of Tema, just east of Accra.

This makes Ghana one of the few countries in the world where the clock and the sun are genuinely synchronized. At solar noon in Accra, the standard time is very close to 12:00 noon. The meridian and the clock agree.

Ghana uses GMT, UTC+0, year-round with no daylight saving time.

## The first republic of sub-Saharan Africa

Ghana became independent on March 6, 1957, the first sub-Saharan African country to gain independence from a European colonial power. The country was formerly the Gold Coast under British rule. Kwame Nkrumah, the independence leader and first prime minister and president, became a pan-African icon.

Nkrumah famously said "Seek ye first the political kingdom," meaning that political independence must precede economic and cultural liberation. The adoption of Ghana's standard time, aligned with GMT rather than the previous British administrative time, was a small piece of that assertion of self-determination.

## Accra at the crossing

Accra is one of the few major cities that sits close to the intersection of the Prime Meridian and the equator. The equator runs through the Gulf of Guinea offshore from Accra; the Prime Meridian passes through the eastern edge of the city's region.

This makes the coordinate [0°, 0°], the origin point of the global coordinate system, a location in the ocean off the coast of Ghana. There is no land there, no monument. It is simply where the two reference lines of the earth's coordinate system cross, in water, a few degrees from the Ghanaian coastline.

## Kente cloth and the measured thread

Ghana's Kente cloth is woven on hand looms, in narrow strips that are then sewn together. Weaving Kente is a precise, time-intensive craft. Master weavers can produce specific traditional patterns from memory, following patterns passed down through apprenticeship over generations.

The weaving process is measured not in clock minutes but in rows completed, in the rhythm of the loom. Like all craft production that predates industrial time discipline, Kente weaving has its own internal temporal logic.
