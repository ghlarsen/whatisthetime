Senegal observes Coordinated Universal Time with no offset: UTC+0, also known as Greenwich Mean Time, year-round. No daylight saving. The IANA identifier is `Africa/Dakar`.

Dakar, the capital, sits at roughly 17.4 degrees West longitude and 14.7 degrees North latitude. At this longitude, UTC+0 places solar noon at around 11:10 AM local time, slightly early. But Senegal's position at the western edge of Africa makes UTC+0 an appropriate alignment; no significant West African country is further west.

## The tip of Africa

The Cape Verde Peninsula, on which Dakar sits, juts into the Atlantic Ocean and terminates at Cap-Vert, the westernmost point of the African continent. From here, the nearest South American coast (in Brazil) is approximately 2,800 kilometers away.

In the age of transatlantic navigation, Dakar's position was strategically important. Ships leaving Europe for the Americas could provision at Dakar and use the trade winds that blow west across the Atlantic from this latitude. Dakar was also a major point in the transatlantic slave trade: enslaved people from across West Africa were held on the island of Gorée, just off Dakar's coast, before being shipped across the Atlantic.

## Gorée Island

The island of Gorée is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its role in the Atlantic slave trade. The Maison des Esclaves (House of Slaves) museum contains the "Door of No Return," a doorway that opened directly onto the sea, through which enslaved people were forced onto ships.

Historians have debated the scale of Gorée's specific role, some arguing that other West African ports handled larger volumes. The debates do not diminish the island's symbolic significance as a site of memory for the African diaspora. Multiple heads of state, including Pope John Paul II and Nelson Mandela, have visited and paid tribute.

## Teranga and time

Senegalese culture is often described through the concept of *teranga*, a Wolof word meaning hospitality, generosity, and openness. It extends to how time is experienced socially: social events run on an extended, flexible schedule. Arriving on the stated hour is understood as early in many contexts.

This is not disorganization. It is a different calibration of what time means in social relationships: presence and warmth take priority over clock precision.

Senegal's musicians, particularly in the genres of mbalax (a fusion of Wolof percussion rhythms with Western instruments, made internationally known by Youssou N'Dour) and the older griot tradition of spoken praise singing, work in musical time that has its own internal logic, responsive to the moment rather than locked to a metronome.
